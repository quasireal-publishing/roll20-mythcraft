CHAPTER 9 |  EXPLORING ANCERRA

Bridgemoor spans one of its narrowest points, connecting Nalathesh to Kendaria. Intrepid explorers have discovered crypts full of strange technology from an ancient civilization, but some have also met their grizzly end at the hands of equally strange beasts that lurk in the Chasm’s endless caves.

QUEST IDEAS

The Meritocrats are preparing to choose their heirs, and some merchants want the heroes to help fix the results.

The Underbridge Cult has successfully summoned a Bonewraith, who lurks in the sewers and sends Zombies to terrorize the city-state.

An explorer wants to hire the heroes as protection as they venture into the chasm.

Ser Kellion Blacksky pursues authoritarian control of the streets in response to cult activity, but this harms Bridgemoor’s economy. How can the heroes step in and fix this?

BUYLEIST

For many generations, Buyleist was significantly weaker than its neighbor nation, Unjaarn. Buyleist’s rulers lived under the constant shadow of fear that Unjaar would decide to invade them or cut them off from international trade, and unfortunately this did come to pass a couple generations ago.

As it turns out, this was a mistake on Unjaar’s part. Buyleist made an alliance with Vel, and Vel’s nefarious mages sowed discord in Unjaar’s internal affairs. This eventually led to a complete schism within Unjaar, which resulted in the nation splitting in two. Now, the two split nations - Unjaar and Vadan - are both smaller and weaker than Buyleist, allowing the Buyleistians to flex their muscles in the region.

Academic. Buyleist is building its first major university and hopes to attract scholars from other nations to help get the educational program off the ground. Vel’s Mages of the Beach are more than willing to assist in this process.

Aristocratic and Political. Buyleist rejects the notion of aristocracy or a caste system wholesale. Instead, their governing body is a representative democracy elected by any citizen who makes a large enough annual salary to be offered a ballot. While these elections offer the illusion of choice, the Mages of the Beach from Vel are really in charge and make sure that each “election” actually promotes a Buyleistian who will do exactly what the Mages instruct.

There’s a stink about Buyleist. The folk purport to be free, but Vel plays the nation like a fiddler plays her strings.

-Lyla

Mercantile and Trade. Buyleist exports many dye pigments, spices, and foodstuffs unique to the swamp delta.

While Unjaar and Vadan also export many of the same materials, Buyleist’s economic advantage allows them to undersell their competition.

Militant. The military in Buyleist is medium-sized and quite competent. It could almost certainly defeat Vadan or Unjaar independently, but if the two made an alliance against Buyleist, then Buyleist might be in trouble.

Religious. While Buyleistians can practice any religion that they wish, doing so is generally discouraged. The governing body reminds Buyleist’s citizenry that they are in such times of prosperity because of the wisdom and generosity of the Mages of the Beach, and it is truly the Mages who should be venerated.

Social. Citizens of Buyleist are encouraged to pursue the careers and hobbies that most interest them. This keeps Buyleistians generally content and preoccupied with their own ventures, though some political dissidents speak out against their nation’s alliance with Vel.

Underworld. The most fervent political opponents to Vel have formed a covert rebellion. This has not yet developed into violence, although many who are aware of the resistance movement think that it’s only a matter of time.

SELMORT

The capital of Buyleist is the fortified stilt city of Selmort. The city is built in tiers, with wide, flat barges holding apartments where the poor live. Elevated platform houses, suspended over the swamp on stilts and connected by rope bridges, are home to the middle class and to the marketplaces.

POINTS OF INTEREST

Swamplands of the Great Chasm. A humid tropical swamp spreads across a vast river delta on the southern end of the Great Chasm. Many small islands blanket this region, separated by shallow waters that would be treacherous for large boats. A wide variety of natural flora and fauna mix with the strange minerals and ancient technologies buried deep in the Chasm; as a result, unnatural land formations and horrible monsters lurk outside of the stilted cities and villages that speckle the swamplands. “Travel by day, and you’ll be safe; travel by night, you’ll get a fright” is a common rhyme in these lands, and children dare one another to explore the swamps outside their settlements.

QUEST IDEAS

The heroes get accused of political malfeasance and risk becoming a M’l’ykkri snack if they cannot escape.

The heroes might get caught up in the three-way trade war between Buyleist, Vadan, and Unjaar. They will have to carefully evaluate who they support.

The Mages of the Beach promise great rewards to the heroes if they will find and quash a burgeoning rebellion.

Illegal drug trade is running rampant in the swamps beneath the stilted districts of Selmort. The heroes must find and put an end to these trades.

CHEE

The forest nation of Chee used to be much larger and more prosperous. Originally, Chee was part of Chehozran, but after a short civil war they split along the mountain range that separates the two regions. Chee never recovered economically from the civil war, struggling to make an international name for itself. Before it was able to achieve any notable successes, Roshallan annexed Chee.

Academic. Chee has no major academies. All of its small schools that teach basic educational subjects are managed by Roshallan.

Aristocratic and Political. Chee is governed by lesser kings assigned to the region from Roshallan. The Chee folk have no say in their own governmental practices.

Mercantile and Trade. Chee’s forests produce a bounty of foodstuffs and timber, along with colorful pigments. The coast teems with fish, and Deeprock’s shipyards manufacture powerful shallow and deep water ships.

Chee’s fine shipyard economy does not erase the tragedy of her erasure by a tyrant’s hand.

-Finneas

Military. Chee has no military; its armed forces were completely defeated and disbanded by Roshallan.

Religious. In the privacy of their own homes, most Chee folk keep alive the traditions of their ancestors. Like the Chehozran, the Chee believe that one day the kaijan of old will return: some will be benevolent, and many will be tyrannical. They pray that the kaijan who return to the nation of Chee will strive to protect, rather than to destroy.

Social. While many Chee folk wish that they could run their own country instead of being controlled by Roshallan, they also recognize that Roshallan’s powerful military offers them great security. Most folk of Chee simply go about their lives, helping out their friends when they can and avoiding strangers.

Underworld. Some nationalistic Chee folk have taken it upon themselves to overthrow Roshallan. While the more rational among these rebels simply try to disrupt Roshallan’s presence in Chee’s territory, some ambitious rebels intend to overthrow the entire Roshallani empire.

DEEPROCK

Chee’s only major city, Deeprock, is micromanaged by Roshallan. It has a fortified palace where a Roshallani regional king oversees the nation of Chee. A strong military presence keeps the peace, and Deeprock’s enormous shipyards produce large, powerful naval vessels at a rapid rate, which Roshallan uses to conquer other nations that border the Inner Sea.

QUEST IDEAS

Roshallan hires the heroes to head into Chee and put down a developing rebellion. The rebels might also approach the heroes and ask them to help chase Roshallan out of the nation. Caught between the two parties’ desires, the heroes must weigh their morals and their desire for wealthy compensation.

Roshallan will have unparalleled naval superiority if they can finish building their flagship, the Thunderhead. Chee’s rebels want the heroes to help them sabotage the vessel so that it will sink once many hundreds of Roshallani soldiers are on deck and out in the deep sea.
