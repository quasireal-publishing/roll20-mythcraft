Sailing a Rovelese ship would be a fascinating experience, Id wager. I wonder where one might take me, and who I might meet.

-Lyla

Militant. Rovels seafaring vessels are fortified and well-armed. Their military is small but adequate to defend against Rendens coastal raids.

Religious. Rovelese are more enamored of their economic success than displays of piety. Temples are not very common in Rovelese towns or cities, and the religious among the nation usually practice their religion in the privacy of their own homes with small gatherings of likeminded friends and family.

Social. Most Rovelese folk are very results-driven and enjoy bragging about their successes. They are willing enough to lend a hand to someone in need, but this is on some level just another data point that they can use when demonstrating that they are good, upstanding folk.

Underworld. Falthic spies run rampant in Rovelese society. Some such spies - and even some unscrupulous Rovelese - will sell merchant ship sailing routes to Renden, allowing the violent raiders to ambush boats.

HOLLYPORT

Rovels capital city is perhaps unsurprisingly its largest port. The monarchy has a decadent but not very defensible palace, and the most powerful merchant lords have incredibly well defended manors that are almost as plush and luxurious as the monarchs palace. Hollyport has an enormous network of docks and piers that allow their large merchant vessels to maintain year-round trade with the nations in and beyond the Inner Sea.

QUEST IDEAS

The heroes are hired as added security against raids from Renden as a merchant ship sails through the Inner Sea.

Someone in the royal palace covertly gets word to the heroes that the real power is in the hands of the merchant class, and asks the heroes to help restore the nation to a traditional monarchy.

A merchant leader approaches the heroes and asks them to investigate the leaders guild, as rumors are circulating that Falthic spies have infiltrated the guild.

RUPTAEA

A nation clinging to chilly rocks and sparse boreal forests, Ruptaeas harsh land has solicited a generous response from its people. The rainy summers and blizzard-ridden winters have instilled in Ruptaeans a sense of kinship and shared suffering. Ruptaeans will commonly invite one another over to share in what little food they have.

Academic. Most of Ruptaeas settlements have schools that cover the basics of reading, mathematics, and history. Ruptaea has no major universities or advanced educational institutions, which is something that Queen Belle is trying to remedy.

Aristocratic and Political. The aristocracy of Ruptaea are the lords and ladies that govern each individual settlement. These aristocrats elect a monarch from among their ranks; that monarch rules until their death or abdication, at which point the aristocrats elect the monarchs successor. The current monarch is a hondu named Belle, better known for her empathetic nature than for her political smarts.

Her reputation precedes her. Queen Belle is both beautiful and kind. I hope to one day kneel in her service.

-Chauncey

Mercantile and Trade. Ruptaea is economically weak with few natural resources to offer in international trade. It has some mineral mines, some stone quarries, and some forests that can produce timber and plant and animal byproducts, but none of its resources are notable enough to make Ruptaea competitive with other nations.

Militant. Ruptaea has a weak military, but they have a good relationship with Fjordenhaven and Eryndor. They dedicate most of their military resources to the border they share with Vaenyth.

Religious. In general, Ruptaeans are very religious. They worship many pantheons, making sacrificial offerings to the Celestial Council for good luck, to the Fiends and Exiles of Inferno to ward off their wrath, and to the Archfae Conclaves for bounty. They also worship the Avadri and Avatars, primarily because they do not want any pantheon to feel overlooked or slighted.

Social. Ruptaeans are humble folk, and this usually extends to the aristocracy as well. They will make plans for their days or weeks, but if a neighbor or traveler needs food or shelter, most Ruptaeans will invite them into their own homes and provide for those who need it, setting aside their own plans for the day.

Underworld. Small, localized gangs are uncommon but not unheard of in some of the larger towns and cities. Given Ruptaeas poor economy, large crime syndicates have not formed in the nation as there is no major profit to be gained.

ESSUR

The capital of Ruptaea is Essur. Built on a hill rich with groundwater springs, it is one of the most defensible locations in the nation. A small palace of common stone polished to resemble marble is the residence of the reigning monarch. Marketplaces and cramped residential houses spread out in a tangle of crooked streets radiating from the palace. Many temples stand proud throughout the city as well, and the foundation has been laid for the new Essur University.

POINTS OF INTEREST

The Ruptaea Glacier. In the north, an enormous glacier looms over Ruptaea. Moving at its snail-like pace, it carves a trench on its journey west towards the ocean. In hundreds of years, it will likely complete its journey. Ruptaeans harvest ice from the glacier, and young adventurous types try to climb it and explore its frigid top.

The Storm. A couple days sail from Ruptaeas west coast, a tremendous hurricane churns in perpetuity. It has been there for hundreds of years, never moving, never growing or shrinking either in size or in magnitude. Known simply as the Storm, the Ruptaeans do not know why it has sustained itself without moving, but so long as it continues to remain static it poses no threat to the nation.

QUEST IDEAS

Some foolish would-be adventurers have sailed into the Storm. Queen Belle asks the heroes to mount a rescue mission.

Vaenyth has been acting belligerent near Ruptaeas border, and the aristocrats would like for the heroes to travel to some Vaenythan towns and tell them to back off.

A warm summer has cause chunks of the glacier to break off and melt, which is causing serious flooding in some Ruptaean towns. Can the heroes rescue the commoners?

SHADRADOTH

In the subtropical hills of Thunderhead Vale, Shadradoth has a storied and chaotic history. It seceded from Valstrom long ago after a bloody civil war. The king regent, who was an advisor to the recently deceased king, refused to abdicate when the kings eldest daughter came of age and made her claim to the throne. The ensuing war split the nation apart, with Princess Shadra leading the region that eventually became Shadradoth.

Shadradoth upheld the heirship of Queen Shadra for several generations thereafter. However, the kingdom crumbled again when Shadras descendent, King Thunharad, did not sire an heir or pronounce any of his cousins next in line. Anyone who could claim relations to King Thunharad claimed that they were the heir apparent. The ensuing war devastated the country, and to this day Shadradoth still has not recovered.

A tale as old as time. All it takes is one power-hungry criminal and one legitimate ruler unwilling to budge, and you have civil war.

-Belozog

Academic. Shadradoth has many schools in its larger towns, most of which were established when Shadradoth was still part of Valstrom. However, few of these institutions are still staffed in any meaningful way.

Aristocratic and Political. The Shadradothan governmental system is a complete disaster. Half of the nation runs itself like a feudal system, but there is no central uniting authority. Many merchants and some whole towns or small regions instead govern themselves through democratic elections and consensus votes. The result is a mishmash of half-baked political theories that do not work on their own, let alone when interacting with one another.

Mercantile and Trade. Individual settlements often trade with one another within Shadradoth. Those on the border near another nation will also trade with their neighbors, but any form of organized international trade is nonexistent due to Shadradoths dysfunctional government.

Militant. While the political process by which settlements run themselves vary widely among Shadradothan towns, each town has a militia that can be rallied by whatever political force leads that town.
