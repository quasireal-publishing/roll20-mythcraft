PHALANX

The mountainous capital of Noord, Phalanx resides in the northernmost stretches of the nation. Built on the icy cliffs on the northern end of the mainland Cloudpiercer Range, Noords believe Phalanx to be utterly impregnable. The elders of Noord have invested much time and energy in expanding Phalanx to the point that the capital now stretches across four mountain peaks, but is only accessible through one. In the unthinkable event of Noord’s military suffering a crippling defeat, the entire population of Noord could fit inside Phalanx. It has two freshwater springs underneath it, not to mention the vast quantity of snow and ice, so Noords would only need to worry about their food supply in the event of a prolonged siege. The harsh winters would then force any rational foe to retreat.

While it is built to survive warfare, Phalanx also offers amenities such as a sports arena and an orchestra hall.

POINTS OF INTEREST

The Glowing Forest. On the west side of the Cloudpiercer Range, strange trees grow in oddly regular patterns. These trees seem to be made of some synthesis of vegetable and metal matter, and they grow pear-shaped glass bulbs that occasionally glow from within. Noords are generally hesitant to enter the forest, but will occasionally go out and collect these glass lights, which they use to illuminate Phalanx.

QUEST IDEAS

The Noords ask the heroes to harvest resources from the Glowing Forest.

In the face of invasion from a terribly powerful enemy (perhaps fiends from Inferno), heroes need to help the Noords evacuate to Phalanx and then must endure a harsh winter fighting a supernaturally strong foe.

The Noords are on a rampaging war path, so representatives from Kandiff ask the heroes to infiltrate Phalanx and destabilize its defenses or assassinate its leaders.

PASH

Located at the mouth of a great bay that opens up into the ocean, Pash is an economically and militarily powerful city-state that facilitates major international trade. The Pashan International Bank is the largest bank in Gathandia and contains a vast sum of riches. When Pash is threatened by a foreign power, the autocrats weigh the costs of fighting the invading force or simply bribing them to move on. This calculation, when also weighed by the aggressor, usually mitigates bloodshed.

Pash also controls a handful of mining towns located along the Vaenyth River, south and east of Vaenyth’s border. The climate ranges from mild to hot each year and is always quite humid.

Academic. Pash has a large internationally acclaimed university that offers virtually any course imaginable but specializes in economic theory. Pashan merchants that retire for a quieter end of life will often teach these courses. The Pashan Academy attracts scholars from many nations.

Aristocratic, Political, and Militant. Pash is ruled by an autocracy. Military generals are also legislators and magisters, overseeing every aspect of Pashan life. Both the Pashan army and navy are well-equipped, well-staffed, and well-trained. The military keeps everything in Pash carefully controlled. Their borders with Vaenyth and Roshallan are both heavily guarded and fortified.

Mercantile and Trade. Under the direction of the generals, the merchant lords and bankers of Pash play Vaenyth and Roshallan off of one another in order to keep either belligerent nation from conquering Pash. As Pash is built on both banks of the massive Great Bay, they thrive on running merchant ferries across the bay and docking international trade ships the world over. The river that feeds into the Great Bay is also quite rich in gold, so Pashan divers mine the gold from the riverbed to feed their economy. Pash has powerful and reliable banks that many wealthy merchants and royalty from dozens of nations will use to keep their assets safe.

Religious. Most Pashans fervently worship the Celestial Council and the Avatars, believing that their devout faith is part of what affords them ongoing safety from both Vaenyth and Roshallan.

Social. Pashans are not unkind, but they are generally quite cunning. Pashan merchants are ruthless hagglers, and the divers and miners are typically rugged and competent fighters.

As hard-edged as Pashans usually are, they are also hospitable, believing that treating visitors well will reflect positively and draw more international business.

Underworld. A huge crime syndicate thrives in Pash’s grimy underworld. It has its hands in every aspect of Pashan life, from mining to banking to ferrying merchants across the Great Bay. The crime lords also act as points of contact for other international syndicates, especially the mob bosses of Falth.

POINTS OF INTEREST

Floating Islands. Large chunks of floating rock hover above much of the Great Bay and the river that feeds it. These structures extend up the Vaenyth Range. Pashan bankers have paid mages to fly to the rocks and determine if there is anything valuable to be gained from them. The assessment was conclusive: the floating islands are rich in Arcanium and Primor, two rare and valuable minerals with unique magical properties. Pash is currently in the process of determining how best to mine these resources.

The Great Bay. The Great Bay is so wide that it takes just over a day to sail from one side to the other. The Vaenyth River feeds into the Great Bay, which opens into the ocean at its mouth. The main city-state of Pash is built on both of the Great Bay’s banks and on the islands that dot the region.

QUEST IDEAS

Pash’s enormous crime syndicate recruits the heroes to help siphon funds from the Pashan International Bank.

Pashan merchants pay the heroes to infiltrate and expose the leaders of the city’s criminal empire.

One of the most lucrative mining outposts has gone quiet, so the autocrats hire the heroes to investigate.

The first floating island mine is about to become operational, but a dangerous creature has taken up residence in the mineshaft. Can the heroes defeat it?

Roshallan, Vaenyth, or another nation poses a threat to Pash and is unwilling to accept bribes. Will the heroes help protect the independent city-state?

RENDEN

Renden is a nation of seafarers that brutally raid coastal towns and cities along the Inner Sea. Originally, the people of Renden shared much of their culture with Fjordenhavian, Noord, and Teivosi society, but over the generations, the people of Renden lost all shred of morality.

Known as the terrors of the Inner Sea, the Renden do not discriminate between any town or seafaring vessel of any nation. They will raid anything, leaving boats and buildings in burning ruins and leaving sailors and townsfolk to rend their clothes and beg for mercy.

Aristocratic and Political. Renden is administered by the Circle of Matriarchs. These are elder Renden women with a deep connection to the Primal Source. Capable of calming great sea storms, they rule from their enormous vessel, the

Ravager, which often has several smaller boats and wide, flat barges lashed to it or anchored nearby.

While the Circle of Matriarchs make legislative decisions for their nation, the Kings of the Sea enforce these laws and conduct raids. Each captain of a coastal longship or a deep-sea pirate ship is considered a King of the Sea.

Mercantile and Trade. Renden do not trade with other nations, for they never willingly give anything to those who are not their own. Rather, they plunder and pillage whenever they need to supplement their internal economy.

Militant. The Renden are competent sailors and vicious coastal fighters. Their longships are narrow, quiet, fast, and low to the ground. This allows them to quickly come upon a coastal town and catch any watchguards by surprise. Renden deep sea vessels, like the Ravager, are much larger and slower.

Renden really isn’t doing anyone any favors. The threat of raids has only strengthened the hold that Falth’s coastal crime lords keep over their citizens.

-Charlie

Religious. The Renden have no religion. They live and die by the iron in their hands, the blood in their veins, and the salt air in their lungs.

THE RAVAGER

The Ravager is a twelve-masted boat of tremendous size. Covered in living vegetation grown by the magical hands of the Circle of Matriarchs, it could be mistaken at a distance for a small island. The Ravager contains an entire marketplace, two inns and taverns, and a council chamber where the Circle of Matriarchs make decisions for their nation.

Several large Renden barges often float nearby, housing stolen goods and additional taverns and marketplaces aboard their wooden decks.

THE ISLANDS

The Renden are more at home on sea than land, but they have many hidden coves and obscure islands in the Inner Sea where they stash their wares or occasionally go for leisurely hunting expeditions.

NACHTSTROND

The Renden will, at all costs and on pain of death, avoid Nachtstrond, or the Black Coast. This stretch of woodland appears to be in a constant state of blighted decay, and an enormous but withered World-Tree stretches its thorny boughs over the expanse of craggy forbidden shores. The Renden believe that the area is haunted by the souls of all humanoids killed violently. Needless to say, if this is true, then any Renden that sets foot on the shore will be torn apart by vengeful ghosts in a matter of moments.
