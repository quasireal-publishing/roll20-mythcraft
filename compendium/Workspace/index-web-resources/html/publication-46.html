BISHAL

The wide open plains and arid steppes of Bishal are home to several large tribes, all of whom share a general culture but who are often at war with one another.

Each Bishalian tribe follows a nomadic lifestyle, leading their massive horse herds to fresh pastures or following the herds of bison that they use for food, tools, and clothing.

Aristocratic and Political. Each tribal lord is responsible for their own tribe’s actions but has little interest in the other tribes beyond occasional trades or skirmishes. On the rare occasions that multiple tribal lords feel that their entire nation is threatened, then the lords will come together where the two major rivers in Bishal join together into one. There, the lords will negotiate working together and for how long they are bound to do so. The largest and most belligerent Bishalian tribal lord is named Tilion, and he has made quite a name for himself and his tribe as a military power to be reckoned with.

Mercantile and Trade. Bishalians typically trade internally instead of internationally. They use clay from the riverbeds to make pottery, and use every part of bison and other game animals to make food and various tools or clothes. Usually, if they consider trading with a foreign town or merchant caravan, they will instead just raid it.

Militant. Bishalians make excellent cavaliers, and many Bishalian warriors are more at home on horseback than they are on foot. Capable of firing shortbows while riding, a Bishalian tribe’s warriors can conduct quick and brutal raids on the towns of other nations that border their territory.

Religious and Social. The people of Bishal tend to be very religious and believe horses to be sacred animals. They worship A’rl’ng, the great steed of the Shibmar Conclave of the Archfae, as a guiding patron. Most Bishalians believe in a cycle of reincarnation where when a humanoid dies, they are reborn as a horse, and when a horse dies, they are reborn as a humanoid. People generally hold that Centaurs are the reincarnations of especially brave or honorable humanoids or steeds.

THE RIVERFLOW

Where Bishal’s two rivers come together, there is a tranquil set of hills known as the Riverflow. This is where Bishalian lords come together when they need to confer on a national level.

The waters of the Riverflow run cold and sweet. I’m not much a fan of Bishalian warmongering, but the Riverflow, at least, is a place of peace.

-Chauncey

BRIDGEMOOR

Bridgemoor is an economically powerful independent city-state built entirely on a massive bridge that spans the Great Chasm. As the only other ways to cross the chasm involve going hundreds of miles north to circumvent it or climbing down, navigating across its monster-riddled trenches, and climbing back up, Bridgemoor has made a fortune off of merchants and travelers that use the city-state as passage over the Chasm.

Bridgemoor is just large enough that a full day’s travel will not quite get one across the chasm, forcing travelers to spend extra coin in one of Bridgemoor’s many inns and taverns. Through this calculated design and by exacting a small toll on every traveler, Bridgemoor thrives.

Bridgemoor is governed by an oligarchy known as the Meritocrats. Selected once every ten years, prospective Meritocrats must first pass a test proving their scholastic and economic knowledge, and then each former Meritocrat picks their successor. This has generally led to rampant nepotism with very rare exceptions.

Academic. Bridgemoor has a large university and scholastic achievements are widely celebrated throughout the city-state. Those with more formal education are more likely to be rewarded by Meritocrats or become one themselves, so anyone who can afford to send their children to the university will do so.

Mercantile and Trade. Bridgemoor is a thriving mercantile state. Due to a high volume of travelers and merchants flowing into and through the city-state, Bridgemoor has one of the strongest economies in its region. It trades especially frequently with Nalathesh and Kendaria as it connects the two nations, but merchants from other nations will stop in Bridgemoor on their way to or from Kendaria and Nalathesh as well.

Bridgemoor is vital to the health of the Great Chasm nations. Without it, trade patterns would be thrown into horrible disarray.

-Marcus

Militant. Bridge Knight Commander Ser Kellion Blacksky maintains strict discipline in the streets of Bridgemoor. Under his guidance, the Bridge Knights have become more disciplined and competent than they have ever been historically. Few in number, the Bridge Knights could not wage war on a neighboring country, but they could defend Bridgemoor indefinitely or until food and water run out.

Political. The Meritocrats oversee legislation and appoint the Bridge Knight Commander and the Master Magister. These two figures, in turn, will appoint their subordinate administrators (Bridge Knight Captains and Magisters, respectively) to maintain executive and judicial law within the city.

Religious. Bridgemoor does not have an official religion, and each citizen is encouraged to pursue their own beliefs. Several temples dedicated to specific pantheons or tenets can be found throughout Bridgemoor, along with unaffiliated houses of worship that offer a quiet, contemplative space for any belief system.

Social. Bridgemoor has a very diverse culture. Folk from Nalathesh and Kendaria make up most of Bridgemoor’s citizens, but some traders from lands beyond decided to settle in Bridgemoor as well, leading to a confluence of many beliefs. It’s not uncommon for there to be at least two festivals in a given month due to the wide spectrum of cultures and religions and their respective celebratory events.

Underworld. Bridgemoor has no shortage of white-collar criminals that use any loophole in the city-state’s tax system and mercantile economy to line their own pockets with a little more silver and gold. Overt crime is uncommon thanks to Ser Kellion’s iron discipline, but cult activity lurks in the sewers and storage cellars beneath the streets. Called the Underbridge Cult, these miscreants are a countercultural movement looking to prove how meaningless and absurd “Meritocracy” truly is. Among other things, they try to summon fiends from Inferno to wreak havoc in the city.
