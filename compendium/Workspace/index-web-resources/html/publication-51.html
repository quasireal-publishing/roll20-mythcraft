CHAPTER 9 | EXPLORING ANCERRA

Militant. Eryndor has a large and disciplined military. Some of the military occupies the southern border to guard against aggression from Roshallan, while most of the rest maintain the Great Wall to Eryndors northeast. This wall predates the nation, built in a previous supereon, but the Eryndorans obsessively maintain the wall. Nightmarish monsters from Nachtskog lead regular, vicious attacks against the fortification with utmost hostility to the countrys citizens.

Eryndors military often coordinates with Skavrs, as the two nations both suffer from the monstrous attacks that come from the haunted woods.

The Knights of Eryndor are the military elite. Skillful both on the battlefield and as rangers in the woodlands, the Knights train rigorously and are inspirational to all who fight beside them.

Religious. Viewing themselves as the last line of defense against the horrors of Nachtskog, Eryndorans view themselves with a sense of holy pride. Their patron goddess, Rasheen, is believed to prowl the planes of existence to vanquish burgeoning threats. The Eryndorans often lead military expeditions into the woods near Nachtskog, which takes on religious significance for many soldiers and their families.

Social. Most Eryndorans are earnest, duty-oriented folk. From farmers to soldiers to politicians, the average Eryndoran views their job with a seriousness that other nationalities might find excessive. To the Eryndorans, each person offers an important contribution to the fight against the horrors of Nachtskog.

Underworld. While most Eryndorans view their place in the world as valiant knights against the forces of darkness, there are some who - for any number of reasons - are disillusioned with Eryndor or the world at large. These folk often form secretive cults that declare the glory of the monsters from Nachtskog and try to undermine the defenses of the Great Wall.

BRIGHTGUARD

Eryndors shining capital city, Brightguard is a thriving port city built on the mouth of the Eryndor River. A sizable port allows for trade with other nations in the Inner Sea, though Eryndors merchant vessels are not heavily armed enough to safely sail into Renden waters.

Brightguards Republic Palace is a large building made of fortified stone and military buildings on the outside and plush, luxurious trappings deep in the interior complex of buildings. From this building, the chancellor guides the nation.

POINTS OF INTEREST

The Great Wall. Nobody has yet discovered who built the Great Wall, but Eryndoran soldiers maintain it religiously.

They believe that one day the wall will fall, heralding the end times, and the Knights of Eryndor must be ready to raise their swords one final time when this day comes.

Sounds like they need me up there at the Wall.

-Grim

Nachtskog. Stretching for scores of miles beyond Eryndors wall, Nachtskog is a swathe of blighted and haunted woodlands. Home to hag covens, fiends that made it through portals from Inferno, and all manner of horrifying creatures, Nachtskog is a deadly place and its savage progeny often rush forth to attack the Great Wall. With gnarled branches black against the sky, Nachtskogs World-Tree is a twisted and hideous facsimile of the lifegiving world-trees found in other nations. Like the World-Trees in Muskshir and on the border of Sozein and Tafunga, this World-Trees roots connect back to the original World-Tree: Starborea in Doshairiel.

I have been to Nachtskog, yes. My teeth still crave the flesh of the beasts therein.

-Hanniah

QUEST IDEAS

The Knights of Eryndor have lost one of their respected commanders who ventured east of the Great Wall. Will the heroes lead a rescue mission?

Roshallans war drums beat again, and Eryndors southern border is under threat. Can the heroes shore up Eryndors defenses?

Eryndors current chancellor is corrupt and cruel. Many in Eryndor wish to see them removed from office; using legal or revolutionary means, can the heroes enact such a change?

Cultists near the Great Wall are planning something terrible. Can the heroes find the cultists, unearth their plan, and prevent it before they open the floodgates to the horrors of Nachtskog?

FALTH

A federation of large cities sprawling from the lowest, most navigable point in the Cloudpiercer Range to the coast of the Inner Sea, the vast majority of Falths cities are run by mafiosos and criminal kingpins.

Falth generally shares the natural climate of southern Doshairiel: deciduous forests, humid summers, and rainy winters. Falth is rockier and hillier than most of Doshairiel, and the easternmost reaches of Falth - through the Cloudpiercer Pass - are scrubby desertland that receive little rain year on year.

Academic. Many of Falths cities fund universities or academies. The crime lords that run the cities typically believe that an educated citizenry will lead to more economic prosperity, even if it does come with potential threats to their power.

Aristocratic and Political. Each of Falths cities are run autonomously by crime families. The larger families, along with those that have intermarried, control two or more cities that almost function as miniature nations within the Falthic federation. Falth is primarily held into cohesion due to shared cultural history and the mob bosses mutual respect for one another. While one mafioso would order another one dead without hesitation, that does not mean that they do not admire each others clever political machinations and see one another as respected rivals.

Everyone reports to someone. Even someone as feared as my uncle still has his superiors that he has to keep happy.

-Avery

Mercantile and Trade. Falths rocky foothills near the Cloudpiercer Mountains produce a healthy amount of precious metals and gems. Farther to the west, the forested regions of Falth offer lumber and plant and animal byproducts. Fiercely capitalistic crime lords will compete with one another to sell their goods on the international black market.

Given Falths central location in the middle of Gathandia, many merchants find it difficult to get from the east to the west and back without traveling through Falth. Most mob bosses allow safe passage through their towns in exchange for hefty tolls.

Militant. Each crime lord maintains a private army of thugs, spies, and assassins. Those that prove incompetent are quickly disposed of, and even common grunts know that they play a dangerous game. Most crime lords put a good deal of resources towards buying out the thugs and spies that work for their rivals. If the price is right, and a spy becomes a double agent, it can be lucrative, but at the risk of death if they are discovered.

Religious. While some Falthic commoners might hold religious beliefs, it is uncommon for anyone in Falth to act in obviously moral fashion. Those who do refrain from stealing, grifting, or conning are usually left behind. A few mafiosos practice religion, though it is hard to tell whether this is borne of true conviction or a desire to project an appearance of moral superiority.

Social and Underworld. Needless to say, even the lowliest Falthic commoner will not go out of their way to do favors for anyone unless something is in it for them. An act of pure, uncalculated altruism is as rare as a five-moon eclipse in this selfish land.

The one notable exception is the Gray Hoods. Led by former members of the Hoods syndicate in Thunderhead Vale, the Gray Hoods use all of the unscrupulous methods of typical Falthic mobs, but they use these methods in an effort to build a better society. They steal from crime lords and give money to the needy, trying to foster a sense of goodwill among the common folk. When possible, they leap at the opportunity to assassinate a corrupt leader and try to replace them with one that has at least some measure of scruples.

DIRGE

Felson Weskh, a cunning polar urkou, is widely regarded as the most powerful and influential crime lord in all of Falth. His city, Dirge, is the largest in Falth and straddles both a lucrative mine and the main road that traverses the Cloudpiercer Pass. Dirge is miles wide and also covers many rocky hillsides that stretch west of the Cloudpiercer foothills. Felson deviously manipulates the lesser crime lords, ensuring that they fight one another rather than challenging his authority over this vital region. He has designs on uniting some of the least ambitious crime lords into an organized nation, from which he will springboard his conquest of the surrounding nations, beginning with Nalathesh.
