FJORDENHAVEN

If not for the volcanic activity, Fjordenhaven would not likely exist. Sulfurous hot springs and lava flows provide warmth enough to maintain a boreal climate despite the scouring, frigid winds that sweep down from the north pole.

Fjordenhaven developed along these hot springs, making great use of the heat to produce both agriculture and energy for industry. The hot spots beneath the surface of the earth extend up a chain of craggy islands, and Fjordenhaven has inhabited all of these islands capable of supporting vegetation.

Fjordenhavens architecture is simple, with stone foundations and walls and steep wood-shingled roofs. They commonly decorate their entryways with scrimshaw carvings or decorative caribou antler displays.

Academic. Fjordenhavens capital has a large library with conference rooms and study halls, but does not have formal education. Mystics and historians pass on their wisdom orally, traveling from town to town sharing their knowledge.

Aristocratic and Political. Fjordenhaven is a traditional monarchy, the heirship passing from eldest child to eldest child. The younger siblings of the monarch are assigned governing positions over various regions, towns, or industries. Its current monarch is King Alfur Svansson.

Mercantile and Trade. Fjordenhaven has a surprising number of wares to offer in international trade. They have a large shepherding community and industrial textile mills that process wool into fine cloth. They also mine valuable minerals from the volcanic mountains.

Fjordenhaven maintains a few large caravels that make long voyages down Gathandias west coast, trading with Ruptaea, Vaenyth, and Pash.

Militant. Fjordenhavens folk are hardened by the adversity of long, cold winters. The Fjordenhavian military is small but strong, and they maintain several fortified outposts along the mainlands coast and their islands.

Religious. Like many northern folk, the Fjordenhavian are fascinated by the northern lights. They believe it to be a manifestation of the Celestial Councils will, and believe that when it appears in the sky, its colors and the sequence in which they appear foretell different events to come. Fjordenhavian priests interpret these signs, and the monarchy usually acts based on these predictions.

Social. Fjordenhavians punish crime with swift and decisive harshness. A thief might have their hand chopped off and exiled from their town. If they are given no reason to be suspicious, however, the common folk are usually very warm to visitors from other towns and other nations.

It is said that Fjordenhavens geysers and hot springs are rich with medicinal minerals. Those who bathe in the waters emerge cleansed of any skin deformities and even some diseases. I would love to witness this myself one day.

SKEVIK

Skevik, the capital of the nation, is just north of the eastern river. Spread along the riverbank and the coast leading out to the Ice Sea, Skevik is the largest port city in Fjordenhaven. In addition to the library and the castle of the monarchy, Skevik also has a grand chapel with a beautiful pipe organ that is almost always in use, spinning its haunting melodies through the frigid air.

The Frostvale. A wide valley between two icy mountain ranges, the Frostvale is a massive graveyard. Divided into a miles-wide plot of land for the common folk, a sizeable plot for warriors that fell in battle, and cavernous mausoleums for deceased monarchs, Fjordenhavians visit the Frostvale when they wish to pay respects to any of their ancestors. FORSTEN

Forsten exists almost entirely within the Old Forest. With mild summers and cold winters that often bring snow, the folk of Forsten are a tough lot. Most treat the forest with deep respect and a little fear.

Once a single kingdom along with Constel and Kendaria, Forsten has only seceded from Constel within the past couple generations. The oldest folk of Forsten would remember the days when it was the northern half of a larger kingdom, though virtually none living today would recall it being one with Kendaria, which split away after a civil war several hundred years hence.

Academic. Most Forsteners place more stock in ones ability to chop timber or skin game than in their ability to recite poetry. Formal education is uncommon, but

entertainers do hold a special place in society as they spin fanciful tales long after the sun has set.

Aristocratic and Political. Forsten is a troubled nation. Having only been its own autonomous country for a few score years, many burgomasters of middling towns feel that they are qualified to manage the entire nation. Although the young dwarf Janice Magillory is nominally the queen of Forsten, she commands little respect and has been unable to cajole other political figures into following her leadership.

Mercantile and Trade. Although its relationship with Constel is still quite tender, Forsten maintains a trade relationship with its neighbor. Many Forsten merchants are also sailors and trade with Kendaria to the south and Derk to the north. Brusch Clydesden, a renowned urkou explorer in his early life and skilled long-distance trader now that he is getting on in years, will occasionally guide a merchant vessel as far north as Yodvan.

Forsten primarily exports timber, although some of the locals fear that deforestation will anger the ancient gods of the Old Forest, as they believe happened in Constel.

Militant. Forsteners tend to be a strong, sturdy lot and make competent fighters. Many hunters, lumberjacks, fishers, and long-distance merchants have had to fight off numerous monstrosities from the untamed stretches of the Old Forest. Janice Magillory wants to establish a national army, but has been unable to rally support for such a movement.

Religious. Forsteners are quite spiritual and often superstitious. Many believe that small actions will have unforeseen consequences and worry about the wrath of the old gods. Most will pray to multiple pantheons, but especially the Shibmar Conclave of the Archfae.

Social. Forsten is not a particularly hospitable region. Fear of the forest has led many to treat strangers with suspicion or outright hostility, even if the strangers are just from another town in Forsten.

Underworld. Due to Janice Magillorys utter failure to unite her country, crime syndicates flourish in the underbellies of all major cities in Forsten. Smugglers traffic weapons, gems, and drugs in and out of their nation, and mercenaries will take any protection or assassination job for the right price.
