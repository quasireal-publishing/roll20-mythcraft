Fighting Uvior

Uvior fights with great tact, snuffing out light sources to keep the upper hand and summoning skeletons to overwhelm the heroes. He tries not to engage in melee, using his abilities to keep the heroes at bay. He tries to continuously maintain a tether so that he can pass damage off to other creatures whenever he is harmed.

Dark Flight. Uvior gains a fly speed equal to his walking speed while in dim light or darkness. If he is exposed to bright light while flying, he falls.

Growing Horde. When Uvior Takes a Rest, at the end of that time he automatically summons 1d4 Skeletons.

ACTIONS

On Uviors turn, he can MOVE and take TWO of the following actions.

Move. Uvior moves up to his speed.

Assailing Shadow. Ranged 60 ft Arcane Spell. +3 vs WILL of a creature in dim light or darkness. Hit: 6 (1d12) necrotic and target is Tethered.

Draining Touch. Reach 5 ft Natural Weapon. +3 vs AR. Hit: 8 (1d10+3) necrotic and target is Tethered.

Snuff. One source of nonmagical light or light from a divine prayer or primal chant within 60 ft of Uvior is extinguished.

OR Uvior can MOVE and take the following action.

Undead Horde. Uvior summons 1d4-1 Skeletons, which appear in random unoccupied spaces within 30 ft of him.

REACTIONS

Tether (1/Round). When Uvior takes damage, he may use his reaction to choose a creature that he can see within 60 ft of him. If that creature has been Tethered since the start of Uviors last turn, then that creature takes of the damage that Uvior would have taken.

FIGHTING MOGG

Mogg fights cautiously, hiding as much as able and using his magic to animate furniture to fight for him. Other gretchlings that participate in this battle gain access to the Disarm maneuver and will attempt to run off with the heroes weapons and gear.

RESOLUTION

The adventure ends when the animosity between Uvior and Mogg is resolved. This will happen in one of three ways:

The party confronts and defeats Uvior

The party confronts and defeats Mogg

The party negotiates a peace between them

The best possible outcome for the heroes is negotiating a peace between Uvior and Mogg, and using Greater Restoration on Humphrey the Cleric. Doing so will result in the heroes getting the maximum amount of treasure from both Uvior and Mogg (see their respective random encounter entries for this treasure), and earns them +4 Goodwill whenever they enter a settlement that is home to an adventurer who was turned to stone at Uviors tragic banquet.
