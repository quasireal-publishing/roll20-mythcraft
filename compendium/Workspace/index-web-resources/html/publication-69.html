Social. Most Valstromians are content with their feudal hierarchy, but some are discontent with it. Many of the peasantry have their basic needs met: food, drink, comfortable shelter, and basic leisure activities. These peasants often don’t care whether they get ahead in life or not, so long as their needs remain fulfilled. Some of the peasantry, by contrast, think that the feudal system is out of date and should have been abolished long ago.

Many counts and dukes also feel tremendous discontent. Having achieved success, but knowing that the dukes will always have more power and influence, counts and countesses often lose sleep scheming about how they might get ahead in the political system. Dukes and duchesses, in turn, often fret about maintaining the power they have.

Underworld. Valstrom’s traditional feudal structure leaves ample room for crime syndicates to operate in the shadows, as details often get lost in the chain of command from mayor to monarch and back. Notable syndicates include the Twice Strikes, the Hoods, and the Galepiercers (see Shadradoth for information on each syndicate).

STORMVALE

The capital and oldest city in Valstrom is Stormvale. Stormvale is an expansive city that extends from the banks of the Thundervale River up into the foothills of the Hadla Mountains. Parts of Stormvale have fallen into utter disrepair and are almost more forest than city at this point, but much of the city - especially that closest to the river - thrives as a bustling metropolis.

The royal castle overlooks the river and is surrounded by an artificial moat connected to the river. On the mainland, the Stormvale University holds classrooms and dormitories full of intellectuals.

The Stormvale Cathedral is also a marvel of architectural and artistic achievement, and the religious among the nation often travel to behold the wondrous stained glass depictions of the various members of the Celestial Council.

POINTS OF INTEREST

Cloudpiercers. See Shadradoth for information on the Cloudpiercer Range and the tallest mountain in the known world.

Thundervale River. The Valstromians and Shadradothans have built a shared city at the base of a magical waterfall in the Thundervale River. See Shadradoth for more information on this remarkable place.

QUEST IDEAS

Valstrom faces another heirship crisis, and various nobles across the kingdom make their power plays. The heroes must interact with these nobles and determine who they wish to support.

The entire Valstromian/Shadradothan town at the base of the magic waterfall has vanished. Perhaps it’s in the Everwilds.

Monsters have been reported in the overgrown neighborhoods of Stormvale. Will the heroes free the city from these menaces?

Adventurers and a famous noble have gone missing after boasting that they could climb the Great Cloudpierce, the tallest mountain in the world. Another noble calls upon the heroes to mount a rescue mission.

VEL

A tiny but powerful city-state clinging to the craggy beaches of Gathandia’s southeast peninsula, Vel is ruled by a kleptocratic oligarchy known as the Mages of the Beach. The Mages control every aspect of life in Vel and their powerful diviners keep a watchful eye on the city-state, ready to quash dissent at a moment’s notice. Common laborers and merchants live in crowded, narrow, and steep switchback streets built precariously along the beach’s steep cliffs, and the Mages’ enormous towers soar high above the cliffs like jagged teeth.

On the beach itself, trained cannon crabs ward off any plunderers and a single fortified pier allows merchant vessels to trade with Vel.

Vel’s influence is felt far and wide internationally, and the Mages of the Beach have a thinly concealed desire to dominate the world, first through the soft power of influence and then through the hard power of conquest.

Academic. The Mages of the Beach have a large university in one of their central towers. They commonly invite well-known scholars to spend time teaching in their university’s halls, and while scholars visit Vel, the Mages aggressively try to brainwash these scholars to mirror Vel’s philosophical talking points when the scholars set forth for other universities.

Aristocratic and Political. The Mages of the Beach hold unquestionable control over every aspect of life in Vel. Any administrative position or legal office is held by a Mage of the Beach.

Mercantile and Trade. Vel is currently pursuing an international strategy of soft influence. They export entertainment goods, games, novels, and leisure activities, trying to inculcate Velan culture into every other nation on Gathandia.

Highly interested in the city-state of Husk for its location on a point of power, Velan scholars are working on developing a way to open a permanent teleportation portal between two areas so that they can trade with Husk much more readily.

Militant. Under the command of General Vilhelm Cynthar, a skilled combat mage, Vel’s military is highly trained and effective. Every Velan soldier is capable of basic magic casting of some kind and is trained in fighting with unique magical weapons.

Religious. The Mages of the Beach have introduced a unique religion to Vel. Many gods are inscribed on illuminated cards, which citizens are encouraged to buy and trade. The Mages of the Beach started with pantheons that are commonly accepted throughout Gathandia, and released fewer copies of certain gods to increase their scarcity. The more god-points you have, the more religious you are and the more blessed you will be, the Mages declare.

They have released several “seasons” of cards and have made up a vast number of fictitious pantheons so that they can keep printing more and more cards. The more cards that they print, the higher number of god-points a commoner will need in order to maintain their good fortune.

Their bastardization of religion for personal profit is utterly disgraceful. Tovare forgive me, but I hope the whole lot of them rot in the dungeons of Melaneth.

-Marcus

Social. It is governmentally required that every merchant and every commoner is happy and merry at least 80% of any given week. In order to promote this idea, mandatory festivals and gaming tournaments are commonplace.

Underworld. The vigilant diviners among the Mages of the Beach are constantly on the lookout for signs of criminal conspiracies and are quick to make examples of anyone who gets conspiratorial inclinations. They do not tolerate black markets in Vel, but encourage Velan merchants to engage in black market negotiations in other nations.

QUEST IDEAS

The heroes might have a run-in with Velan merchants or mages anywhere in Gathandia. This may lead the heroes to look for a way to reduce Vel’s influence or even overthrow the malignant city-state entirely.

YODVAN

Long ago, the bravest sailors from Fjordenhaven braved the Ice Seas of the north and made landfall on a strange and wondrous island of snow. They explored a forest whose plants seemed to be made of a vegetable-metallic hybrid and ventured into the fog-choked mountains beyond. There, they found Yodvan’s true treasure: a mythically hard and strong material that came to be known as adamantine.

Several expeditions later, the Fjordenhavians had sent enough people and supplies to this island that they were able to establish a permanent outpost. It grew into a city, and from there several more towns were established as eager miners and explorers ventured into the Glowing Forest and the Mountains of Adamant. Eventually, facing political pressure from his youngest sister and struggling to manage Fjordenhaven proper and the distant island of Yodvan, the king of Fjordenhaven gave the island of Yodvan to his sister as an independent nation.

The snowblown island of Yodvan experiences days where the sun does not set during the summer, and experiences long nights during winter. In the darkest days of winter, the sun does not rise at all.

Academic. Aurora’s Hearth, the capital of Yodvan, has a large scientific academy that conducts endless experimentation with adamantine and the various biotechnical discoveries that explorers find in the Glowing Forest.

Aristocratic and Political. Like Fjordenhaven, Yodvan practices a monarchy of pure primogeniture: the eldest child of each monarch becomes the next monarch. Subsequent children (or other family members) are assigned to be counts and countesses over Yodvan’s towns.

Mercantile and Trade. To this day, Yodvan remains independent but is closely allied with its parent nation: Fjordenhven sends foodstuffs to Yodvan in exchange for an influx of glass lights and adamantine. Yodvan can sell its commodity goods for enormous returns in virtually any nation that it can reach.
