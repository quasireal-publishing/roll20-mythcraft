VADAN

The capital of Vadan, by the same name, was the third-largest city in Unjaarn before the secession. While most of the citizens of the capital strongly prefer Vadan’s system of governance to Unjaarn’s, there are clear signs of poverty in Vadan’s mucky alleyways, shantytowns, and glamorous fenced-off neighborhoods.

The capital is home of Vadan’s only university, which is also where the governmental buildings are located. Vadan is run by a parliament of the fifteen wealthiest citizens of the country. This is reassessed every half-year, which in theory would allow for a quickly rotating door of legislators. In practice, a couple dozen wealthy elites spend most of their time running their businesses or passing legislation that benefit their business or hurt their rivals, ensuring that they remain in the legislative shortlist.

POINTS OF INTEREST

Swamplands of the Great Chasm. See Buyleist for more information.

QUEST IDEAS

Some impoverished and embittered citizens want to start a revolution against the parliament and ask the heroes to lead them.

A smuggler needs the heroes to bribe an administrator in Unjaarn to help get some Vadanian immigrants into the southern country.

A beloved member of the community has gone missing in the deep bayou. Their friends hope that the heroes can find them.

Rumor has it that the parliament plans on passing legislation that will make Vadan a monarchy. Rival merchants ask the heroes to prevent this at any cost.

VAENYTH

In a word, Vaenyth is cruel. Its jagged shale hills are full of dangerous monsters and deep chasms. Its sparse woodlands are equally perilous. Vaenyth’s mountain range plays havoc on weather patterns and the nation suffers from seasonal typhoons, hailstorms, and tornadoes.

Vaenyth’s population is a humanoid incarnation of this turbulent cruelty. With a savage governing system and near-constant civil wars, Vaenyth is a nation of anger and blood.

Academic. Vaenythan culture places little value in formal education. One’s capacity to perform their job well is usually all that matters.

Aristocratic and Political. With perhaps the most bizarre governing system in Gathandia, Vaenyth chooses their new monarch once every ten years. When they do so, each noble family who wishes to make a bid for the monarchy sends forth a champion to fight on their behalf. These champions each battle to the death in a massive arena riddled with traps and captured monsters. The sole surviving champion is ritually slaughtered, and the new monarch bathes in their blood before ascending to the throne for the next ten years.

So, I’ve been known to summon the occasional demon, but even I think that the Vaenythans have all lost their bloody minds.

-Dungrel

Mercantile and Trade. Vaenyth generally does not trade with other nations, with the exception of Pash. Vaenyth produces iron ore, stone from quarries, and animal and plant byproducts, which primarily circulate within Vaenyth’s own economy.

Militant. Vaenyth’s military is quite strong and well-equipped but lacks discipline and cohesion. More often than not, Vaenythan military contingents go to war with one another than with common enemies.

Religious. Vaenythans primarily worship the Exiles and the Archfiends of Inferno. They believe that the most powerful deities are those who can survive in the least comfortable circumstances. This power then translates into understanding and knowledge. Vaenythan priestly rituals often involve animal slaughter and occasionally include humanoid sacrifice.

Social and Underworld. Criminal activity is standard in any Vaenythan settlement. Cheating on taxes, mugging a neighbor in a back alley, petty burglary, and vengeance-driven murder are all commonplace. The only law of the land is the proclamations of the monarch, and even then, these are more suggestions than hard and fast decrees.

BLOODSTONE

The capital citadel of Vaenyth and the death-soaked throne of the monarchy is Bloodstone. This is a massive fortress carved from stone and reinforced with an iron-Arcanium alloy developed by ancient mystics long ago and since forgotten to history. A hundred thousand soldiers have been turned away by a mere eighty that held Bloodstone, the legends say.

POINTS OF INTEREST

Floating Islands. The floating islands that stretch as far south as Pash’s Great Bay soar high above Vaenyth. Given their disinterest in scholarly pursuits, Vaenythans have no idea that Arcanium and Primor could be mined from these islands in abundance.

QUEST IDEAS

Vaenyth’s rulers ask the heroes to infiltrate Pash and siphon large sums of money back into Vaenyth’s own pockets.

The monarch of Vaenyth asks the heroes to find a way to reach the floating islands and crash them down to the ground so that they can be studied.

Vaenyth has decided to hold a special electoral battle with teams of champions. The heroes have been chosen as one such team.

VALSTROM

In the shadowy Thunderhead Vale to the east of the Cloudpiercer Range, the kingdom of Valstrom sprawls along the Thunderhead River. With the Cloudpiercers to Valstrom’s west and north, and the Hadla mountains to its east, Valstrom catches a tremendous amount of rain and humidity from the Kozia Rainforest. Valstrom’s summers are hot and humid, its winters chilly and often snowy. Its falls are vibrant with bright leaves, its springs with bright flowers.

Valstrom’s charming seasons have drawn many peoples over the generations, and in the clearings of the subtropical forests its ancient towns are steeped in layers of history. Most towns and all of Valstrom’s cities are heavily timber-

based, with log buildings and wooden tiles or thatched roofs. A few fortified buildings, such as the town watch and the lord or lady’s castle, are predominantly classical stone castles.

Generations ago, Shadradoth split away from Valstrom as an independent nation. When the king passed away, his chief advisor became king regent, but refused to abdicate when the late king’s daughter came of age. The resulting civil war tore the kingdom in half, and Princess Shadra, unable to claim the throne from Rehan Jehoram, had to make do with her half of the kingdom. The Jehorams continue their rule to this day.

A pleasant natural environment mixed with dozens of tragic stories. I may be here a while.

-Flanagan

Academic. Valstrom’s larger towns usually have some kind of academy or university. Some of these are in a state of disuse, but many still have thriving libraries, research facilities, and classes.

Aristocratic and Political. A classical feudal society has been implemented in Valstrom for all of its recorded history. Mayors report to counts and countesses, who report to dukes and duchesses, who report to the monarch. The monarch’s eldest child assumes the monarchy after their parent passes away or abdicates the throne.

Mercantile and Trade. Valstrom has had its share of unrest, but over many generations it has proven to be a reliable trade partner. Its neighboring land nations include Sozein and Tafunga to the south, and Falth, Nalathesh, and Husk to the north. Valstrom maintains a robust trade relationship with Shadradoth despite their historical tensions. Merchants competent in mountain passes will also trade with Hadla to the east.

Militant. Valstrom maintains its classical feudal system in how its military functions. Each mayor has a small local army that keeps the peace in and around their settlement. Counts and countesses also have small armies, and have the political capital to levy the armies of the mayors that live within their territories. Dukes and duchesses can likewise levy any army under the control of any count in their province, and the monarch can of course levy any duke’s or duchess’s forces when the need arises.

Religious. Valstromians traditionally worship the Celestial Council, seeing its leaders as the gods that bless or curse them in accordance with their piety, or lack thereof. Any settlement will have at least a small temple with an altar dedicated to these gods, and the larger cities compete with one another to have the grandest, most beautiful temple out of any Valstromian society.
