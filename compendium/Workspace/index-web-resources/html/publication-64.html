Religious. Some Shadradothans share religious beliefs with the Valstromians. They worship the Celestial Council and believe that the good or evil actions of an individual will be blessed or punished by the Council. Other Shadradothans believe that the Exiles should be venerated and see their own nation as exiled in a sense, as Shadra should have been the rightful queen of Valstrom.

Social. Shadradoth’s population is angry and distrusting. They cannot agree with one another on politics, religion, or basic cultural practices. This is less pronounced within individual settlements, where folk may overlook perceived slights in their neighbors’ behavior. On a national scale, however, many towns and regions despise one another.

Underworld. Given the chaos inherent in Shadradoth’s culture and politics, crime syndicates flourish. Notable syndicates include the Twice Strikes, the Hoods, and the Galepiercers. These syndicates operate widely in both Shadradoth and Valstrom.

The Twice Strikes get their name from their earned reputation of robbing a place twice: first nonviolently burgling it and getting a lay of the land, and second by brutally attacking any who try to defend the place. The Hoods are beloved by the peasantry as they rob from the rich and give their richest to the poor. Finally, the Galepiercers are so named for the elementals that they summon to cause chaos, which they use to draw attention from their illicit activity.

THUNHARAN

Formerly the capital city of Shadradoth, Thunharan is now a smoking ruin. After the tremendous civil war that ravaged Shadradoth, there is no central authority around which all regions can unite.

POINTS OF INTEREST

Cloudpiercers. The Cloudpiercer Range’s tallest mountains overshadow Shadradoth. One of the mountains is so enormous that nobody has ever seen its peak. It always disappears above the clouds, or is so tall that the slight haze of the atmosphere obscures its magnificent heights. Although foolish adventurers have tried to climb it, none have even come remotely close to seeing its peak.

Thundervale River. In the bend of the Thundervale River, the Shadradothans have discovered a wondrous point of power. The river strikes a cliff and flows up the face of it like an upside-down waterfall. It then continues on its flow southward towards Sozein.

As Valstrom’s sovereign territory begins on the other half of the river, Shadradoth and Valstrom have agreed to construct a neutral city at the base of the waterfall so that they can experience its wonder together. Explorers have found that the waterfall also contains a portal to the Everwilds.

QUEST IDEAS

Boiling discontent washes through Shadharoth as multiple nobles and democratically elected officials each claim that they should oversee the entire nation. The heroes might get involved to make a power play themselves or to back a contender whose views they find commendable.

A noble who claims to be the direct descendent of Shadra asks the heroes to guard them as they travel to the Great Cloudpierce, the tallest mountain in the world, where they believe they can procure evidence verifying their claim.

Valstrom has conquered the city that they have historically shared with Shadradoth at the base of the waterfall. Nobles and diplomats ask the heroes to negotiate with both word and sword to reclaim the Shadradothans’ right to occupy the city.

SKAVR

It is said that the Skavri are born with axes in hand and sent to battle fell beasts by their second birthday. A cold, hard nation of cold, hard people, Skavr sprawls across the northwest forests of the Inner Sea.

The Skavri stretch of forest is old and steeped in ancient legend. The boreal trees themselves seem to whisper of the many monsters, battles, and feats of heroism that they have witnessed over the generations. Skavr has chilly summers and icy winters. As brutal winds from the Ice Sea roll down on Skavr, snow smothers the nation and the Northern Lights seem to taunt the frigid settlements.

The Skavri are skilled woodcarvers and carpenters. Their buildings have steep, wood-shingled roofs that drain the heavy snowfall down to the ground, but their buildings are as decorative as they are functional. Relief scenes are carved in the outsides of many log cabins and merchant stores, and these scenes generally depict tales of Skavri heroism in the face of any number of horrid monsters or the dreaded Renden raids.

Academic. The capital of Skavr possesses a large library run by a handful of dedicated scholars, but beyond this there are no formal institutes of education in the nation.

Aristocratic and Military. Skavr is a nation of warrior-kings. Each town is governed by the strongest warrior, who tends to legislative and judicial responsibilities during times of peace and who leads their warriors from the front lines during times of war.

At any point during times of peace, another warrior may challenge their town’s warrior-king for the throne. The challenger and the incumbent battle with blunted weapons until one yields. When incumbents feel that they are too old to continue leading battles from the front lines, they may abdicate to a challenge whom they believe is a worthy heir.

All warrior-kings defer to the High King. The High King can also be challenged by warrior-kings, but not by any Skavri citizen who is not a warrior-king. Skavr’s current High King, named Muris Irontusk, is an enormous trollkin who has no decorum or manners but is nonetheless an intuitively successful leader.

Rule by the sword. They’re my kind of people.

-Hanniah

The Skavri are competent sailors and stalwart soldiers. The belligerent nation of Renden often attacks coastal towns and cities, but find that the Skavri can hold them off more often than not.

Mercantile and Trade. Skavr trades readily enough with both Eryndor and Fjordenhaven, as its two closest neighbors. Skavr generally exports mercenaries and carved wood in exchange for food and spices.

Religious. The Skavri are not particularly religious, but those who do practice religion often worship the Vertumna Conclave of the Archfae, venerating each season as it comes in turn year after year.

Social. Skavri are generally hostile to foreigners and prefer to keep to themselves. The exception is folk from Eryndor, whom the Skavri see as siblings in the fight against the horrors of the forest. The Skavri also tolerate Fjordenhavians, but they privately believe these folk to be too soft for the harshness of the far north.

ICEKAIRN

The capital city of Skavr is a fortified mountain city, hard to access and even harder to besiege. From the stony walls, the High King commands their warrior-kings and calls them to arms when a national threat arises.

POINTS OF INTEREST

Nachtstrond and Nachtskog. Skavr often fights off terrible monsters from the Black Coast or the Black Woods. See Eryndor or Renden for more information.

QUEST IDEAS

Renden has invaded Skavr, and they have some kind of terrible new weapon. The High King calls on the heroes to discover what the Renden are using and eliminate it. Maybe it’s a terrible monster they have tamed, or maybe it’s an artifact they plundered from a merchant vessel.

Skavr and Eryndor mounted a joint quest into the heart of Nachtskog a year ago, but nobody ever returned. The heroes must venture into the Black Wood and determine what became of them.

Skavr and Eryndor pushed too far into the Black Wood and awakened some eldritch terror from its eternal slumber. Now it threatens to destroy vast swathes of Gathandia if the heroes cannot find a way to stop it.

SOZEIN

Sozein populates the western stretch of the Kozia Rainforest. Like Tafunga, Sozein has tremendous trees that divide the rainforest into three separate habitats: the emergent layer high above the rest of the forest, the canopy where most natural life dwells, and the forest floor, reserved primarily for rodents and some reptiles such as snakes and crocodiles. The Sozeini swathe of the Kozia Rainforest also has dense bamboo forests, which are smaller than the rainforest at large but are impossibly dense to navigate.

Sozein’s cities are savvily built with drainage in mind, directing monsoon rains to flood their water reservoirs rather than their streets.

Academic. Sozein’s largest cities all have well-staffed educational institutions. Magic and its practical applications is a chief area of study among Sozeini scholars.

Aristocratic and Political. Sozein operates on a governmental system similar to a feudal system, but with only a dynastic emperor and the local governors of settlements. There are no lords or ladies that act as middle management between individual towns and the dynastic authorities, though there are emissaries from the emperor trained to speak on behalf of the royal family. The same dynastic family, the Long’han dynasty, has been in charge of Sozein for over a thousand years.
