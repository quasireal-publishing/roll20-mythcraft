Mercantile and Trade. Sozein is known for its spices, dramatically flavored food, bright pigments, vegetation (especially bamboo), and mineral-rich clay. It exports these goods to Nausa, Shadradoth, Valstrom, and its merchant vessels will also take their wares as far west as Nent and as far east as Vel.

Militant. Sozein has a cutting-edge military with fighters that obsessively train in the art of using magic in combat. Different contingents of soldiers will focus on the same function of magic, and some elite contingents blend soldiers from multiple functional specializations.

Religious. The Sozeini worship five of the six Archfae Conclaves in equal measure. They have little concept of the Vertumna Conclave as the only seasons in the Kozia Rainforest are humid, rainy, and monsoon. The other five Conclaves are all important to Sozeini culture.

Social. Sozein offers little social safety for those who are unable to keep up with their jobs or with the advent of magic. Merchants and governors are often unnecessarily cutthroat in an effort to ensure that they will not be left behind.

Underworld. Spies from Tafunga engage in elaborate undercover missions to destabilize Sozein as much as possible. They might stoke the flames of social unrest or undermine a strategically important governor at a crucial moment.

TAFUNGA

Deep in the heart of the Kozia Rainforest, Tafunga is one of the most biodiverse regions in all Gathandia. The enormous rainforest can roughly be divided into three tiers. The emergent layer is composed of the tops of the highest trees, where raptors and other flying creatures roost. Beneath the emergent layer, the thick canopy is home to the majority of Tafungan life. Jaguars, monkeys, various birds and reptiles, and all manner of mythical creature make their homes in the thick, tangled overgrowth that ranges from a couple dozen to a couple hundred feet above the ground. Finally, the forest floor exists as a dark, shadowed realm that never sees the full light of day due to the thick canopy. Enormous rodents, snakes, and crocodiles lurk in this murky region.

The tribes of Tafunga primarily make their homes in the wide boughs of the canopy, though some tribes or criminal groups are denizens of the forest floor. Tribes that live in the canopy are generally stationary, and those on the forest floor are usually forced to be nomadic due to monsoon weather causing seasonal flooding.

Academic. Tafunga does not have schools confined to libraries and lecture halls. Instead, wise town and village elders will hold practical lessons with their students in the real world, showing them how to harvest mushrooms, interact safely with wild animals, navigate the trees, and the like.

Aristocratic and Political. Tafungan society revolves around tribal communities. These tribes generally cooperate with one another, and tribal leaders will typically defer to whichever leader in a negotiation has the most populous tribe. Tribes will often team up with one another to fight monsters or foreign nations that pose a threat to all involved. If someone feels that their leader is insufficient to the task, then they can challenge the leader to a psionic duel imposed by mushroom consumption.

Mercantile and Trade. Tafunga is most internationally notable for its exportation of many types of mushrooms with culinary, medicinal, and recreational properties. Tafungan merchants generally do not trade with Sozein due to their ongoing conflicts, but they will readily sell their mushrooms to Hadla, Nausa, Valstrom, and Shadradoth, as well as to any international merchants who make the journey to Tafunga. However, upstanding Tafungans do not sell the starcap mushroom to any foreigners, as this mushroom is considered sacred and commodifying it in any form of trade arrangement would be seen as blasphemous.

Militant. While all of Tafungaâ€™s tribes together do not have a particularly large military, they do have a key advantage in tactical combat. Tafungan soldiers consume mushroom spores that allow them to communicate telepathically with other soldiers, which allows them to silently coordinate their attacks. This makes them especially effective at sabotage, infiltration, and similar high-skill missions.

POINTS OF INTEREST

Kozia Sinkhole. In the heart of Tafunga, less than an hourâ€™s journey from the outskirts of Lootsdaot, the Kozia Sinkhole drops some two hundred feet down into the ground. It experiences flooding during monsoon season, but the Tafungans have dug artificial cave networks in and around the sinkhole to mitigate as much flooding as possible.
