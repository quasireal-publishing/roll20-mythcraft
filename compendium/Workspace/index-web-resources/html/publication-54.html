Academic. Most Getarese receive a mixed education. Cultivation of intelligence is highly valued in Getarese culture, so students usually learn a mixture of language skills, math, and history, but the focus is usually placed on science. Having discovered two strange minerals - Avadrium and Arcanium - in their shallow lakes, the Getarese enthusiastically pursue experimentation with these materials.

Political and Religious. Getar is a theocratic society. From the capital city of Pount, the Theocrats purportedly receive cryptic visions from the Avadri beyond and translate these mysteries into actionable policy. Citizens of Getar are expected to comply with these proclamations as both political and religious truth. In fact, now that Getar is controlled by Roshallan, the Theocrats simply do what Roshallani commanders order them to do.

Mercantile and Trade. Under the Roshallani Empire, Getar has become a valuable mining nation for the greater empire. They export their Avadrium and Arcanium to Roshallan’s mainland in exchange for undervalued compensation and the ongoing right to live on their own land.

Militant and Underworld. Weakened rebel forces engage in guerilla warfare against Roshallan, but to little avail. A network of Getarese thieves and saboteurs coordinate with the rebels of Chee in an effort to bring down the Roshallani.

Social. Before Roshallan’s occupation, most Getarese saw one another through a vague caste lens. The citizens of the outermost ring were considered the peasantry, and those on the innermost ring the nobles. Now that Roshallan has conquered Geter, most Getarese citizens see one another as victimized siblings and try to look out for their neighbors when able.

POUNT

The innermost island of Getar, its capital city, is home to the great temple of the Theocracy. This temple functions as both a religious center and a legislative hall.

Pount also has large industrial facilities where Avadrium and Arcanium are systematically processed and purified before being exported by the Roshallani navy. Well-equipped research facilities on the island are also staffed by renowned alchemists looking for new applications of these materials.

QUEST IDEAS

The Getarese want to recruit the heroes to help them covertly battle the Roshallani. Will the heroes join the side of the beleaguered rebels?

The Theocracy and/or leading scholars from the Getarese want the heroes to conduct some experiments with an Avadrium-Arcanium alloy. The resulting reaction teleports the heroes into the Celestial Sea, with no obvious way back…

HADLA

A quiet, uneventful, and unremarkable nation, Hadla can be the perfect place for retired heroes to spend the remainder of their years in undisturbed peace.

With steamy, rainy summers and damp, rainy winters, Hadla’s fertile soil can produce crops virtually year-round. The jungles that occupy its southwest can be dangerous, so younger would-be adventurers looking to make a name for themselves often head into the jungle looking for trouble. The rest of the nation, where most of its population resides, is quite peaceful and little ever seems to happen.

Academic. Bundimor has a large library, and most of the major cities and towns in Hadla have some form of library or school center. There are no major universities or academies in Hadla, so most scholarly information is passed down orally or ignored as unimportant.

Aristocratic and Political. Hadla operates on a traditional feudal model. The monarch delegates certain regions to the dukes and duchesses, who divide their regions into vassal states run by counts and settlements overseen by barons. Hadla’s current monarch is Queen Magya VI, who commands respect from the other nobles and of whom the common folk hold almost no opinion, positive or negative.

Mercantile and Trade. There are few natural resources of much worth in the nation aside from some pigment dyes that can be extracted from plants in the jungle. Capable of sustaining itself on its own food supply, Hadla’s economy primarily functions from inter-settlement trade, but some merchant caravans will occasionally journey north across a small mountain range to trade with Nalathesh. Others will descend into the Great Chasm to trade with Vadan and Unjaarn. Trade with Tafunga is very rare due to the undeveloped nature of the Hadlan jungle. There are no well-maintained roads and only a few outpost towns in this region.

Militant. As is traditionally the case of feudal nations, there is not a nationalized military. Instead, each noble has a small private militia that they can levy on their own behalf to quell bandits, or on behalf of their superiors when the dukes or monarchs fear that a neighboring nation might take interest in invading Hadla. All of the combined levied soldiers of Hadla would be unlikely to stave off an invasion, but none of Hadla’s neighboring nations have much interest in conquering them as they have little in the way of valuable resources.

Religious. Hadlans commonly worship the Celestial Council and the Stravon Conclave of the Archfae. Many Hadlans hold the belief that it can’t hurt to try to appease every deity, so many households have whittled likenesses of dozens of deities scattered throughout their houses and on their mantles.

Social. Hadlan society usually has some minor holiday coming up. With a focus on cycles of emotion, cycles of the seasons, and the cycle of life and death, every few weeks there will be a festival of one kind or another celebrating these states of existence.

I visited Hadla once. Didn’t like it. Too peaceful. Nothing exciting ever happened.

-Hanniah

Underworld. Many of the nobles in the feudal model want to expand their own importance and influence, and most are willing to employ underhanded means to achieve this. Hiring scoundrels to plant incriminating evidence on a rival noble is all too commonplace.

BUNDIMOR

The capital of Hadla overlooks the second-largest of the nation’s lakes. The monarch’s palace is a traditional stone castle with a large enough public park inside its courtyard that all of the capital city’s residents could live there in times of siege. Other than this impressive castle, Bundimor is just a larger version of any other Hadlan town: wooden homes, shingled or thatched rooftops, livestock and humanoids traversing the same muddy streets and working the same damp fields.

POINTS OF INTEREST

The Great Chasm. Hadla’s entire southeast border overlooks the Great Chasm. See Bridgemoor for more information.

Kozia Jungle. The Kozia Jungle is home to two full nations along with part of two other nations, including part of Hadla. The Kozia Jungle is vast and supports huge biodiversity, offering plenty of lethal challenges to young adventurers.

QUEST IDEAS

The heroes might be young upstart adventurers looking to test their mettle in the jungle.

The heroes are tasked with guiding a merchant caravan to Nalathesh, Unjaarn, or even Tafunga or Valstrom.

The king of sixty years has died without leaving an heir. Various nobles throughout the nation vie for the throne.

HUSK

By all mundane metrics, Husk should not exist as a nation. Its desert landscape is predominantly rocks and pebbles and there are no noteworthy areas that could function as arable farmland. In fact, chunks of pebbly landscape have been known to spontaneously levitate for a time before showering down on whomever is so unlucky as to be standing beneath.

And this unexplainable phenomenon is what gives Husk its appeal. When prospective settlers traversed across this strange and hostile land, they were fascinated by the magical phenomena that they witnessed. Defying logic, they built a city-state in the most stable land of the region and began studying these strange events. The formative years of Husk were brutal and deadly, but Husk now thrives as a city-state that attracts scholars and adventurers the world over.

Academic. Husk boasts a large university dedicated specifically to the study of magic and how the different Sources of Magic interact with one another. It was at the Husk Institute that a scholarly Bhrunai named Zitvin discovered that certain Sources of Magic, when applied in equal force against one another, trump each other like a five-way game of Rock, Paper, Scissors: each Source is stronger than two others, and weaker than two others.

Aristocratic and Political. Although it was originally a small community of a few curious researchers, as generations progressed, the ruthless Licten family took over by force and has ruled Husk ever since. The Lictens purport that they are the chosen of the Lumen Cloud, one of the strange magical phenomena of the area.
