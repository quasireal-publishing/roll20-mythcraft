LORVAN

The capital city of Kess is nestled in the craggy foothills of the East Range. The streets of Lorvan are often shrouded in thick fog, which helps Palorva navigate the daylight without fear of retribution.

The capital has nothing in the way of entertainment. Aside from some lodgings, some inns and taverns with regulated prices, and a few shops that are equally regulated, Lorvan has nothing to offer except heartache.

POINTS OF INTEREST

The Great Chasm. Lorvan forces her serfs to constantly work the fields to produce food and mine the Chasm to produce resources that she can trade with other nations. For more information about the Chasm, see Bridgemoor.

QUEST IDEAS

Serfs from Kess get word out about their dire situation and request that the heroes help them overthrow Palorva.

Palorva hires the heroes to infiltrate Forsten or Constel and assess how she can most easily overthrow them.

LESMISHAN

Lesmishan is a wealthy nation sprawling along the lip of the Great Chasm. Having been founded a couple hundred years ago, it quickly became remarkably wealthy thanks to miners finding many veins of precious metals in the regions of the Great Chasm nearest their town. Lesmishan quickly grew from a single town to a nation of mining communities and trade outposts built precariously along the Great Chasms massive cliffs.

Aristocratic and Political. Lesmishans mighty banking guild - the Chasm Financing Co. - governs the nation. Each Lesmishanian town has a bank where representatives from the CFC choose a sheriff to keep order in the town and collect taxes for the CFC. The CFC will also loan out assets to people that they deem deserving. Both the bankers themselves and the citizens of any given town engage in wildly corrupt activity, but there are no written laws to enforce, so besides overt violence or resisting the CFCs taxation, anything goes.

Mercantile and Trade. Mining an abundance of gems, precious metals, and strange technologies, means that Lesmishan has vast trading capital. They trade widely with their neighboring nations and even distant nations in exchange for all manner of luxury goods and raw materials that they cannot naturally produce in their arid land. Lesmishan is especially known for a lightweight but rock-hard metal - called avadrium - that occasionally seems to glow in thin stripes and has been noted to enhance arcane and psionic power.

I need me a reliable merchant to hole up in one o them mines and send me some avadrium. Ill make you somethin legendary, you bet your last button I will.

-Ingrid

Militant. Lesmishan has a well-funded standing militia, and most of the CFC hire private guards as well. Most Lesmishanian miners are also capable of combat and are very skilled in the rocky, cavernous territory of their home nation. Furthermore, due to Lesmishans tremendous wealth, most aggressors can be bought off for a low price, as earning just a few silver coins is better than risking violent combat in most peoples books.

Religious. Many miners in particular are religious or superstitious in some mix. Untold monsters do lurk in the caverns of the Great Chasm, and most miners will claim that theyve seen unexplainable things. Many worship the Celestial Council or Avatars of protection and guidance. Others carry charms and trinkets to ward off evil spirits.

Social. Lesmishan has a pretty bleak society. The rich hoard their wealth while the poor explore dangerous caverns in hopes of earning a little coin only to have it exorbitantly taxed by the bankers. Miners will often find that their friendships with one another are their only real solace in an otherwise unforgiving nation.

Underworld. There are likely secretive crime syndicates working in Lesmishan, both operated by Lesmishanians and by foreign operatives such as Nalatheshs Sandsnakes. Most notably, of course, the bankers themselves operate much like a mafia.

LESMISHAN

The capital of Lesmishan, a city by the same name, is the largest mining town in the nation. As it is the oldest mining outpost, most of the veins of minerals in this particular region have since dried up except for those very deep in the caverns dotting the Chasms cliffs. While some mining still takes place there, most of the more shallow mineshafts have turned into black markets and criminal haunts. The city as a whole is wildly lawless and most miners spend far more than they earn in this city. The Chasm Financing Co.s headquarters is in this city: a large stone bastion with enough cured food and water to survive two years under siege.

POINTS OF INTEREST

The Great Chasm. See Bridgemoor for more information on the Great Chasm. Lesmishanians often descend into the Chasm or build small outpost towns on the Chasms cliffs to access their mineshafts more easily.

QUEST IDEAS

The CFC needs the heroes to clear out some monsters from a lucrative mineshaft.

Miners ask the heroes to help them clear a cave-in so that they can rescue their companions.

A banker hires the heroes to undermine, blackmail, or rob another banker.

An outside nation asks the heroes to scout out the capital city to see how best to overthrow it.

MUSKSHIR

Muskshir is a loose federation of several tribes that inhabit the stretch of jungle between the Great Chasm and the eastern sea. It has humid springs, monsoon summers that can reliably be predicted, dry falls, and mild, rainy winters.

Some of the tribes in Muskshir are nomadic, moving from region to region with the change of the seasons. Most tribes are more or less stationary. Regardless of their approach, most buildings of any tribe are easy to deconstruct and reassemble elsewhere, which helps the Muskshiri avoid the worst of monsoon flooding. Some tribes build their permanent buildings in the tree canopies.

Academic. Largely uninterested in the pursuits of other nations, Muskshir does not have schools in the sense of buildings with books and tests. Instead, they have a rigorous apprenticeship system, with youth training under hunters, bakers, weavers, and the like to become experts in their crafts from a young age.

Aristocratic and Political. Although each tribe is self-governing, the Muskshiri share a culture and have historically all united into one when they feel their land is under threat. In such times, the leaders of each tribe will negotiate with one another and cast votes on the actions that their nation will pursue.

Tribal leaders are generally hereditary, with the eldest child of the leader becoming the new tribal leader when their parent passes on. Some tribes prefer a democratic election or a contest of strength, such as a wrestling tournament, to determine their next leader.

Mercantile and Trade. Muskshir usually just trades goods and services between Muskshiri tribes rather than with other nations. They produce a wide variety of plant-based goods, including valuable tinctures and salves, so alchemists and physicians pay top coin for Muskshiri herbs when a tribe does decide to trade with foreign merchants.

Militant. Most Mukshiri folk spend their entire lives in the jungle. As such, Muskshiri warriors are extremely competent in jungle warfare but are not very strong outside of familiar territory.

As an artist of carnage myself, I respect how vicious a Muskshiri squad of scouts can be in combat.

-Hanniah

Religious. The tribes of Muskshir generally share the same outlook on religion, venerating the Archfae above any other gods. The particular Archfae will vary tribe by tribe, but the Letspina and Vertumna conclaves tend to be the most worshiped. The exception is Amazonia from the Pashak conclave, who is generally accepted as the patron goddess of all Muskshir.

Social. Muskshiri are usually welcoming of other tribes but suspicious of folk from other nations. At times, tribes will go to war with one another, but these conflicts are quickly resolved through negotiations between the tribal leaders and often are low-lethality.
