ROSHALLAN

A tremendous empire sprawling from Gathandia’s west coast to the Cloudpiercer Range, as far north as Chee and as far south as Nent, Roshallan is widely considered the most powerful nation on the continent and is unquestionably the largest. Roshallan’s natural environment changes dramatically given how large it is. It encompasses swathes of desert, two small mountain ranges, enormous stretches of plains, and dense forests. Roshallan is also the undisputed naval power in the lower Inner Sea. Roshallan’s navy has yet to be tested against the Renden of the upper Inner Sea.

Thus far, Roshallan has conquered Chee, Nent, Getar, and most of Thaaj, running each one as vassal states. Roshallani believe themselves to be destined to conquer the whole of Gathandia, bringing order and justice to a chaotic land. To learn more about Roshallan’s relationship with each of its vassal states, see their entries.

Roshallan is beautiful, oh yes. The pinnacle of humanoid accomplishment… and of humanoid greed.

-Chauncey

Academic. Roshallan has built some spectacular universities, primarily in its stretch of grassland along the southern coast of the Inner Sea.

These universities are some of the best-designed in Gathandia and attract Anedonian scholars and other prestigious thinkers despite Roshallan’s negative international reputation.

Aristocratic and Political. Roshallan’s emperor oversees the entire empire from the capital. The emperor delegates individual sectors of governance, such as finance or security, to their council, who work with regional monarchs to ensure that Roshallan’s interests are maintained. These rulers often employ a feudal system within their territory but are given the authority to structure the government of their own region however they deem best. Monarchs that show any signs of insubordination against the emperor are quickly taken care of. Roshallan’s first mighty emperor who began Roshallan’s era of conquest was Exandros. Its current emperor, Nerian, is still very competent if not yet as legendary as his predecessor. Emperor Nerian has every intention to decisively conquer Chehozran within the next five years.

Mercantile and Trade. Given its massive sprawl, Roshallan is a tremendous economic powerhouse. It facilitates trade within its vast empire by developing sturdy, durable roads that cut down on travel time. It also trades with Pash, Ahten, and Anedonia, each of whom have proven strong enough to resist Roshallan’s aggressions. Many international merchant vessels dock along Roshallan’s southwest coast.

Militant. Roshallan boasts a large and disciplined military. It has a naval presence in the Inner Sea thanks to the shipyards of Chee. On land, Roshallan’s military is divided into legions - groups of one thousand soldiers led by generals. Each general oversees a commander, who leads one hundred of the legion’s troops. Roshallan benefits from well-crafted weaponry and advanced tactical training.

Religious. The Roshallani worship the Celestial Council, believing that the Council has blessed them with prosperity due to their own righteousness. Roshallani believe that so long as they continue their divine mandate to unite Gathandia, fortune will continue to smile upon them.

Social. Roshallani citizens are fiercely proud of their nation. They are not very hospitable to foreign travelers, and are equally unfriendly towards the peoples of Roshallani vassal states. Annexed folk, such as Nentese, generally despise the Roshallani.

Underworld. Crime lords lurk in most large Roshallani cities, conducting their own business out of the sight of their ruling monarchs. Brigands and bandit gangs also make the countryside their hunting ground, preying on merchants and travelers.

TIBERIA

A shining city of grand proportions, Tiberia’s architecture reflects Roshallan’s splendor. It is built between the foothills of the Marble Mountains and Lake Tiberia. Many of its finest buildings are constructed of white marble mined from the mountains that sparkles gloriously in the sun. Tiberia offers much entertainment for its citizenry, and the Grand Coliseum draws crowds from all across the empire to watch magnificent battles. Tiberia’s temples are frequented by those who wish to make offerings to the Celestial Council, and the bathhouses are also a popular haunt of well-off citizens of the region. The emperor’s palace is nearly as large as the Grand Coliseum with scores of luxurious rooms for the emperor and their guests to enjoy.

POINTS OF INTEREST

Lava Fields. Roshallan’s eastern region is split by large lava fields that flow from the mountains down to the sea. While the land in the immediate vicinity is a charred ruin, the land just beyond is quite fertile and Roshallani farmers sow it to produce great quantities of food each year.

The Marble Mountains. A rocky, craggy mountain range rife with volcanic activity marks the border between Roshallan and Ahten. These mountains are full of marble, which the Roshallani mine as a great economic resource for themselves and for international trade.

ROVEL

The most prolific seafaring traders in Gathandia live in Rovel. A large forested peninsula west of Falth and prominent in the Inner Sea, Rovel enjoys warm summers and chilly winters but virtually no major swings to unbearably hot or cold. Seasonal thunderstorms occasionally bring tornadoes with them, though this is not a common occurrence.

Rovel’s docks - especially the docks of Hollyport - are the largest in Gathandia, capable of housing massive fleets of military or merchant vessels. Although they share the Inner Sea with the bloodthirsty Renden, they have proven both militarily strong enough to withstand raids and economically cunning enough to buy out the Renden.

Academic. Rovel places a good deal of emphasis on a robust education. Merchants that trade internationally bring in most of Rovel’s economic success, and students that learn the cultures of other nations, learn the technical aspects of trade and commerce, and understand the physics of sailing are - in Rovelese belief - the most likely to flourish. Children are expected to receive a basic education and encouraged to take specialized classes in sailing, cultural communications, international commerce, or banking.

Aristocratic and Political. Rovel has a monarchy, but at any given point a king or queen is really no more than a figurehead. The real political and legislative power in the nation is in the hands of the merchant guilds, and they use the monarch to give legitimacy to any laws that they want enacted.

Mercantile and Trade. The powerful merchant families in Rovel really run the nation. If they were to remove their support from the monarch, then the monarch’s entire reign would economically fall apart. With their massive, well-armed trade caravels, Rovel’s merchant fleet trade far and wide, extending as far as Yodvan to the northeast and Pash to the southwest. Many merchant vessels keep small chests of gold on board as insurance against Renden raids. Renden raiders know that the Rovelese can put up quite a fight on the water, so they will often accept small tolls in exchange for letting the Rovelese sail through their waters otherwise unmolested.
