LENTIRA

The shining capital city of Constel overlooks the eastern sea, about a day and a half north of Kendaria’s capital when the wind is in your sails. Lentira has a splendid university, several theatres, a sporting stadium, and famously large gardens where students and philosophers might relax or contemplate life. Its governmental buildings are constructed from hewn marble, and many of its other buildings are made of fine stone and polished wood.

POINTS OF INTEREST

Dreamhaze Mountains. The mountains that overlook the Old Forest are known as the Dreamhaze Mountains, so named because of the strange, dreamlike quality evoked by their misty slopes. It’s very easy to become lost in the Dreamhaze Mountains, even for skillful trackers, so most just avoid the mountains altogether. Those who do journey into the mountains often find inexplicable runes, come face to face with incarnations of their old memories, and must contend with strange monsters they have never heard of.

The Old Forest. The Old Forest sprawls across most of Kendaria and Forsten, and was once a major feature of Constel as well. While monster sightings are rare in these picturesque woods, travelers and locals alike are often left with a vague sense of unease after hiking therein. Children tell each other ghost stories that seem a little too real, somehow, and even skilled hunters have occasionally gone missing with no explanation. Although much of the Old Forest in Constel has been chopped down, these feelings of unease persist even when people spend time where the forest used to loom.

DERK

Derk is a rocky and hilly country blanketed in the west by a sprawling pine forest. With mild summers and frigid winters, Derk suffers the brunt of the arctic winds rolling down from the north. Its stony hills and cold forests make agricultural practice difficult, so Derk largely relies on pastoral farming inland and fishing along the coastline.

Academic. Derk has a few small schools along its coastal cities, but these are significantly lacking in most contemporary scientific practices and philosophical thought. Most Derkese that live inland do not receive a formal education.

Aristocratic and Political. Derk has a classical feudal model with a monarch whose eldest child takes their place when they die. Lords and ladies oversee regions, and individual towns and communities are run by mayors. Each mayor can levy a small militia from their town. The Derkese monarchy - currently ruled by Erwin Derk II - is nationally weak and is not very successful at enforcing cooperation from lords or mayors.

Mercantile and Trade. Derkese sailors trade regularly with Forsten, Constel, and Kendaria to the south as well as Yodvan to the north. Overland, Derk primarily trades with Forsten.

Militant. Derk’s military is strongest along the coast, with several dozen naval ships sailed by ferocious warriors. Their military is much weaker in the forested lands to the west, and towns in this region are often subject to raids from Bishal.

Religious. Derkese religious practice revolves around the Stravon Conclave of the Archfae. By worshiping the Conclave that embodies emotional states, the Derkese place an emphasis on being fully true to one’s own emotional reaction to an experience and what lessons one might learn from that.

Social. Towns in Derk’s inland tend to be down-to-earth, honest folk. They are hospitable but guarded around strangers, and if strangers prove to be trustworthy then they will be welcomed as one of the town’s own. Along the coast, Derkese society is a little more vibrant and one or more theatres for a wide variety of performing arts are common in each city.

Underworld. Small gangs work to ship illicit goods in and out of Derk’s ports, but there is no centralized criminal authority. Derk’s poorly organized town guards are helpless to stop these smugglers.

ICEPOINT AND WHITEBREAK

The capital of Derk is not actually its largest city. Icepoint is a stony fortress on the northern coast from which the reigning monarch decrees laws and settles disputes, so it is technically the capital. However, Derk’s largest city is Whitebreak.

Nestled in a cove along the southern side of the peninsula and so named because of the large waves that break into white foam against the cliffs around the bay, Whitebreak is home to a massive port. Whitebreak is responsible for over half of Derk’s whole economy. As such, the lord of Whitebreak often treats the monarch with an ironic deference in public and bullies the monarch in private.

DOSHAIRIEL

Doshairiel is quite a large nation, sprawling along the northeastern side of the Cloudpiercer Mountains. The nation is almost entirely covered in temperate forest, but is wide enough from north to south that different regions experience quite different summers. Southern Doshairiel, primarily deciduous, has muggy summers with frequent thunderstorms and mild winters with occasional rain and sleet. The north of the nation, more boreal than deciduous, has dry, mild summers and snowy winters.

Doshairiel is one of the oldest nations on Gathandia, having been founded by the star elves before the events of written history. As elves are long-lived and can communicate with one another in their dreams, Doshairiel has a complicated and highly functional governmental system. While star elves and forest elves are both highly populous in Doshairiel, the nation is home to many lineages from across the continent.

Academic. Virtually every settlement in Doshairiel has some kind of school. The larger cities, naturally, will have well-funded universities, as opposed to the single-room lecture halls that one might find in a small village. Education is mandatory for children, but optional for teens, who might choose to apprentice themselves to a master of a particular trade instead. Those who continue their formal education are more likely to become governors or oligarchs.

Aristocratic and Political. Doshairiel does not have an aristocracy. Instead, an oligarchy of fifteen elders oversees the happenings of the nation, delegating responsibilities to regional governors. These oligarchs each choose their heirs and choose when to step down from office, promoting their heirs to become the next oligarch in their stead.

Mercantile and Trade. Doshairiel produces a bounty of timber, foodstuffs, and animal byproducts. In addition to trading internally, Doshairieli merchants also trade with Betaal and whoever they can safely reach via the Inner Ocean. They also trade with Falth, though successful merchants that do so are careful not to get swindled or sucked into a blackmail scheme with one of the crime lords.

Militant. Doshairiel’s military is centralized and commanded by generals appointed by the oligarchy. Its military commanders study philosophy and magical practices as much as military theory, implementing both knowledge bases into their training regimens and combat techniques. Doshairieli soldiers, especially those stationed near the Inner Sea where raids from Renden are common, are quite competent.

Religious. Given their heritage as a nation founded by star elves, Doshairieli ascribe great meaning to the patterns of the stars. Most religious scholars in Doshairiel associate certain constellations with Avatars and beings from the Celestial Council. Many also pay homage to Ancerra’s five moons and will have religious services any time there is a full moon.

Social. Governors and town administrators are responsible for maintaining a delicate balance of free market and communist methodologies to keep the balance of wealth spread equally across their settlements. Doshairieli towns exact heavy taxes on their citizenry, keep a fraction of the profit for infrastructural advancement, and return the rest of the taxes to the town’s citizens in equal shares.
