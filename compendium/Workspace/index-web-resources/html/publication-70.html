Militant. Yodvan does not have a standing infantry, but has highly trained and well equipped town guards. Most town guards have “electric torches” fashioned from the glowing glass lights that people harvest from the forest. They are also equipped with adamantine shields and spears, making them quite tough to beat. Yodvan does maintain a reasonably powerful navy with vessels and sailors that are well-equipped for dealing with icy waters.

Religious. Yodvan observes many of the same religious practices that are held by their southern neighboring nation, Noord. Most folk worship both winter as a harsh but strengthening goddess, and Aurora - the northern lights - as a goddess of bounty, beauty, and good fortune. They also pay homage to the Fjordenhavian patron god of fire, Aeshir, but he is not as prominent in Yodvanian religion as Aurora or Strack, the winter.

Social. Yodvan’s culture is a blend of Fjordenhaven’s and Noord’s. Having generally adopted the religious beliefs of Noord, Yodvan has the less militant attitude shared by the Fjordenhavians. The folk of Yodvan are generally warm and hospitable to strangers, but punish crime very severely.

Underworld. A thriving crime syndicate constantly smuggles new inventions from Yodvan out to the rest of the criminal world throughout Gathandia. This is dangerous work: if caught, thieves are dumped in frigid water and left to the fish. Even so, the lucrative profit is enough to lure many into this line of work.

AURORA’S HEARTH

Aurora’s Hearth is the first outpost founded by Fjordenhaven hundreds of years ago. It has since developed into a sprawling city that hugs the coastline for dozens of miles. The old town, where Fjordenhavian sailors first made landfall, is where the monarch’s palace stands tall and proud against the cold. The Yodvanian Academy of Sciences is a few miles south of the old town.

Aurora’s Hearth is clean, well-maintained, and well-lit at night, thanks to the glowing glass lights harvested from the forest. The city watchguards do an excellent job of mitigating violent crime and home burglary, but struggle to tamp down on controlled goods being smuggled out of the city’s numerous ports.

POINTS OF INTEREST

The Glowing Forest. The glowing forest was created from ancient Avadri technology, though this has not yet been discovered by Yodvanian researchers. Its vegetation grows in strangely intentional patterns: trees might have limbs at precise angles, and a bush’s leaves might all be exactly the same size. The vegetation seems to be made of an alloy of both plant and metal materials, and scientists are still experimenting with the best uses for this material. Glass bulbs grow from many trees and bushes in a variety of shapes, and these bulbs will often glow with internal light. Scientists have discovered how to control when these lights turn on and off, and the bulb’s light will last for several years after being plucked.

Other discoveries no doubt lie deep in the Glowing Forest, but strange monsters of untold power lurk in the forest’s depths as well. Determined but cautious, the Yodvanians have made slow progress in uncovering the secrets within the forest’s depths.

The Mountains of Adamant. Shrouded in thick fog and palpable mystery, the Mountains of Adamant loom cold and foreboding along the center of the large island. The promise of great wealth in the form of adamantine lures many miners and adventurers, but both the terrain and the fauna are vicious in these mountains, not to mention the winds that could rip flesh from bone. Jagged cliffs, treacherous glaciers, and narrow passes each pose threats to explorers. Terrifying creatures that lurk deep in the mountain’s caves often leave even stoic heroes babbling with inarticulate fright.

QUEST IDEAS

Yodvan holds many valuable materials, notably adamantine and glowing glass. The heroes might be recruited by Yodvanian scientists or by patrons from another nation to travel deeper into the Glowing Forest or the Mountains of Adamant to find more of these materials or to make new discoveries.

An incredibly brutal winter has completely buried a town in snow. The heroes must oversee efforts to rescue the town, but starving polar bears and hungry abominations from the Glowing Forest have other ideas for the town’s trapped citizens.

The heroes are recruited by a network of magic item smugglers working for Husk or Vel. They want the heroes’ help in procuring a new item that scientists have created with the plant-metal of the Glowing Forest.

PLANES OF EXISTENCE

Gathandia and Ancerra are not the only places that daring heroes might explore. Adventurers, especially those of higher character level, might journey into the planes of existence beyond the mortal coil. Ancerra and other worlds on the same plane of existence are believed to be on the “Prime Material” plane, according to planar scholars.

AVADRIA

Once a plane of boundless light and radiant creations, the Eon War between the Paragon and Corrupted Avadri destroyed Avadria’s former splendor. It is now a silent, desolate plane filled with tendrils of the Pale and debris of millions of dead worlds. Avadria consists of entire galaxies that have gone dark. Cold, lightless stars still form the centers of silent solar systems and lifeless mechanical planets orbit around them.

There is no gravity here, and the space is breathable even when between planets. There is no light whatsoever, and the shadows seem to actively eat away at any light source. Terrors larger than planets stalk the umbral plains, surviving off of the refuse of an obliterated civilization. Maddened fae linger here, as well as denizens of the Pale.

Many scholars believe that the enormous machines that orbit dormant in these galaxies will one day awaken again and either set their galaxies back in order or break out of Avadria, bringing the plane’s desolation to the whole multiverse.

POINTS OF INTEREST

Alshahath. The ruined shell of Alshahath, homeworld of the Avadri, still drifts through empty space as its star detonated eons ago. Its surface a mess of molten metal and frigid wastelands, the technology possibly contained within the deepest recesses of the planet holds the power to ascend any mortal to godhood or beyond.

The Star-Forges. The various star-forges and world-forges that survived the Eon War still retain the ability to create and disassemble entire solar systems. Anyone capable of harnessing these massive machines in their full power can create their own demi-planar planet.

Thyre. Thyre, last fortress of the scattered Avadri resistance, lies at the intersection between Avadria and the long-forgotten Fundamental Plane. The entire planet has been converted to a colossal warcamp, radiant structures forming a majority of its observable landscape. The air and sky are near absolutely pure. The Avadri guardians who are still alive dwell within this bastion.

THE CELESTIAL SEA

The Celestial Sea is a vast planar ocean that connects the Prime Existence, Avadria, Everwilds, Inferno, and Iztari. It allows safe travel between planes without having to travel through the dangerous Pale, which envelops the Celestial Sea on all sides beyond the planes.

The Celestial Sea always teems with trillions of stars, but these are not physical stars that produce heat and energy. Instead, stars on the Celestial Sea are bits of Essence that form together to create souls.

SOULS AND ESSENCE

A soul is the Essence cultivated within a single living creature. One soul cannot live multiple times, because when a creature dies and their Essence returns to the Celestial Sea, their Essence doesn’t remain stuck together. This makes resurrection impossible after a number of years, depending on how long it takes for a Soul’s Essence to dissolve back into the Celestial Sea.

This is also why some Resurrection results in a person having their personality changed, some of their memory being lost, or some other combination of unique mental/personality challenges: a person’s Soul might have begun to dissolve, and when they are forcibly resurrected back into a body then the process might bring back their incomplete Essence or might bring other ambient Essence (perhaps Essence that was once part of a different soul and still has different memories and personality associated with it) back into the same body.
