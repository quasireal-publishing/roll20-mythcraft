APPENDIX C: | COMBAT REFERENCE

Charmed

You look upon the creature that Charmed you as a friendly acquaintance. You will not intentionally harm the creature that Charmed you. When that creature targets your ANT, LOG, or WILL, they get TA. When that creature makes an INT or CHA check to interact with you, they roll 2d20 and use the higher result. Unless stated otherwise, you know you were Charmed after this condition fades.

Chilled

Moving and making weapon attacks cost 1 more AP than they would normally. A creature that uses creature actions can take one fewer actions than it normally would (min 1).

Concealed

You are Concealed when you are not completely hidden, but Line of Sight is interrupted by fog, smoke, foliage, or other obscuring phenomena. Creatures are aware of exactly where you are but cannot see you clearly. While Concealed, you have +1 TA on attacks provided that you can see your enemy clearly, and attacks against you suffer 1 TD.

Cover

If at least Â½ of your body is protected by an object that would block physical damage, then you have Partial Cover. Your AR and physical defenses gain between +1 and +3 against ranged attacks, determined by your MC and the amount of cover you have.

When you have Total Cover, you cannot be targeted by ranged attacks or any effect that requires Line of Sight to you.

Dazed

Your current AP is reduced to 3, unless it was already 3 or lower. You gain no more than 3 AP at the start of your turns while Dazed and cannot use Reactive AP or carry over AP from previous rounds. A creature that uses creature actions can take no more than two basic monster actions on its turn and cannot take reactions.

Deafened

You cannot hear and are immune to effects that require hearing. You automatically fail any roll that requires you to be able to hear. Casting magic that requires verbal input requires you roll 2d20 and take the lower result.

Demoralized

All attacks against a Demoralized creature gain +1 TA.

Engaged

A target that is Engaged provokes a 0 APC Reactive Attack when it leaves the reach of the creature that has Engaged it. If the attack hits, the creature takes damage as normal and its speed is reduced to 0 until the start of its next turn.

Enthralled

You are Charmed and lose your sense of self, following the instructions from the creature that Enthralled you even to the point of putting yourself in danger. You will not intentionally harm the creature that Enthralled you, and will not undertake actions that will obviously lead to your own death. For example, you may attack your ally, but would not fall on your own sword. When a creature that Enthralled you attacks your ANT, LOG, or WILL, or makes a check against you using INT or CHA, they roll 2d20 and use the higher roll.

Fatigued

Whenever you suffer Fatigue, immediately gain +1 Death Point and become Fatigued. While Fatigued, you do not remove your Death Points when you Recoup or Take a Rest. Instead, when you Take a Rest, you remove the Fatigued condition if you eat and drink the minimum amount needed to sustain you.

Flanking

Gain +1 TA when making a melee attack against a creature if one of your allies occupies a space adjacent to the creature the space they occupy is on the horizontally or diagonally opposite side of the creature from you.

Focused

You gain +1 TA against a specific target. When you gain this condition, it will specify which target you gain TA against.

Frightened

You suffer 1 TD while the source of fear is in sight, and cannot willingly move closer to the source of your fear.

Grappled

While Grappled, you cannot use two-handed weapons and your speed is impacted by the other Grappled creature.

If two Grappled creatures are the same Size, the one that initiated the Grapple is considered larger for purposes of determining size when Grappled.

If the larger Grappled creature is no more than two Sizes larger than the other creature, then the larger creatureâs speed is halved; the smaller creatureâs speed is reduced to 0 ft.

If the larger Grappled creature is three or more sizes larger than the other creature, then the larger creatureâs speed is unaffected; the smaller creatureâs speed is reduced to 0 ft.

Forced movement does not end the Grappled condition.

If the larger creature is subjected to forced movement, the smaller creature moves with it.

If the smaller creature is subjected to forced movement and the larger creature is not, the larger creature decides if the smaller creature moves or not.

When a creature moves while Grappled, the other Grappled creature moves with it.

A Grappled creature that did not initiate the Grapple can try to break out of a Grapple once on each of its turns. It does so by spending 2 AP (or using one action, if it is a monster) to make a STR or DEX attack roll against the target creatureâs REF or FORT (their choice). On a success, the creature frees itself.

If you use magic to Grapple another creature, your speed is unaffected. The creature may break the Grapple normally, making a STR or DEX check against a DC of your magic attribute + 10. You are not considered Grappled in this case except when determining the target creatureâs movement.

Grounded

You cannot teleport or move to a different plane, nor can an external effect impose teleportation or plane shifting effects on you.

Helpless

When you take melee damage, you lose all of your HP.

Paralyzed

You are Helpless and your AP becomes 0. You gain 0 AP at the start of each of your turns while Paralyzed. A creature with creature actions cannot take any creature actions or reactions while Paralyzed.

Phobic

You are Frightened when you can see the subject of your phobia. This effect is permanent until removed.

Pinned

You are Prone, Grappled, and gain suffer 1 TD on attacks. Creatures making melee attacks against you gain +1 TA, and creatures making ranged attacks against you suffer 1 TD.

Prone

You suffer 1 TD on all attacks while Prone, and cannot make attacks with two-handed weapons. Additionally, melee attacks against you have +1 TA, and ranged attacks against you suffer 1 TD.

Protected

You gain +2 to your AR and physical defenses while Protected.

Rallied

You cannot be Demoralized, Frightened, or Shaken. If you are already suffering from one of these conditions, the effects are suppressed while you are Rallied.

Restrained

A creature is Grappled. When attacking a Restrained creature, roll 2d20 and use the higher roll. Unless otherwise stated, escaping the Restrained condition is the same as escaping the Grappled condition, but requires 3 AP (Or two of an NPCâs actions).

Shaken

You suffer a -2 penalty to all d20 rolls.

Sickened

Whenever rolling a d20, roll 2d20 and take the lower result.

Silenced

You cannot vocalize. You automatically fail any roll that requires the ability to speak or use verbal components.

Slowed

Your speed is halved.

Staggered

You gain half the AP than you normally would at the start of each of your turns. A creature that uses creature actions can take only Â½ the creature actions that it normally would.

Stunned

Your current AP is reduced to 1, unless it was already 1 or lower. You gain only 1 AP at the start of your turns and your AP cannot be raised above 1 while Stunned. If a creature uses creature actions, the number of actions they have is reduced to 1.

Suffocating

After Suffocating for a number of rounds equal to your END + 1, you gain a Death Point at the start of each of your turns.

Suppressed

You cannot use your AP to move. A creature that uses creature actions cannot use them to move.
