When a new Licten is ready to assume the throne of Husk, they journey into the desert to be claimed by the Cloud. Thus far, every Licten who has done so has returned with a mark as though struck by lightning, cementing their belief that they are divinely chosen.

Mercantile and Trade. Husk imports a vast majority of its foodstuffs, water, textiles, and basic raw materials. Capable manufacturers turn these raw materials into finished products, often infusing these items with minor magical properties. Husk was the first nation to commodify magical items and still dominates the international magic item trade. In addition to magic items, Huskan merchants sell bottled ambient magic with unpredictable effects. Careless travelers will be scammed and sold empty glass vials.

Militant. Husk has some of the fiercest infantry fighters in all Gathandia, but the Huskan army is so small that this does not actually mean much. Trained to fight with unique magical weapons, each elite warrior crafts their own magic weapon at the end of their training. While their army is small, it is nonetheless strong enough to dissuade any other nation from invading Husk.

Religious. The Lumen Cloud is the god of the region, and the Licten family is its chosen emissary to sapient creatures. The Lumen Cloud is a great glowing cumulus cloud that appears at random times over the region. When it is visible, normal daily affairs halt and the citizens of Husk praise the Cloud and pray that it bestows its everlasting power upon their nation and their beloved Licten family. The Cloud is especially spectacular to behold at sunset when it catches rays from the setting sun and magnifies its own glowing properties, or at nighttime when it refracts the silver light of the stars.

Social. Because they live in a region that experiences frequent chaotic magical occurrences, the folk of Husk tend to be both cautious and unflappable. Having seen many strange things, they accept each new ripple of magic with almost disinterested stoicism.

Underworld. The Licten family has failed to crush multiple criminal organizations that smuggle magic items and bottled ambient magic out of the region to sell at black market prices to other adventurers and merchants.

The Lictens are as dangerous as they are powerful, and they rival many of the strongest Falthic crime lords in the reach of their influence and their utter absence of morals.

-Avery

POINTS OF INTEREST

The entire region of Husk is a point of power where arcane, primal, and psionic magic swirl together with unpredictable results. Sometimes this causes rocks and pebbles to levitate and rain from the sky; other times it causes the Lumen Cloud to appear. Records show that it has snowed in Husk despite there not being a cloud in the sky, and occasionally portals to other planes will briefly open.

KANDIfF

The steppes of Kandiff, although quite dry, are nonetheless temperate and a reasonably pleasant environment in which to live. The summers are warm and arid, the winters chilly and often snowy. Kandiff is a land of mediocrity: it is not so mountainous as Betaal to the west, not so fertile as Bishal to the east, and not so magical or wealthy as Husk or Lesmishan to the south. It is, ironically, this very mediocrity that allows Kandiff to thrive: if its ground were as fertile or its mines as valuable as its neighbors, it might have been conquered long ago.

Academic. Most Kandiffese value education but do not have the means to pursue it in any serious way. Local schools will teach children the basics of reading and writing, but few pursue a scholarly career.

APAKKU

The only truly remarkable aspect of Kandiff is its capital city. Apakku is an ancient alligator snapping turtle who has grown to be the size of a small mountain. Having grown up in the dusty streams of northern Kandiff, the turtle observed the humanoids in her region and grew to love their homey attitudes. As she grew to be the size of a horse, then of a house, her fame spread throughout Kandiff. She traveled from province to province, contentedly observing children at play and ranchers and miners hard at work.

Finally, when Apakku grew old enough to be the size of a large hill, she telepathically commanded the Kandiffese to rebuild their oligarchic capital on her shell. For a hundred years now, she has continued to grow, and Kandiffâ€™s capital city has grown with Apakku as her shell accommodates more and more buildings. She travels across Kandiff from north to south and from east to west three times annually, bringing the Oligarchy with her to ensure that all in their nation are well.
