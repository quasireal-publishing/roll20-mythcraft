INTERNATIONAL GUILDS

In addition to the many nations of Gathandia, international guilds are also major players on the continent. Note that this section deals specifically with guilds that hold an international presence and are not explicitly associated with a nation. Falthic crime syndicates, for example, do not have a dedicated entry here as they are closely tied to the nation of Falth.

CRIME SYNDICATES

ARDENTS

Based in Roshallan, the Ardents are a covert order of zealots who believe that Roshallan’s aggression and annexation of other nations is unforgivable. The Ardents view themselves as the tools of the divine, as just punishment for Roshallan’s actions. Ardents believe that they are morally superior to crime syndicates - the Ardents act out of a strong desire for justice, while criminal entities act out of a drive for profit. Even so, Ardents will begrudgingly make alliances with any network, criminal or otherwise, that might help weaken Roshallan in some way. The Ardents also implement assassination and skullduggery to weaken Roshallan’s imperial grip on its provinces.

HARLAN’S GUILD

Harlan’s Guild has many interests and is known best as a magic items smuggling operation. By thieving from caravans and robbing stores known to keep magic items in stock, Harlan’s Guild then resells these items on the black market. When other syndicates in their territory try to get in on magic item fencing, Harlan’s Guild takes very unkindly to this and harrasse the up and coming syndicate until they back off of the market.


The Guild is well known for crafting unique poisons with which they coat their weapons. They use these poisons to gain an edge when they must fight, and they are not afraid of trading blows when needed. Robbing stores or caravans that traffic magic items is usually a dangerous and bloody ordeal, so these poisons are integral to giving them an upper hand in their heists.


Harlan’s Guild is a widespread network sprawling across most of Gathandia. Its headquarters is in Falth, where Harlan himself governs Breachvale, a large city on Nalathesh’s border.


Guild Poisons


Members of Harlan’s Guild readily have access to these three poisons. Harlan’s Guild can also access many more poisons; these are simply their specialties and are always available to guild members.


Drake’s Breath: After application, your attacks with that weapon have a 50% chance to deal +1d6 fire damage for 1 minute. The extra damage occurs on odd-numbered attack rolls.


Ice Blood: The first target struck with a weapon coated in this poison must make an END check with a DC of 10 at the start of their turns for the next 1 minute. On any turn where they fail, they gain the Chilled condition.


Harlan’s Breach: After application, your attacks with that weapon ignore all Resist properties, as well as Immune Blunt, Immune Sharp, and Immune Physical, for 1 minute.


ICEGRAVE

Icegrave is a relatively small but effective crime syndicate that operates in the far north. Its headquarters is in a small, fortified town tucked away in a treacherous mountain pass in northern Noord. Icegrave also has major operating cells throughout Yodvan and even has a small presence as far south as Nalathesh, where Icegrave’s operatives sell their smuggled goods to Falthic crime guilds, the Scorpions, and other black market buyers.


Icegrave primarily concerns itself with the theft and black market selling of the strange materials taken from the Glowing Forest. Chiefly a syndicate of smugglers, Icegrave will nonetheless take on other jobs when the opportunity and potential rewards are great enough.

MUL’ATOR

Originally based in Betaal, the Mul’Ator have since been exiled for their bloodthirsty reputation. They were a faction of the Shinus that still dwell in Betaal, but have since become their own organization. Nobody knows where they are headquartered now - nobody except for Felson Weskh, who has bought their loyalty and who pays for them to maintain a headquarters in Gavare even as they report to him directly.


The Mul’Ator have a reputation for being unparalleled assassins who seem to revel in dismembering their victims and leaving their corpses as gruesome monuments to death. They operate all across Gathandia, and when a wealthy and influential person wants to hire a brutal assassin, they need turn no farther than the Mul’Ator.

SCORPIONS

Masquerading as a simple merchant caravan, the Scorpions are a dangerous and unpredictable crime guild. They operate primarily in Nalathesh, Kandif, and Lesmishan, but are known to travel as far east as Kendaria and as far south as the Thunderhead Vale.


The Scorpions could almost be considered a mercenary group, as they will take jobs for high-paying patrons but will also rob and bully other merchant caravans. They primarily facilitate international black market trade by transporting illicit goods across their vast territory. This is usually a lucrative business, but they will turn to their more violent acts - robbing other caravans and even raiding towns - when the black market is in a dry season.

UNDERMINERS

Based in Bridgemoor but operating along the entire east coast of Gathandia - and even as far west as Nalathesh - the Underminers are a crime syndicate with strong cult elements. Many members of the Hierophants are also involved with the Underminers due to the Underminers’ quasi-religious fascination with the Avadri. The Underminers believe that gaining a better understanding of the Avadri will allow them to reshape the world, much as the Avadri shaped the world ages ago. The Underminers primarily consist of thieves and smugglers that amass Avadrium and other magical objects, and of scientists who experiment with these materials to discover new uses of them. When possible, the Underminers also destabilize local governments or place their own members in seats of power so that they can prepare the way for their idea of a greater future.

THE WEB

An expansive crime syndicate that prominently stretches across the western half of Gathandia and even has a few operatives east of the Cloudpiercers, this secretive syndicate is known simply as the Web. Its members are some of the most unobtrusive, trustworthy-looking folk in any given settlement. Grandmotherly herbalists, kindly general store owners, and bumbling town guards all might be members of the Web. Members can identify one another by their decorative doilies, which they keep hidden in plain sight in their stores, homes, or with their belongings. The Web’s mission is to destroy the Illuminati by exposing and discrediting its members, and they are willing to employ any means necessary according to the skill set of their local members.


The Web believes that the Illuminati suppresses the freedoms and privacy of the people throughout Gathandia, and seek to dispel what they view as a corrupt and tyrannical shadow over the land.

MAGICAL GUILDS

THE CABINET

The Cabinet is a secret council of twelve masters of psionic magic. They oversee instructors, who in turn train hundreds of psionic latents. The Cabinet’s instructors often also serve as neutral advisors to many nations and are dispersed across Ancerra, serving as translators, diplomats, and long-distance telepathic communicators. A representative from the Cabinet serves as a symbol of status and lends legitimacy to a nation’s leader.


When one of the Cabinet’s twelve masters knows that they will soon pass on, they choose a protege to take their place. This is generally a well-respected member of the Cabinet with a strong record as an instructor or advisor. When one of the Cabinet’s twelve masters finally dies, their physical body deteriorates, but their Essence lives on in a shared Mind Palace accessible by all of the twelve.

THE CULTIVATORS

Cultivators typically practice divine magic, and if they do not then they still ascribe to the higher principles of their gods - especially Tovare, the head matron of the Celestial Council. They take vows of poverty, humility, and chastity and commonly travel throughout Ancerra to the poorest communities to help them develop. Some Cultivators travel to many communities over the course of their lives, never settling down, and others (typically the wiser ones) focus on one or two key communities and spend their whole lives and livelihoods helping those specific communities. Cultivators that settle in specific communities often become cherished members of society, while those that travel around more can be seen as out of touch.

THE FIXERS

The Fixers is an institution of psionic casters that boasts thousands of members throughout Ancerra. They primarily view themselves as the solutions to direct problems like collapsed bridges or roving monsters. They travel their regions to collect bounties, defeat monsters that are troubling settlements, and telekinetically repair infrastructure.

HAVEN CONCLAVE

The Haven Conclave are guides and sentinels skillful in the natural world. Many members of the Haven Conclave are rangers, oracles, and witches, and most can use at least a little primal magic.


Haven cloisters offer shelter and solace from the storm. Haven sentinels stand on the edges of the more depraved elements of wilderness, defending innocent settlements and beasts alike from unnatural threats. Guides often offer their services to merchants or adventuring parties struggling to navigate a fearsome land.

THE HIEROPHANTS

The Hierophants are an esoteric divine organization. Prophets of the avadri, the Hierophants believe that they can learn to divine the future by deciphering the coding written into the universe. The organization is small and reclusive, but widespread. If one knows where to look and whom to ask after, a clever investigator can usually find a Hierophant alcove.

ILLUMINATI

An order of arcane casters who consider themselves more knowledgeable and more discerning than any other order, the Illuminati master arcane divining, enchanting, and illusory magic. Members of the Illuminati keep their affiliation secret and communicate with one another covertly. Members of the Illuminati range from prominent scholars to minor nobles to monarchs of entire kingdoms. They use their collective wealth, knowledge, and influence to steer the geopolitics of Gathandia as they see fit. While they strive to keep the whole of Gathandia safe and peaceful, their means are often seen as invasive. The largest concentration of Illuminati dwell in Valstrom, and Valstrom’s monarch is one of the preeminent members of the organization.

LIGHTS FROM BELOW

Colloquially known as Lebellos or Elbes, the Lights from Below is a small wing of psions dedicated to improving Ancerra. The Lights from Below use their telekinetic powers to construct bridges and dig wells. Those with psionic powers of persuasion will dissuade bandits and monsters from their violent actions. Members of the Lights from Below do not seek wealth or fame from the outside world, taking internal satisfaction in their work. While traveling, full members will nurture young latents. Their headquarters, the Hall of Heroes, is a massive hall carved into a mountain face in the Cloudpiercer Range. It is filled with statues and frieze sculptures that depict heroes from the organization and their famous adventures.

PATH OF ASCENDANCY

The Path of Ascendancy is an infamous school of the occult. Ascendants profess that anyone can become a god in their own right if they try hard enough and are willing to sacrifice enough; this sacrifice comes with immense physical and mental toll. For new initiates in the Path to advance in rank, they must bring in additional new initiates.


Ascendants cast their magic by drawing intricate, beautifully complex geometric shapes. Many Ascendants get these shapes tattooed on themselves as signs of rank, belonging, and power.


The Great Ascendant is the leader of the Path. Although once human, he has undergone so many permanent transformations that he now looks like an archfiend. He has goat legs, huge claws, cat eyes, ram horns, a snake’s tongue and fangs, and enormous bat wings. Every inch of once-human flesh has been burned and branded so many times that it’s as tough as leather and as red as raw meat. His skin is also covered in geometric tattoos that are never quite symmetrical.

REWEAVERS

A widespread and decentralized organization of arcane casters specializing in alchemy and altering, members of the Reweavers are a welcome sight in any struggling town. Capable of quickly repairing and rebuilding basic objects and even whole buildings, Reweavers believe that their magical powers should be freely shared with those in need. Reweavers are generally content to receive a modest income and free room and board for their services, caring not for great wealth. While the Reweavers do not have an official home and can be found in most nations, they are most active in Anedonia and Doshairiel. They used to operate widely in the region that is now Roshallan, but as the empire took over many of the Reweavers looked elsewhere. Roshallan’s effective infrastructure and its tyrannical rule gave Reweavers both little reason to stay, and great reason to leave.

SAVANTS OF THE PALE

By focusing on the mystical mists of the Pale, members of this cryptic institution learn how to hide and obscure their tracks. They believe that this is of fundamental importance, because once they become experts at hiding their magical presence, they can steal more powerful abilities from gods and planar beings and escape undetected, free to use their newfound powers however they please. Casters from this group are notable for wearing deep, hooded cloaks and for casting occult magic by singing strange, dissonant choral melodies.

SOOTHSAYERS

A monastic collection of hermits, most of whom have oracular abilities, dwell in the Cloudpiercer Range in and around Betaal. As their numbers have grown, some have migrated and started new monastic communities to the south and west. These folk, known as the Soothsayers, have untold powers of divining. They typically keep to themselves, living within their own monasteries and only traveling into the greater world when they feel that a great prophecy must be shared.


Some Soothsayers share prophecies with specific kingdoms or even specific people, while others prophesy to a broader audience.


The founder of the Soothsayers, Elindira Stavrelyden, began manifesting psionic divining abilities even before magic was widespread. She predicted the Tear exactly one year before it happened, down to the very minute.

STORM CONCLAVE

Members of the Storm Conclave are vengeful naturalists trying to burn down society and return everyone to hunter-gatherer lifestyles, as this is the way that they believe nature intended for humanity to live. They especially target international trading groups such as the WGC, or popular routes utilized by intercity merchants such as the Golden Road, trying to destabilize the veins of modern living and return the world to its days of so-called glory.

SURROGATES

A small and ambitious group of arcanists, the Surrogates specialize in summoning magic, taking their name from the fact that they conjure forth other creatures to act in their place. Primarily operating in Doshairiel and Nalathesh, the Surrogates strongly oppose the Unsoiled. The Surrogates profess that reanimating a corpse to do manual labor or any other task is disrespectful to the memory of the person, and that summoning other creatures is not only more humane, but also more effective as one can summon a creature suited to the task at hand. The Unsoiled, in turn, argue that any living creature should be free to do as it wishes while the dead see to the grunt work of society. The two groups often get into verbal - and sometimes physical - bouts with one another.

UNSOILED

Some arcanists hold that the greatest stain on humanoid achievement is death. None have been able to outsmart it or outrun it. Some powerful psions live on as ghostly presences in their mind palaces, and some druids - especially elven druids - might live for a thousand or more years before they die, but even they cannot guarantee that their bodies will never fade.


The Unsoiled find this an abomination. With a contemporary understanding of magic, surely a person ought to live forever if they wish to. The Unsoiled are expert necromancers who generally use their magical abilities to help their communities. They will raid graveyards and animate skeletons to perform manual labor around a town, freeing up other folk to relax or pursue other activities.


This has been well-received in some communities, but others find the presence of the Unsoiled unnatural and repulsive. Many of the Unsoiled also aggressively pursue experimentation on humanoid corpses, trying to discern a way to extend a body’s livelihood indefinitely.


A majority of Unsoiled are well intentioned and are harmless to the public, if a bit creepy. Some, however, become transfixed by their fascination with death, and this obsession consumes them. Most famous among these is Mirva the Carver. Mirva was a Gavarese halfling with a relatively normal life. She practiced some arcane magic, but was more concerned with tending her family, including her two sons and her daughter. When a monster razed her town and tragically slaughtered her family, she turned to necromancy to revive her husband and her three children. Unfortunately, they were only shells of their former selves, so Mirva continues to seek enough power and understanding to restore their personalities. She wanders the southern Cloudpiercer Range, murdering anyone who loses their way and raising more and more corpses into her service.

VIGIL

A large, decentralized denomination that worships all Celestials and Avatars - especially those that have a militant attitude against evil and corruption, members of Vigil or “Vigilants” have sworn to protect the innocent and weak from threats that lie beyond. Some branches of Vigil are welcome in settlements that fear attacks from monsters. While all branches of Vigil are founded on good intentions, there are totalitarian branches of Vigil that do more harm than good.

W.A.N.D.

W.A.N.D. (Worldwide Arcanists who Neutralize Danger) is a large and widespread organization of mages and arcanists. Their headquarters is officially in Constel on the east coast, but W.A.N.D. has regional strongholds across all of Gathandia and, according to their leaders, maintain a presence on Ancerra’s other continents as well. W.A.N.D.’s arcanists view themselves as magic-wielding soldiers, spies, and diplomats whose primary goal is the protection of Ancerra from extraplanar threats. Occasionally, if a tyrannical regime grows strong enough to threaten the world order, then W.A.N.D. might intervene, but they generally reserve their concerns for otherworldly threats. W.A.N.D. arcanists specialize in evoking and warding magic, but many arcanists choose a third function to focus on as well.

MERCENARY ORGANIZATIONS

BLACK SOIL

A brutal mercenary band that operates in the northwest, Black Soil can typically be found in Skavr, Eryndor, and Ruptaea, though they occasionally operate in Fjordenhaven as well. This mercenary band is utterly unscrupulous, enjoying the more violent jobs with unsettling glee.


They burn, loot, and pillage towns with reckless abandon.


Black Soil also has strong cult elements and has been known to perform horrifying chants and dances in homage to the dark spirits that live in Nachtskog. Indeed, some folk whisper that the evil of Nachtskog inhabits the mercenaries of Black Soil, giving them additional strength and menace.

THE GENTLEMAN BRIGANDS

In the troubled lands of the Thunderhead Vale and the Kosia Rainforest, corruption runs rampant in most governmental institutions from the top down to local administrations. While many common folk are able to scrape together a modestly comfortable life despite this, there are many others who suffer under this inequality. The Gentleman Brigands seek to help those who cannot fend for themselves under such circumstances. They rob from the wealthy, keeping a cut to fund their own crusade and giving the rest of their spoils to those who they believe need it the most. They travel throughout the Rainforest and the Vale, willing to sell their services to the rare honest noble in exchange for better wages for the noble’s subjects. The Brigands are something of a legend throughout this region, celebrated by the poor among the society and reviled by many of the rich - especially the corrupt. The Gentleman Brigands are always led by a person known as the Hood, who passes their title on to their successor when they believe it time to lay down their own blade. The current Hood is a kleppin with the features of a red-tail fox, and his best friend and bodyguard is a grizzled Urkou named Big Butch.

RESERVES

An order of hermetic zealots, the Reserves can be found in small monasteries throughout Gathandia. They are self-sufficient communities that maintain their own sustenance farms and spin their own clothes from sheep’s wool. Besides sustaining themselves, the Reserves train rigorously for the world’s hour of need. They believe that one day, ancient threats or new foes will arise and threaten the planet’s very existence, and on this day, the Reserves will stand ready to protect existence as they know it.

THE IRON RIDERS

Equestrians skilled at mounted combat and horseback archery, the Iron Riders roam the north and west of the Great Chasm. Although it is uncommon, they can be seen as far south as Nalathesh and as far east as Derk. They primarily operate in Bishal, southern Noord, Kandiff, and Lesmishan. The Iron Riders are highly competent in desert environments and have learned how to ride their steeds such that they produce little dust, allowing them to sneak up on unsuspecting merchants. Following the basic practices of most mercenaries, the Iron Riders will leave alone those who pay their shakedown prices and will fight on behalf of those who reward them more significantly.

THE SEA SERPENTS

The Sea Serpents are an armada of pirates who prowl the eastern coast of Gathandia, from Vel in the south to Derk in the north. The Sea Serpents nominally agree that other vessels under the same flag are off-limits for piracy, but in practice, as long as there is no evidence remaining, then anything goes. Bloody ocean battles between the Sea Serpents will leave one ship sinking and in flames while the other makes off with its loot. When they feel that they are facing existential threat, however, the Sea Serpents will set aside all of their internal rivalries to take on whatever might threaten their way of life.


The Sea Serpents are based in a vast oceanic cave on the coast of Muskshir. Most Muskshiri do not condone the Sea Serpents, but are not militarily powerful enough to oust them. Aside from generalized piracy, a Sea Serpent captain and crew will take on virtually any job for well-paying clients.


Individual Sea Serpent captains will have personal flags, and the Sea Serpents’ shared flag is a white serpent on an emerald-green banner.

TYPHOON

A rowdy armada that prowls the southern seas, Typhoon is a pirate guild that will take on any job for wealthy clientele. They escort, plunder, or sink merchant vessels as asked and are not above changing sides mid-assignment if the price is right. Typhoon ships strictly do not battle one another - a rule that is maintained successfully, unlike the Sea Serpents on the east coast. Typhoon ships often clash with Roshallan’s navy and with the WGC.


As is common for seafaring folk, captains affiliated with Typhoon will fly both their personal flags and the Typhoon colors: a pale blue vortex on a black flag.

MUNDANE GUILDS

ADVENTURE ADVISOR

Adventure Advisor is a service provided to many adventurers and travelers that ranks taverns, inns, marketplaces, smithies, and even chapels according to different metrics. Each location that has an adventure advisor review is ranked on a scale of one to five shields, coins, and stars. The more shields, the safer the location is; the more coins, the greater its expenses; and the more stars the greater the overall quality and enjoyment reported by its patrons. These rankings are created using specially cultivated arcane Signatures and are only visible to people who have purchased an Adventure Advisor Assistant, which is a magical whittled owl that reveals these rankings on an establishment’s main door.


Adventure Advisor is administered by a handful of mages and arcane casters who developed their unique application of the signature cantrip to create such rankings.

ARTISANS INTERNATIONAL

Artisans International certainly has a legitimate side, but is essentially a front for an enormous crime syndicate. This company maintains showrooms, exhibits, and auctions of fine art of many varieties. Patrons can easily find paintings, fine jewelry, and elegant sculptures at these events and storefronts. Artisans International keeps detailed records of all of their transactions and has curated an elaborate system of small and large organizational operations within the umbrella company. Their international status and complicated business practices enable them to easily source, acquire, and resell fine art across Gathandia. Their methods of acquiry generally include extortion, blackmail, and burglary, and Artisans International is careful never to resell a work of art in the same city that they acquired it.

THE GOLDEN ROAD

The Golden Road is not literally a road that connects Kendaria to Anedonia, but it may as well be for how effective it is at facilitating international trade. Based and administered in Bridgemoor, the Golden Road is a collection of affiliated inns, taverns, and marketplaces that facilitate international trade. Participating locations offer deals and discounts to traveling merchants of any nationality who opt into the business. Such merchants must pay an annual dues. The administration of the Golden Road use these funds to finance renovation and monetary aid to participating locations that need such services. Locations that are members of the Golden Road must maintain a clean and safe space, lest they lose membership. The Golden Road has enabled spices, raw goods, ideas, and finished products to be disseminated across broad swathes of Gathandia.

HISTORIANS GUILD

The Historians Guild is an independent institution that catalogs historical artifacts, transcribes and translates recorded histories, and offers presentations to educate the public on historical matters. They often work in collaboration with the Order of Discerning Prewritten History and with Artisans International, both of whom are institutions with overlapping interests. The Historians Guild is controversial among people who are already deeply in touch with their historical roots because the Guild frequently buys or otherwise acquires artifacts of historical significance, removing them from their original context and placing them on display in museums. Some members of the Guild are also secretly soldiers that protect the world from ancient threats, especially those that they awaken in crypts and other archaeological sites.


Their role as silent protectors makes members of the Guild feel that they have earned the right to hoard ancient artifacts in their treasured museums.

WEST GATHANDIA TRADING COMPANY

The West Gathandia Trading Company, or WGC, is a private corporation that builds enormous deep-sea vessels in the shipyards of Pash. The WGC has warehouses and administration buildings in virtually every port city along the south and east of Gathandia, and has many established trading posts on the west coast and in the Inner Sea, as well. The WGC is currently developing vessels that should be capable of long-term voyages, allowing their crew to explore beyond Gathandia and make contact with the other continents.


Most national governments are not fond of WGC because of the company’s aggressive and monopolistic trade practices, but the wealth that they bring to the port cities is usually enough to make monarchs and other rulers begrudgingly overlook WGC’s less savory practices.

PERFORMING TROUPES

THE BUMBLEFUMBLE FESTIVAL

Hundreds of nomads travel together to put on the Bumblefumble Festival outside different cities across the continent. This festival includes carnival food and games, along with performances by the Bumblefumble bards and acrobats. Fire eaters, sword jugglers, lion tamers, and other eccentric performers also draw crowds in droves. The Bumblefumble Festival has a dark secret, however. All of their games are shams designed to separate participants from their coins while giving them just enough hope that they might win if they participate again. Most folk that work the carnival are also cunning pickpockets, lifting just a few coins at a time from their unsuspecting victims, but never stealing enough at once to raise undue suspicion. The entire carnival, which is the size of a small town when set up, can be broken down overnight, allowing the group to quickly clear out when they have overstayed their welcome. The Festival is overseen by conjoined twin halflings Bumble and Fumble, who are both skilled charlatans and can cast arcane magic, respectively specializing in enchanting and illusory functions.

CIRQUE DU PSION

A renowned and spectacular performing group that travels throughout Gathandia, especially in its southern nations, Cirque du Psion astounds commoner and noble alike.


Highly skilled acrobats enhance their performances with psionic powers, enabling them to leap to stunning heights, levitate during their somersaults, and telekinetically manipulate their silk ribbons and sparkling hoops to achieve beautiful synchronized dances. These performances are highly acclaimed and they usually fill a stadium to overflowing for several nights in a row when they perform in a highly populated town.

HARMONIEM

Nobles and other folk from high society can often be found attending performances put on by Harmoniem. This troupe of thirty talented orchestral performers travels Gathandia, performing in prestigious halls, at royal palaces, and in other highbrow locations. There are many such orchestras, but Harmoniem is by far and away the most famous. Harmoniem consists not only of the musical performers, but also skilled caravanners and security who accompany the musicians and protect them on the road.

THE MERRY MINSTRELS

The Merry Minstrels are a large, disorganized group of bards and musicians. They often travel in groups, but some strike out on their own. Whatever the case, the Merry Minstrels bring joy and cheer to the inns and taverns whence they perform. They eagerly seek out news songs and stories to add to their repertoire, informally spreading news, history, and folklore across Gathandia. Joining the Merry Minstrels is not a particularly formal process; one must perform a handful of songs or poetic recitations to a minimum of two current members of the Merry Minstrels. The most famous Merry Minstrel alive today is a six-fingered lute player named Daiquiri Fillingsworth.

STRAVONITES

Taking their name after the Stravon Conclave, the Stravonites are a traveling group of performers who put on elaborate plays. They are led by their dragonfolk playwright named Shilliam, and they have achieved international fame for their lowbrow humor and heartfelt acting. They perform most frequently in the Thunderhead Vale but have been known to travel as far west as Pash and as far northeast as Forsten.

SCHOLARLY ORDERS

ESSENCARY EPISTEMOLOGISTS

The Essencary Epistemologists are a group of scholars that travel around Gathandia giving lectures and debating with students and other scholars at any educational institution where they can find willing participants.


They can be identified by blue academic regalia with silver embroidered threading. By nature, these scholars are most interested in one’s state of being, of existing, and contemplating how the process of thinking works. They are especially interested in how Essence works, what it is, and how it affects a person’s capacity for thought and for experiencing the world. Essencary Epistemologists can be found in virtually any major university across Gathandia.

EVIDENTIARY ANALYSTS OF SCIENTIFIC PRINCIPLES

Physicists, chemists, biologists, and other scientists compose the academic guild known as the Evidentiary Analysts of Scientific Principles. They pursue both mundane and magical experimentation with alacrity, eager to better understand the workings of the physical and metaphysical. Some members of this scholarly order are themselves mages, while others are simply skilled scientists. Recognizable in their deep green academic regalia with red embroidery, these analysts can be found at any major university and some smaller academic institutions, as well. Scholars within this scientific order who specialize in astronomy and metaphysics have strenuous but constructive disagreements about the grand design of the multiverse.

ORDER OF DISCERNING PREWRITTEN HISTORY

In their light tan regalia with dark red embroidery, members of the Order of Discerning Prewritten History can be found both in the lecture hall and at archaeological sites. Scholars from this order are particularly interested in discovering and preserving anything that they can discern about ancient history, from the times before anyone began to record historical events in physical form. A mix of academic scholars, painstakingly careful archaeologists, and daring tomb raiders, members of the Order network with one another to broaden their understanding of ancient events.


The Order also corresponds regularly with the Historians Guild.

TUTORS EXTRAORDINAIRE

A nomadic and decentralized order of oracles, the Tutors Extraordinaire travel throughout Gathandia. They are highly sought after in any nation’s top universities. When a Tutor arrives in a university, they will offer classes and hands-on practice of their craft for any amount of time from a single day to multiple years. This generally depends on how beneficial a Tutor thinks that they can be to a school, but sometimes depends on the Tutor’s own whims and desires.


To become a Tutor Extraordinaire and earn the hooded sash that accompanies the rank, one must instruct classes for three years, overseen by a different Tutor Extraordinaire each year. After being approved by each of the three overseers, the teacher will be granted their sash.

WITCH COVENS

COVEN OF THE EVERWILDS

The Coven of the Everwilds is primarily located in its namesake haunt. Throughout the Everwilds, witches of this coven make their homes in villages or in the wilderness along with at least two of their fellow witches. Witches from this coven actively pursue positive relationships with the Archfae of all six Conclaves and are generally respected by fae of all types. Some cells of this coven can be found in Gathandia, especially in heavily forested areas such as the Kozia Rainforest and the nations around the northern Inner Sea. While these witches are seen as eccentric and at times creepy, many folk understand that their role as intermediaries between the mortal plane and the Archfae Conclaves is very valuable.

SYMPATHY COVEN

The Sympathy Coven is a secretive group of witches that rivals the Illuminati in influence and reach. The Sympathy Coven has far fewer witches than the Everwilds or Naturalist Covens, but these witches are cunning and powerful. Skilled in the art of creating poppets and subtly influencing the people represented by these poppets, the Sympathy Coven’s tentacles subtly pull the strings of power across Ancerra. Some members of the Sympathy Coven have designs on controlling whole empires or continents themselves, but the more altruistic members of the coven simply want to use their invisible hand to guide Gathandia’s nations away from violent conflict with one another. The Sympathy Coven often conducts acts of espionage against the Illuminati.

COVEN OF NATURALISTS

Of the three major witch covens on Gathandia, the Coven of Naturalists is the least consequential to world events. The Coven of Naturalists consists of witches who admire nature, especially its fauna. Many cells of this coven travel the world together, documenting and befriending the various beasts and monstrosities that they encounter. Others remain in roughly the same region for their whole lives and are beloved by the mundane and magical beasts of the region. A young halfling named Katsumash is believed to have the most potential to become a legendary Naturalist; although he has only been a member of the coven for a year, he has already caught the eye of several senior witches in the coven as having a particular way with animals.
