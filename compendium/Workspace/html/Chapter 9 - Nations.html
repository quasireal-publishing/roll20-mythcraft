AHTEN

An ancient nation that developed along the River Ahten, this region would be terribly inhospitable if not for its mighty water source. The River Ahten flows reliably from a ground spring in the nation’s northwest and follows a predictable seasonal calendar down to the week. It floods every spring, and the water level lowers by a couple feet every fall. The river’s predictability has allowed the nation of Ahten to thrive.


The land, otherwise arid and rocky desert, is a flourishing oasis along the river. Farmers use the predictable seasonal flows to harvest abundant crops, which they use to enhance Ahten’s international economy. This is all overseen by a revered godking.


Academic. Ahten has several prominent universities, the largest of which is in Khamen, the capital. Ahten’s scholars and scientists are quite prestigious, and their universities draw the interest of scholars from across the continent. The headmasters of each university in Ahten are sphinxes who relish the constant access to books and intellectual stimulation.


Aristocratic, Political, and Religious. A theomonarchical dynasty rules Ahten. Each monarch is proclaimed to be a demigod, an emissary between gods and humanoids. When a demigod monarch dies, the nation goes into mourning for thirty days. Monarchs have enormous, elaborate mausoleums and are believed to ascend to full godhood upon their deaths, where they watch over Ahten’s prosperity. Ahten’s current godking, Pharenkan, places a strong focus on the nation’s military given the threat that Roshallan poses.


Mercantile and Trade. In addition to Ahten’s renowned universities, Ahten produces tremendous amounts of crops given its arid climate. It also mines salt and gold from the foothills of the volcanic mountains to its northeast. Furthermore, Ahten has negotiated mining agreements with its neighbor, Gavare, which allows Ahteni miners to operate in Gavare’s territory of the Smoldering Canyon. This supplements Ahten’s economy with magical crafted goods and with ink pigments that contain magical properties.


Militant. Ahten’s soldiers are sturdy and resilient to the extreme heat of the arid summer. Each soldier that graduates from training is tattooed with magical ink pigments from the Smoldering Canyon, enabling them to go for more days at a time without food and water than they otherwise could. Competent on chariots and horseback, and skilled in the ways of desert fighting, they have been able to keep Roshallan at bay.


Social. Ahten’s large agrarian community quickly developed advanced agricultural practices and infrastructure.


The River Ahten floods predictably every year, allowing farmers to utilize floodwaters to their greatest irrigational effect. Because of the river’s predictability, many in Ahten view it as sacred and understand that their society might crumble if the river ever missed a year of its predictable patterns. Ahteni common folk tend to be hospitable towards one another but suspicious of strangers. Most Ahteni are very reverent of their godkings.


Underworld. When one of the Ahteni monarchs dies, they are buried with many riches. This draws irreverent tomb raiders in search of easy loot. The catch, of course, is that many of these tombs are riddled with booby traps and curses designed to repel looters. Furthermore, as repayment for being made headmasters of the universities, Ahteni sphinxes guard the tombs with alacrity.

THREE MAJOR CITIES

Ahten has three major cities, in addition to numerous towns and farming communities along the river. These major cities each house one of Ahten’s most famous universities.


Isirem. Isirem is Ahten’s most populous city, located at the mouth of the River Ahten. Its access to the ocean makes it a valuable trading hub with other nations that engage in maritime trade.


Khalen. Ahten’s capital is located approximately halfway between the river’s spring and its mouth. Khalen boasts enormous monuments to the glory of Ahten and its gods, and the capital is also only a day’s camel ride from the Valley of the Gods. Khalen’s monuments were constructed primarily by golems.


I feel sorry for the mummies of Ahten. They will never experience the freedom of having not a single bodily organ to hinder their clean, pure bone.

-Grim


Set’then. The smallest of Ahten’s three major cities, Set’then is a fortified city that guards the river’s ground springs. If Roshallan were to conquer this city, then they could potentially dam the river, devastating Ahten.

POINTS OF INTEREST

Valley of Gods. The Valley of Gods is where many enormous monuments have been built, along with elaborate mausoleums that have been dug into the side of the rocky hills in the area. Sphinxes and tomb raiders are constantly at war over this territory and the riches hidden deep within.


Smoldering Canyon. The Smoldering Canyon, though primarily within Gavare’s borders, is important to Ahteni culture. The canyon is ripe with valuable minerals, such as mysterious avadrium, primor, and mithril. The canyon’s vegetation also produces pigments that can be used to create magical tattoos. Finally, the Smoldering Canyon produces magical flames and other energies that Ahteni scholars have learned how to contain in stable form.

ANEDONIA

Stretching across a pleasant environment of modest humidity, warm summers, and mild rainy winters, Anedonia is a federation of city-states that share the same cultural practices, holidays, and religious beliefs, though each city-state has different governing systems and economic strengths. The Anedonian islands are rocky but temperate, allowing pastoral culture to flourish alongside orchards of olives and citrus.


The Anedonian city-states operate independently of one another in most instances and have waged war on one another at various points throughout history. However, the whole of Anedonia has rallied together whenever they feel that their way of life faces existential threat, and when united in such fashion they are a tremendous force to be reckoned with. Even the mighty Empire of Roshallan has been unable to subjugate Anedonia due to the Anedonians’ fierce loyalty to their nation above the differences of their individual states.


Religious. Anedonians primarily worship the Ashyidir and Stravon Conclaves of the Archfae and have spun elaborate mythologies around these deities. Although many of these stories never actually occurred, the way that the deities are depicted in these stories is reasonably accurate. Each Anedonian city-state has one patron deity but pays homage to all deities out of these Conclaves.


Social. Anedonian society revolves around free-market maritime traders. Many of Anedonia’s city-states are island nations, so in addition to pastoral and orchard cultivation, the city-states also thrive on a fishing industry. Anedonians are very hospitable to those from any other Anedonian city-state unless they are actively at war with that state. They are generally courteous but suspicious of foreigners.


Underworld. It has not acquired great power yet, but a criminal empire has taken root in Anedonia and is working to extend its influence to every city-state. The criminal empire aims to unite the states into a formal nation with the crime lords as the new oligarchs.

CITY-STATES OF NOTE

Anedonia’s three most prominent city-states are described in a little more detail below, but this is only three out of a couple dozen city-states that are part of the Anedonian federation.


Sethan. The most economically influential of Anedonia’s city-states, Sethan occupies a large island near Anedonia’s mainland coast. The Sethan Academy is the most prestigious in the world, drawing scholars from the whole world to teach their disciplines. Sethan is ruled using an elected senate. In times of crisis, the other city-states of Anedonia will rally behind Sethan as the effective capital of all the city-states.


I dream of one day learning from the musicians in the Sethan Academy! Their pentatonic compositions are effervescent.

-Flanagan


Taspar. Taspar is the largest city-state on Anedonia’s mainland, and the closest to the Roshallani border. It is an autocratic city-state that trains its citizens to be soldiers from the time they are only four years old. This produces some of the toughest, most calloused soldiers in all Gathandia and has prevented Roshallan from gaining a foothold in Anedonia. While Taspar and Sethan have strenuous philosophical disagreements, they respect one another’s accomplishments and view each other as steadfast allies. Taspar’s King Lendonar has proven a mighty military commander and Roshallan has yet to make any headway on conquering Anedonia due to Lendonar’s efforts.


Terec. Terec has a much smaller population than Sethan and Taspar, and is far from the third most populous city-state. It is, however, the third most notable in terms of its contributions to Anedonian society. Situated on a large island deeper into the Inner Sea, Terec possesses deep mines rich in many minerals, including the mystical minerals known as Avadrium and Mithril. Terec’s deep mines have entire towns in their crossroads, but the still unexplored caverns beneath this island are riddled with all manner of dangerous beasts.

  • Taspar has come under threat from Roshallan, and they are looking for any competent soldiers to take up arms and help reclaim a tactically advantageous set of foothills.

BETAAL

A small nation that makes its home on the tops of several of the Cloudpiercer Mountains, Betaal’s size in no way reflects its significance. Its people are tough, innovative, and capable of surviving with quite little. The Betaalese are also held to be generous and hospitable with foreigners, although few ever reach Betaal’s highest cities in the peaks of its tallest mountains.


Academic. Each of Betaal’s towns has a monastery where scholars, clergy, and mystics can study or meditate. These monasteries focus much more on the spiritual aspects of the humanoid experience than on traditional academic topics, although Betaal’s two largest cities do have large libraries as well.


Aristocratic and Political. Betaalese society rejects the idea of a ruling elite and believe that those capable of shepherding other people’s souls should do so on both a spiritual and political level. As such, Betaal’s religious leaders - the Prabi - are also its political leaders. If such a leader ever displays an exorbitant lifestyle, they are removed from their order, as asceticism is one of their central tenets.


Mercantile and Trade. Betaal has little in the way of natural resources, other than yaks and snow. Betaal exports both wool and water in abundance, and the dry nation of Kandiff regularly buys water from Betaal. Betaal also exports philosophy, fictional literature, and artistic works, though they must first import dyes and other mediums for these artworks from other nations. Betaal imports raw and finished materials, along with foodstuffs, from any nation that they can reach for trade. They generally trade with Kandiff, Noord, and Doshairiel.


Militant. Taking life is heavily frowned upon in Betaal, which is generally a highly pacifistic nation. When Betaal is attacked, mystics and psions will use their telekinetic powers to cause avalanches or illusory spectacles that will dissuade aggressors from attempting to take Betaal.


Religious. The Prabi practice extreme asceticism. When they are not legislating or counseling their constituents, they often meditate. Many are known to be capable of astral projection and other great psionic powers, and they purport to have spoken directly with representatives of the Celestial Council. It is believed that when a Betaalese monk is chosen to lead a town, they are invited into a psionic mind palace that has been around for thousands of years and contains the wisdom of every Prabi that came before.


One of the Prabi visited me when I was meditating. I have been encouraged to climb the mountain and seek out answers atop the crest of Tapsankad. The journey is long, but one day I will make it.

-Knack


Social. Like the monks that lead or train to lead the nation, many of the Betaalese common folk also practice asceticism, though not to the degree practiced by the monks. Most commoners do not see the need for any wealth beyond enough food and shelter for their families, and perhaps some entertaining books or games to pass the cold, dark nights. Betaalese are hospitable to foreign travelers and will usually offer guidance through the steep and treacherous mountains.


Underworld. Shinus are independently operating cells of skilled assassins that wear folded cloth armor, allowing them to approach their victims silently. Most underworld activity in Betaal is performed by Shinus. A small group of highly skilled Shinus, operating outside the purview of the theocratic rulers of Betaal, will infiltrate and destabilize nations that they think might threaten Betaal. Some of these Shinus also become mercenaries, selling their specialized services to any with enough coin. By both taking life and hoarding wealth, Betaalese Shinus are antithetical to the teachings of the official leaders.

TAPSANKAD

The oldest Betaalese city, Tapsankad is nestled just beneath the highest peak in Betaal. The peak of the mountain is left bare as it is a holy site where monks go to engage in astral projection and, when the time comes, to transcend beyond this world. Tapsankad is a city rich with ancient history, having been founded before the World-Quake.


Within the city, Tapsankad contains many residences, some modest markets, and curated parks lush with cold-weather plants. The citizens of Tapsankad hold festivals on each solstice and each equinox, which are the only days each year where hedonism is not viewed in contempt.

QUEST IDEAS

  • The heroes must climb the Cloudpiercer Mountains to reach Tapsankad. There, they must entreat the monks to send more water and spare Kandiff from a terrible drought.
  • One of Betaal’s towns has been attacked by an icy monster. The Prabi ask the heroes to go defeat the monster and liberate its citizens.
  • Tension is mounting between the Prabi and the Shinus as the Noords threaten to conquer Betaal. The heroes must decide who they agree with and seek to broker a peace or, short of that, throw their support behind one faction or the other.

BISHAL

The wide open plains and arid steppes of Bishal are home to several large tribes, all of whom share a general culture but who are often at war with one another.


Each Bishalian tribe follows a nomadic lifestyle, leading their massive horse herds to fresh pastures or following the herds of bison that they use for food, tools, and clothing.


Aristocratic and Political. Each tribal lord is responsible for their own tribe’s actions but has little interest in the other tribes beyond occasional trades or skirmishes. On the rare occasions that multiple tribal lords feel that their entire nation is threatened, then the lords will come together where the two major rivers in Bishal join together into one. There, the lords will negotiate working together and for how long they are bound to do so. The largest and most belligerent Bishalian tribal lord is named Tilion, and he has made quite a name for himself and his tribe as a military power to be reckoned with.


Mercantile and Trade. Bishalians typically trade internally instead of internationally. They use clay from the riverbeds to make pottery, and use every part of bison and other game animals to make food and various tools or clothes. Usually, if they consider trading with a foreign town or merchant caravan, they will instead just raid it.


Militant. Bishalians make excellent cavaliers, and many Bishalian warriors are more at home on horseback than they are on foot. Capable of firing shortbows while riding, a Bishalian tribe’s warriors can conduct quick and brutal raids on the towns of other nations that border their territory.


Religious and Social. The people of Bishal tend to be very religious and believe horses to be sacred animals. They worship A’rl’ng, the great steed of the Shibmar Conclave of the Archfae, as a guiding patron. Most Bishalians believe in a cycle of reincarnation where when a humanoid dies, they are reborn as a horse, and when a horse dies, they are reborn as a humanoid. People generally hold that Centaurs are the reincarnations of especially brave or honorable humanoids or steeds.

THE RIVERFLOW

Where Bishal’s two rivers come together, there is a tranquil set of hills known as the Riverflow. This is where Bishalian lords come together when they need to confer on a national level.


The waters of the Riverflow run cold and sweet. I’m not much a fan of Bishalian warmongering, but the Riverflow, at least, is a place of peace.

-Chauncey

QUEST IDEAS

  • In his old age, a cruel Bishalian lord has grown militant and violent against the other tribes. The heroes must put an end to his rampage.
  • Bishal is threatened by some external force. The heroes must travel to each lord in turn and convince them to travel to the Riverflow and establish a consensus.

BRIDGEMOOR

Bridgemoor is an economically powerful independent city-state built entirely on a massive bridge that spans the Great Chasm. As the only other ways to cross the chasm involve going hundreds of miles north to circumvent it or climbing down, navigating across its monster-riddled trenches, and climbing back up, Bridgemoor has made a fortune off of merchants and travelers that use the city-state as passage over the Chasm.


Bridgemoor is just large enough that a full day’s travel will not quite get one across the chasm, forcing travelers to spend extra coin in one of Bridgemoor’s many inns and taverns. Through this calculated design and by exacting a small toll on every traveler, Bridgemoor thrives.


Bridgemoor is governed by an oligarchy known as the Meritocrats. Selected once every ten years, prospective Meritocrats must first pass a test proving their scholastic and economic knowledge, and then each former Meritocrat picks their successor. This has generally led to rampant nepotism with very rare exceptions.


Academic. Bridgemoor has a large university and scholastic achievements are widely celebrated throughout the city-state. Those with more formal education are more likely to be rewarded by Meritocrats or become one themselves, so anyone who can afford to send their children to the university will do so.


Mercantile and Trade. Bridgemoor is a thriving mercantile state. Due to a high volume of travelers and merchants flowing into and through the city-state, Bridgemoor has one of the strongest economies in its region. It trades especially frequently with Nalathesh and Kendaria as it connects the two nations, but merchants from other nations will stop in Bridgemoor on their way to or from Kendaria and Nalathesh as well.


Bridgemoor is vital to the health of the Great Chasm nations. Without it, trade patterns would be thrown into horrible disarray.

-Marcus


Militant. Bridge Knight Commander Ser Kellion Blacksky maintains strict discipline in the streets of Bridgemoor. Under his guidance, the Bridge Knights have become more disciplined and competent than they have ever been historically. Few in number, the Bridge Knights could not wage war on a neighboring country, but they could defend Bridgemoor indefinitely or until food and water run out.


Political. The Meritocrats oversee legislation and appoint the Bridge Knight Commander and the Master Magister. These two figures, in turn, will appoint their subordinate administrators (Bridge Knight Captains and Magisters, respectively) to maintain executive and judicial law within the city.


Religious. Bridgemoor does not have an official religion, and each citizen is encouraged to pursue their own beliefs. Several temples dedicated to specific pantheons or tenets can be found throughout Bridgemoor, along with unaffiliated houses of worship that offer a quiet, contemplative space for any belief system.


Social. Bridgemoor has a very diverse culture. Folk from Nalathesh and Kendaria make up most of Bridgemoor’s citizens, but some traders from lands beyond decided to settle in Bridgemoor as well, leading to a confluence of many beliefs. It’s not uncommon for there to be at least two festivals in a given month due to the wide spectrum of cultures and religions and their respective celebratory events.


Underworld. Bridgemoor has no shortage of white-collar criminals that use any loophole in the city-state’s tax system and mercantile economy to line their own pockets with a little more silver and gold. Overt crime is uncommon thanks to Ser Kellion’s iron discipline, but cult activity lurks in the sewers and storage cellars beneath the streets. Called the Underbridge Cult, these miscreants are a countercultural movement looking to prove how meaningless and absurd “Meritocracy” truly is. Among other things, they try to summon fiends from Inferno to wreak havoc in the city.

POINTS OF INTEREST

The Great Chasm. The Great Chasm stretches for thousands of miles from north to south along the eastern half of Gathandia.


Bridgemoor spans one of its narrowest points, connecting Nalathesh to Kendaria. Intrepid explorers have discovered crypts full of strange technology from an ancient civilization, but some have also met their grizzly end at the hands of equally strange beasts that lurk in the Chasm’s endless caves.

  • The Underbridge Cult has successfully summoned a Bonewraith, who lurks in the sewers and sends Zombies to terrorize the city-state.
  • An explorer wants to hire the heroes as protection as they venture into the chasm.
  • Ser Kellion Blacksky pursues authoritarian control of the streets in response to cult activity, but this harms Bridgemoor’s economy. How can the heroes step in and fix this?

BUYLEIST

For many generations, Buyleist was significantly weaker than its neighbor nation, Unjaarn. Buyleist’s rulers lived under the constant shadow of fear that Unjaar would decide to invade them or cut them off from international trade, and unfortunately this did come to pass a couple generations ago.


As it turns out, this was a mistake on Unjaar’s part. Buyleist made an alliance with Vel, and Vel’s nefarious mages sowed discord in Unjaar’s internal affairs. This eventually led to a complete schism within Unjaar, which resulted in the nation splitting in two. Now, the two split nations - Unjaar and Vadan - are both smaller and weaker than Buyleist, allowing the Buyleistians to flex their muscles in the region.


Academic. Buyleist is building its first major university and hopes to attract scholars from other nations to help get the educational program off the ground. Vel’s Mages of the Beach are more than willing to assist in this process.


Aristocratic and Political. Buyleist rejects the notion of aristocracy or a caste system wholesale. Instead, their governing body is a representative democracy elected by any citizen who makes a large enough annual salary to be offered a ballot. While these elections offer the illusion of choice, the Mages of the Beach from Vel are really in charge and make sure that each “election” actually promotes a Buyleistian who will do exactly what the Mages instruct.


There’s a stink about Buyleist. The folk purport to be free, but Vel plays the nation like a fiddler plays her strings.

-Lyla


Mercantile and Trade. Buyleist exports many dye pigments, spices, and foodstuffs unique to the swamp delta.


While Unjaar and Vadan also export many of the same materials, Buyleist’s economic advantage allows them to undersell their competition.


Militant. The military in Buyleist is medium-sized and quite competent. It could almost certainly defeat Vadan or Unjaar independently, but if the two made an alliance against Buyleist, then Buyleist might be in trouble.


Religious. While Buyleistians can practice any religion that they wish, doing so is generally discouraged. The governing body reminds Buyleist’s citizenry that they are in such times of prosperity because of the wisdom and generosity of the Mages of the Beach, and it is truly the Mages who should be venerated.


Social. Citizens of Buyleist are encouraged to pursue the careers and hobbies that most interest them. This keeps Buyleistians generally content and preoccupied with their own ventures, though some political dissidents speak out against their nation’s alliance with Vel.


Underworld. The most fervent political opponents to Vel have formed a covert rebellion. This has not yet developed into violence, although many who are aware of the resistance movement think that it’s only a matter of time.

SELMORT

The capital of Buyleist is the fortified stilt city of Selmort. The city is built in tiers, with wide, flat barges holding apartments where the poor live. Elevated platform houses, suspended over the swamp on stilts and connected by rope bridges, are home to the middle class and to the marketplaces.


A large bastion built of stone and clay is home to Buyleist’s national governing body. In the fetid swamps behind this bastion, a small M’l’ykkri has buried itself in the water. Political dissidents that upset the Mages of the Beach often wind up as snacks for this horrible monster.

POINTS OF INTEREST

Swamplands of the Great Chasm. A humid tropical swamp spreads across a vast river delta on the southern end of the Great Chasm. Many small islands blanket this region, separated by shallow waters that would be treacherous for large boats. A wide variety of natural flora and fauna mix with the strange minerals and ancient technologies buried deep in the Chasm; as a result, unnatural land formations and horrible monsters lurk outside of the stilted cities and villages that speckle the swamplands. “Travel by day, and you’ll be safe; travel by night, you’ll get a fright” is a common rhyme in these lands, and children dare one another to explore the swamps outside their settlements.

QUEST IDEAS

  • The heroes get accused of political malfeasance and risk becoming a M’l’ykkri snack if they cannot escape.
  • The heroes might get caught up in the three-way trade war between Buyleist, Vadan, and Unjaar. They will have to carefully evaluate who they support.
  • The Mages of the Beach promise great rewards to the heroes if they will find and quash a burgeoning rebellion.
  • Illegal drug trade is running rampant in the swamps beneath the stilted districts of Selmort. The heroes must find and put an end to these trades.

CHEE

The forest nation of Chee used to be much larger and more prosperous. Originally, Chee was part of Chehozran, but after a short civil war they split along the mountain range that separates the two regions. Chee never recovered economically from the civil war, struggling to make an international name for itself. Before it was able to achieve any notable successes, Roshallan annexed Chee.


Academic. Chee has no major academies. All of its small schools that teach basic educational subjects are managed by Roshallan.


Aristocratic and Political. Chee is governed by lesser kings assigned to the region from Roshallan. The Chee folk have no say in their own governmental practices.


Mercantile and Trade. Chee’s forests produce a bounty of foodstuffs and timber, along with colorful pigments. The coast teems with fish, and Deeprock’s shipyards manufacture powerful shallow and deep water ships.


Chee’s fine shipyard economy does not erase the tragedy of her erasure by a tyrant’s hand.

-Finneas


Military. Chee has no military; its armed forces were completely defeated and disbanded by Roshallan.


Religious. In the privacy of their own homes, most Chee folk keep alive the traditions of their ancestors. Like the Chehozran, the Chee believe that one day the kaijan of old will return: some will be benevolent, and many will be tyrannical. They pray that the kaijan who return to the nation of Chee will strive to protect, rather than to destroy.


Social. While many Chee folk wish that they could run their own country instead of being controlled by Roshallan, they also recognize that Roshallan’s powerful military offers them great security. Most folk of Chee simply go about their lives, helping out their friends when they can and avoiding strangers.


Underworld. Some nationalistic Chee folk have taken it upon themselves to overthrow Roshallan. While the more rational among these rebels simply try to disrupt Roshallan’s presence in Chee’s territory, some ambitious rebels intend to overthrow the entire Roshallani empire.

DEEPROCK

Chee’s only major city, Deeprock, is micromanaged by Roshallan. It has a fortified palace where a Roshallani regional king oversees the nation of Chee. A strong military presence keeps the peace, and Deeprock’s enormous shipyards produce large, powerful naval vessels at a rapid rate, which Roshallan uses to conquer other nations that border the Inner Sea.

QUEST IDEAS

  • Roshallan hires the heroes to head into Chee and put down a developing rebellion. The rebels might also approach the heroes and ask them to help chase Roshallan out of the nation. Caught between the two parties’ desires, the heroes must weigh their morals and their desire for wealthy compensation.
  • Roshallan will have unparalleled naval superiority if they can finish building their flagship, the Thunderhead. Chee’s rebels want the heroes to help them sabotage the vessel so that it will sink once many hundreds of Roshallani soldiers are on deck and out in the deep sea.

CHEHOZRAN

Once an enormous forest nation that spread from the Inner Sea to the oceans west of Gathandia, Chehozran is now half the size it once was. Generations ago, pragmatic leaders split the northeastern half of the nation off into the new kingdom of Chee in an effort to consolidate resources in two locations. Chee has since been conquered by Roshallan.


Chehozran remains independent of Roshallan, but Chehozran continually adds additional fortifications and trained soldiers to its border as it anticipates invasion from the aggressive nation at any point.


Academic. Chehozran has little to offer in the way of formal schooling. The elderly in towns throughout the nation will often teach the young, and those who desire often travel to Pash to receive additional formal education.


Aristocratic and Political. Chehozran does not have an aristocracy besides the royal family. Following the tradition of primogeniture, each eldest child becomes the next monarch. Besides the monarchy, other officials and representatives are democratically elected. This preserves the continuity of leadership at the highest levels of government while allowing the people’s voices to be heard through local elections.


Mercantile and Trade. Chehozran produces sizable quantities of lumber, game, vegetables, minerals, clay, pigments, and fine art made using such materials. They primarily trade within the borders of their own nation but have open trade agreements with Pash.


Militant. The military in Chehozran is large, skilled, and disciplined. They do not have the strongest iron or the most modern weaponry, but they know their terrain exceptionally well. Thus far, they have held off all preliminary Roshallani scouting parties.


Religious. Looking to the ancient past, the Chehozranese worship the kaijans of old. They believe that one day, the kaijans will return and will scour the world again. While this will cause much devastation, Chehozranese prophets claim that some among the kaijan will be protectors that shelter the faithful from the wrath of the other kaijans.


Social. The folk of Chehozran trust their own and will provide for one another. Shelter and food are considered communal, and few are ever left wanting. This hospitable practice is generally afforded to Pashans as well. This courtesy does not, however, extend to other foreigners, who are treated with suspicion if not outright hostility.


Underworld. Given that Chehozran is focused on the threat of Roshallan, there are a handful of opportunistic gangs. A crime syndicate also extends throughout Chehozran, exploiting the air of fear.


I wouldn’t be surprised if Felson Weskh’s network extended as far west as Chehozran.

-Avery

YIRISK

Chehozran’s capital city, Yirisk, is near the head of the Chehozran River in a wide valley between the two southern prongs of the mountain range that embraces Chehozran’s northern regions. Yirisk’s riverside docks frequently house Pashan merchant vessels. In preparation for the worst case scenario, Chehozranese officials have been preparing a contingency in the event of Roshallani invasion. A fortified network of caverns in the mountains could hold most of Chehozran’s population for several years in the event of a protracted siege.

  • The Roshallani have finally invaded Chehozran. The heroes must help civilians evacuate and reach the fortifications at Yirisk, doing whatever they can to forestall Roshallani advances.
  • The excavators designing fortified caverns have come across a nest of ancient monsters. The heroes must defeat these monsters to make room for additional wartime bunkers.
  • Parsors with a twisted sense of humor have dammed the Chehozran River, blocking their economic ties to Pash. The heroes need to convince the Parsors to remove the dam.

CONSTEL

Constel is a nation of mixed opinions and mixed peoples. Once the heart of a much larger and prosperous monarchy, now a small and economically struggling democracy, Constel has many traditional folk and many who challenge the old ways of living.


Constel’s chief export is timber from the Old Forest. Loggers have deforested Constel so rapidly that it is now more plains and pasture than forest, much the opposite of what it was less than a hundred years ago. Traditionalists warn that this will be Constel’s doom: the Old Forest is strange and unforgiving of such sins. Progressives, however, argue that this was a necessary step to get Constel on its feet. The destruction of the Old Forest is largely what gave Constel the financial means to establish its university, which attracts intellectuals from the world over.


Me? I’m not much one for tradition, but yeah, mess with the Old Forest and the Old Forest will mess back.

-Dungrel


Academic. Lentira has a large university with scholars that specialize in legal theory and philosophy. The University of Constel attracts many students from many nations, leading to a vast dissemination of ideas. Headmaster Edwardton keeps in very close contact with the governing representatives of Constel.


Aristocratic and Political. After the ancient civil war that led to the split with Kendaria, Constel underwent a tremendous governmental change and became a democracy. Each town and city sends a representative to Lentira, where the representatives negotiate legislation on behalf of their constituents.


Mercantile and Trade. Constel exports tremendous amounts of timber and finished wooden products, along with clay goods and philosophical ideas. Constel trades locally with both Kendaria and Forsten and conducts maritime trade with Derk. While Forsten and Kendaria both used to be part of a single nation along with Constel, the Constelines do not generally begrudge either nation and try to foster strong alliances with both.


Militant. The Consteline standing army is quite small and ill-trained. Constel relies on a strong alliance with both Kendaria and Forsten to protect its southern and northern borders, while the Dreamhaze Mountains protect its west and the ocean its east.


Religious. Most Constelines are not particularly religious. While the Kendarians and Forsteners think that the Old Forest should be respected and paid almost a supernatural reverie, Constelines tend to be more pragmatic and do not think much of using the forest for an abundance of timber.


Social. Constelines are usually amicable but reserved. They will make small talk with virtually anyone, but are hesitant to make lasting friendships.


Underworld. A large crime syndicate called the Constellation attempts to undermine Constel’s current regime. Members of the Constellation believe an ancient prophecy that cutting down the Old Forest will spell doom for those who condone such behavior, and they engage in cult-like destruction of governmental property and sabotage of mercantile supply roads.

LENTIRA

The shining capital city of Constel overlooks the eastern sea, about a day and a half north of Kendaria’s capital when the wind is in your sails. Lentira has a splendid university, several theatres, a sporting stadium, and famously large gardens where students and philosophers might relax or contemplate life. Its governmental buildings are constructed from hewn marble, and many of its other buildings are made of fine stone and polished wood.

POINTS OF INTEREST

Dreamhaze Mountains. The mountains that overlook the Old Forest are known as the Dreamhaze Mountains, so named because of the strange, dreamlike quality evoked by their misty slopes. It’s very easy to become lost in the Dreamhaze Mountains, even for skillful trackers, so most just avoid the mountains altogether. Those who do journey into the mountains often find inexplicable runes, come face to face with incarnations of their old memories, and must contend with strange monsters they have never heard of.


The Old Forest. The Old Forest sprawls across most of Kendaria and Forsten, and was once a major feature of Constel as well. While monster sightings are rare in these picturesque woods, travelers and locals alike are often left with a vague sense of unease after hiking therein. Children tell each other ghost stories that seem a little too real, somehow, and even skilled hunters have occasionally gone missing with no explanation. Although much of the Old Forest in Constel has been chopped down, these feelings of unease persist even when people spend time where the forest used to loom.

DERK

Derk is a rocky and hilly country blanketed in the west by a sprawling pine forest. With mild summers and frigid winters, Derk suffers the brunt of the arctic winds rolling down from the north. Its stony hills and cold forests make agricultural practice difficult, so Derk largely relies on pastoral farming inland and fishing along the coastline.


Academic. Derk has a few small schools along its coastal cities, but these are significantly lacking in most contemporary scientific practices and philosophical thought. Most Derkese that live inland do not receive a formal education.


Aristocratic and Political. Derk has a classical feudal model with a monarch whose eldest child takes their place when they die. Lords and ladies oversee regions, and individual towns and communities are run by mayors. Each mayor can levy a small militia from their town. The Derkese monarchy - currently ruled by Erwin Derk II - is nationally weak and is not very successful at enforcing cooperation from lords or mayors.


Mercantile and Trade. Derkese sailors trade regularly with Forsten, Constel, and Kendaria to the south as well as Yodvan to the north. Overland, Derk primarily trades with Forsten.


Militant. Derk’s military is strongest along the coast, with several dozen naval ships sailed by ferocious warriors. Their military is much weaker in the forested lands to the west, and towns in this region are often subject to raids from Bishal.


Religious. Derkese religious practice revolves around the Stravon Conclave of the Archfae. By worshiping the Conclave that embodies emotional states, the Derkese place an emphasis on being fully true to one’s own emotional reaction to an experience and what lessons one might learn from that.


Social. Towns in Derk’s inland tend to be down-to-earth, honest folk. They are hospitable but guarded around strangers, and if strangers prove to be trustworthy then they will be welcomed as one of the town’s own. Along the coast, Derkese society is a little more vibrant and one or more theatres for a wide variety of performing arts are common in each city.


Underworld. Small gangs work to ship illicit goods in and out of Derk’s ports, but there is no centralized criminal authority. Derk’s poorly organized town guards are helpless to stop these smugglers.

ICEPOINT AND WHITEBREAK

The capital of Derk is not actually its largest city. Icepoint is a stony fortress on the northern coast from which the reigning monarch decrees laws and settles disputes, so it is technically the capital. However, Derk’s largest city is Whitebreak.


Nestled in a cove along the southern side of the peninsula and so named because of the large waves that break into white foam against the cliffs around the bay, Whitebreak is home to a massive port. Whitebreak is responsible for over half of Derk’s whole economy. As such, the lord of Whitebreak often treats the monarch with an ironic deference in public and bullies the monarch in private.


Lord Bossypants of Whitebreak really runs the nation. Long as he keeps Derkese junk flowin’ my way I don’t give a crap.

DOSHAIRIEL

Doshairiel is quite a large nation, sprawling along the northeastern side of the Cloudpiercer Mountains. The nation is almost entirely covered in temperate forest, but is wide enough from north to south that different regions experience quite different summers. Southern Doshairiel, primarily deciduous, has muggy summers with frequent thunderstorms and mild winters with occasional rain and sleet. The north of the nation, more boreal than deciduous, has dry, mild summers and snowy winters.


Doshairiel is one of the oldest nations on Gathandia, having been founded by the star elves before the events of written history. As elves are long-lived and can communicate with one another in their dreams, Doshairiel has a complicated and highly functional governmental system. While star elves and forest elves are both highly populous in Doshairiel, the nation is home to many lineages from across the continent.


Academic. Virtually every settlement in Doshairiel has some kind of school. The larger cities, naturally, will have well-funded universities, as opposed to the single-room lecture halls that one might find in a small village. Education is mandatory for children, but optional for teens, who might choose to apprentice themselves to a master of a particular trade instead. Those who continue their formal education are more likely to become governors or oligarchs.


Aristocratic and Political. Doshairiel does not have an aristocracy. Instead, an oligarchy of fifteen elders oversees the happenings of the nation, delegating responsibilities to regional governors. These oligarchs each choose their heirs and choose when to step down from office, promoting their heirs to become the next oligarch in their stead.


Mercantile and Trade. Doshairiel produces a bounty of timber, foodstuffs, and animal byproducts. In addition to trading internally, Doshairieli merchants also trade with Betaal and whoever they can safely reach via the Inner Ocean. They also trade with Falth, though successful merchants that do so are careful not to get swindled or sucked into a blackmail scheme with one of the crime lords.


Militant. Doshairiel’s military is centralized and commanded by generals appointed by the oligarchy. Its military commanders study philosophy and magical practices as much as military theory, implementing both knowledge bases into their training regimens and combat techniques. Doshairieli soldiers, especially those stationed near the Inner Sea where raids from Renden are common, are quite competent.


Religious. Given their heritage as a nation founded by star elves, Doshairieli ascribe great meaning to the patterns of the stars. Most religious scholars in Doshairiel associate certain constellations with Avatars and beings from the Celestial Council. Many also pay homage to Ancerra’s five moons and will have religious services any time there is a full moon.


Social. Governors and town administrators are responsible for maintaining a delicate balance of free market and communist methodologies to keep the balance of wealth spread equally across their settlements. Doshairieli towns exact heavy taxes on their citizenry, keep a fraction of the profit for infrastructural advancement, and return the rest of the taxes to the town’s citizens in equal shares. This allows for one’s own financial advancement slowly over many years, and generally mitigates the worst effects of poverty. On the whole, Doshairieli folk are content with this system.


Underworld. Doshairiel does not have an organized crime syndicate, but local gangs operate in the larger cities, mugging unfortunate passersby and running protection rackets. Powerful Falthic crime networks also have a presence in some Doshairieli cities and even in Starborea.

STARBOREA, THE WORLD-TREE

Looming miles tall above the rest of the forest, and even taller than some of the small peaks in the Cloudpiercer Range, the ancient World-Tree keeps its silent vigil. The World-Tree, known as Starborea, is indeed tall enough to scrape the clouds in the sky.


Visited from far and wide by pilgrims and tourists that wish to gaze upon its greatness, Starborea is also home to both the capital of Doshairiel and an independent city-state.


Shairea, the capital of Doshairiel, occupies the area on and around Starborea’s mighty roots. From Shairea, the oligarchy legislates and oversees the happenings of their nation.


Within the tree, Starborea is an independent city-state. A religious group known as the Celestial Cardinals reside in Starborea, overseeing the orthodox doctrine of all who worship the Celestial Council. There are always twenty-four Cardinals, who offer counsel and guidance to priests throughout Gathandia.


Besides the religious order, Starborea is a fully functioning city-state with its own militia, economy (they harvest sap and fungus grown in farms within the mighty tree), and governing officials. The governor of Starborea passes legislation for the city-state while deferring to the Cardinals on matters of great import. Starborea’s current governor is a star elf named Dencassan Briarye. Dencassan is quite elderly, even by elvish standards, and in his vast knowledge and wisdom he is very slow to act, taking painstaking amounts of time to deliberate on any decision.


Scholars and philosophers find the World-Tree endlessly fascinating, and some scholars have begun to develop theories that Starborea is in fact the parent plant to the other World-Trees found throughout Gathandia.


I can’t wait to lay eyes on the World-Tree! I will compose a magnificent melody reflective of its mighty boughs.

-Flanagan



The World-Tree is endlessly fascinating. It would seem that the world could not support flora so large, and yet here it stands.

-Finneas

DYSPARA

The peoples of Dyspara are generally hospitable and down-to-earth. The natural climate is anything but. Dyspara is a harsh landscape of jagged, rocky deserts and rolling sand dunes. Along the coast, scant vegetation offers marginal relief from the scouring heat and sand-specked wind. Worst of all, far above this parched land, an enormous cube of water floats in the sky, tantalizingly out of reach. Dysparans have never been able to reach the water cube, so to access water they dig very deep wells and by desalinating ocean water.


Academic. Dyspara’s capital city has a small academy that has competent lectures on reading, arithmetic, and basic history, but struggles to attract any prestigious scholars.


Aristocratic, Political, and Social. Dyspara is functionally a tribal federation. Each tribe has a patriarch or matriarch who is elected to lead the tribe. These elders elect representatives who travel to Dyspara’s port city of Stemorna, where these representatives negotiate legislation that all of their representative tribes can abide by.


With a few exceptions, Dysparan tribes are nomadic, following herds of leatherbacks and moving with the weather in an effort to find the most livable conditions for the season. Aside from Stemorna, Dyspara has a half-dozen tribes that live in permanent cities. These tribes welcome traveling tribes when they arrive, with the understanding that nomadic tribes will not reside in a city for more than a month at a time due to the strain that this puts on the city’s resources. Most Dysparans understand and respect the rules set forth by permanent cities and especially by Stemornan lawmakers.


Mercantile and Trade. Dyspara exports semi-precious rocks and salt, which they trade with Ahten in exchange for foodstuffs. They also export sand to nations that wish to press sand bricks or make adobe structures. Stemorna has a large port that accepts international traders, although most other nations prefer to trade with one another and overlook Dyspara due to its lack of luxury goods.


Militant. Dysparan tribes each have a young warrior sect that keep their tribe safe from the fearsome creatures of the desert and from belligerent tribes. Dysparan warriors tend to be skilled at navigating desert terrain, but fight with weapons that other nations might consider primitive.


Religious. As Dysparans can always see an abundance of water but never access it, their religious philosophies wrestle with the problem of scarcity and the question of why good people aren’t rewarded with good things. They largely arrive at the conclusion that the gods only care about themselves, leading them to other philosophical questions that Dysparan elders and monks often debate and teach.


From the scant little I’ve heard about Dyspara, it’s even worse off than Falth.

-Charlie


Underworld. Dyspara has no organized crime, but at times an inexperienced tribal elder might see fit to wage war on another, not realizing that this is far less effective than maintaining a peace.

STEMORNA

By any other nation’s standards, Stemorna is a small and struggling city. By Dyspara’s standards, it is a thriving metropolis. Stemorna has a small academy and a respectable port that can host international deep-sea vessels. It also houses the congressional halls of Dyspara’s representatives sent from the tribal elders. Stemorna’s governor, and the facilitator of inter-tribal deliberation, is an older dwarven fellow named Jethro Sandbeard, beloved for his jovial nature and empathetic demeanor.

POINTS OF INTEREST

The Water Cube. Far above the Dysparan tribes, a miles-wide cube of water warbles above the parched earth. On occasion, a stray fish will swim too low and drop out of the cube, plummeting to the earth. Sadly, the water cube absorbs all moisture from the nearby atmosphere, so it virtually never rains anywhere in Dyspara.

  • A Dysparan mystic thinks that they can draw the waters of the Cube to the ground by using a portal, but they need to create a connection to the Everwilds to do so. The mystic hires the heroes for security on their journey into the Everwilds.

ERYNDOR

Eryndor stands as a bulwark between the horrors of a haunted forest and the rest of the nations of Gathandia. Eryndor’s military maintains a massive wall that runs from north to south. East of the wall, Nachtskog - the Black Forest - spawns all manner of nightmarish monsters, which Eryndor’s valiant knights turn away time and again.


Eryndor’s climate includes mild summers and snowy, long winters. While the nation is reasonably prosperous, Roshallan has made no effort to conquer Eryndor, for it serves as a buffer against the haunts of Nachtskog.


Academic. Brightguard has a sizable university, and several of Eryndor’s notable towns and smaller cities have formal academies as well. Most small towns have a local teacher who tutors children, but formal education is not structured as rigorously as it is in larger towns or in Brightguard. Eryndor readily accepts international scholars to reside at its schools when such opportunities arise.


Aristocratic and Political. Eryndor uses a republic as its governing system. A chancellor is elected by representatives who must vote in accordance with the wishes of their constituency. The chancellor then works with legislators from each of Eryndor’s districts to pass laws and maintain social order. Chancellors rule for eight years at a time and can be reelected. Eryndor’s current chancellor, an earth elemental named Bismarck “the Boulder” Densworth, is in his fifth term as Chancellor of the nation. While he lacks charisma, he is a decisive military leader and has kept the Great Wall very well maintained.


Mercantile and Trade. Eryndor generally does not concern itself with international trade, making an exception for Ruptaea as a reliable overland neighbor that has not exhibited aggression towards Eryndor or other nations. Eryndor’s forests and pastures produce bountiful crops, livestock, and timber.


Militant. Eryndor has a large and disciplined military. Some of the military occupies the southern border to guard against aggression from Roshallan, while most of the rest maintain the Great Wall to Eryndor’s northeast. This wall predates the nation, built in a previous supereon, but the Eryndorans obsessively maintain the wall. Nightmarish monsters from Nachtskog lead regular, vicious attacks against the fortification with utmost hostility to the country’s citizens.


Eryndor’s military often coordinates with Skavr’s, as the two nations both suffer from the monstrous attacks that come from the haunted woods.


The Knights of Eryndor are the military elite. Skillful both on the battlefield and as rangers in the woodlands, the Knights train rigorously and are inspirational to all who fight beside them.


Religious. Viewing themselves as the last line of defense against the horrors of Nachtskog, Eryndorans view themselves with a sense of holy pride. Their patron goddess, Rasheen, is believed to prowl the planes of existence to vanquish burgeoning threats. The Eryndorans often lead military expeditions into the woods near Nachtskog, which takes on religious significance for many soldiers and their families.


Social. Most Eryndorans are earnest, duty-oriented folk. From farmers to soldiers to politicians, the average Eryndoran views their job with a seriousness that other nationalities might find excessive. To the Eryndorans, each person offers an important contribution to the fight against the horrors of Nachtskog.


Underworld. While most Eryndorans view their place in the world as valiant knights against the forces of darkness, there are some who - for any number of reasons - are disillusioned with Eryndor or the world at large. These folk often form secretive cults that declare the glory of the monsters from Nachtskog and try to undermine the defenses of the Great Wall.

BRIGHTGUARD

Eryndor’s shining capital city, Brightguard is a thriving port city built on the mouth of the Eryndor River. A sizable port allows for trade with other nations in the Inner Sea, though Eryndor’s merchant vessels are not heavily armed enough to safely sail into Renden waters.


Brightguard’s Republic Palace is a large building made of fortified stone and military buildings on the outside and plush, luxurious trappings deep in the interior complex of buildings. From this building, the chancellor guides the nation.

POINTS OF INTEREST

The Great Wall. Nobody has yet discovered who built the Great Wall, but Eryndoran soldiers maintain it religiously.


They believe that one day the wall will fall, heralding the end times, and the Knights of Eryndor must be ready to raise their swords one final time when this day comes.


Sounds like they need me up there at the Wall.

-Grim


Nachtskog. Stretching for scores of miles beyond Eryndor’s wall, Nachtskog is a swathe of blighted and haunted woodlands. Home to hag covens, fiends that made it through portals from Inferno, and all manner of horrifying creatures, Nachtskog is a deadly place and its savage progeny often rush forth to attack the Great Wall. With gnarled branches black against the sky, Nachtskog’s World-Tree is a twisted and hideous facsimile of the lifegiving world-trees found in other nations. Like the World-Trees in Muskshir and on the border of Sozein and Tafunga, this World-Tree’s roots connect back to the original World-Tree: Starborea in Doshairiel.


I have been to Nachtskog, yes. My teeth still crave the flesh of the beasts therein.

-Hanniah

FALTH

A federation of large cities sprawling from the lowest, most navigable point in the Cloudpiercer Range to the coast of the Inner Sea, the vast majority of Falth’s cities are run by mafiosos and criminal kingpins.


Falth generally shares the natural climate of southern Doshairiel: deciduous forests, humid summers, and rainy winters. Falth is rockier and hillier than most of Doshairiel, and the easternmost reaches of Falth - through the Cloudpiercer Pass - are scrubby desertland that receive little rain year on year.


Academic. Many of Falth’s cities fund universities or academies. The crime lords that run the cities typically believe that an educated citizenry will lead to more economic prosperity, even if it does come with potential threats to their power.


Aristocratic and Political. Each of Falth’s cities are run autonomously by crime families. The larger families, along with those that have intermarried, control two or more cities that almost function as miniature nations within the Falthic federation. Falth is primarily held into cohesion due to shared cultural history and the mob bosses’ mutual respect for one another. While one mafioso would order another one dead without hesitation, that does not mean that they do not admire each other’s clever political machinations and see one another as respected rivals.


Everyone reports to someone. Even someone as feared as my uncle still has his superiors that he has to keep happy.

-Avery


Mercantile and Trade. Falth’s rocky foothills near the Cloudpiercer Mountains produce a healthy amount of precious metals and gems. Farther to the west, the forested regions of Falth offer lumber and plant and animal byproducts. Fiercely capitalistic crime lords will compete with one another to sell their goods on the international black market.


Given Falth’s central location in the middle of Gathandia, many merchants find it difficult to get from the east to the west and back without traveling through Falth. Most mob bosses allow safe passage through their towns in exchange for hefty tolls.


Militant. Each crime lord maintains a private army of thugs, spies, and assassins. Those that prove incompetent are quickly disposed of, and even common grunts know that they play a dangerous game. Most crime lords put a good deal of resources towards buying out the thugs and spies that work for their rivals. If the price is right, and a spy becomes a double agent, it can be lucrative, but at the risk of death if they are discovered.


Religious. While some Falthic commoners might hold religious beliefs, it is uncommon for anyone in Falth to act in obviously moral fashion. Those who do refrain from stealing, grifting, or conning are usually left behind. A few mafiosos practice religion, though it is hard to tell whether this is borne of true conviction or a desire to project an appearance of moral superiority.


Social and Underworld. Needless to say, even the lowliest Falthic commoner will not go out of their way to do favors for anyone unless something is in it for them. An act of pure, uncalculated altruism is as rare as a five-moon eclipse in this selfish land.


The one notable exception is the Gray Hoods. Led by former members of the Hoods syndicate in Thunderhead Vale, the Gray Hoods use all of the unscrupulous methods of typical Falthic mobs, but they use these methods in an effort to build a better society. They steal from crime lords and give money to the needy, trying to foster a sense of goodwill among the common folk. When possible, they leap at the opportunity to assassinate a corrupt leader and try to replace them with one that has at least some measure of scruples.

DIRGE

Felson Weskh, a cunning polar urkou, is widely regarded as the most powerful and influential crime lord in all of Falth. His city, Dirge, is the largest in Falth and straddles both a lucrative mine and the main road that traverses the Cloudpiercer Pass. Dirge is miles wide and also covers many rocky hillsides that stretch west of the Cloudpiercer foothills. Felson deviously manipulates the lesser crime lords, ensuring that they fight one another rather than challenging his authority over this vital region. He has designs on uniting some of the least ambitious crime lords into an organized nation, from which he will springboard his conquest of the surrounding nations, beginning with Nalathesh.


If Felson has his way, the world will be his plaything, and we will all be little more than slaves. No matter how many of my bones get broken along the way, I will fight until I’m sure that such a day will never come.

-Charlie

POINTS OF INTEREST

Cloudpiercer Pass. The Cloudpiercer Pass consists of a several-dozen mile stretch of the Cloudpiercer Range that is no taller and no rockier than large foothills. This pass has a score of switchback paths, tunnels, and bridges that merchants can use to easily pass the Cloudpiercers. The only catch, of course, is Falth’s notorious road tolls.

  • Merchants from another nation (Nalathesh, Doshairiel, or Anedonia would make sense) ask the heroes to protect them while they travel through Falth’s crime-ridden cities.
  • The Gray Hoods have formed a plan to take down Felson once and for all, but they need the heroes’ help to perform acts of espionage, assassination, and reconnaissance.

FJORDENHAVEN

If not for the volcanic activity, Fjordenhaven would not likely exist. Sulfurous hot springs and lava flows provide warmth enough to maintain a boreal climate despite the scouring, frigid winds that sweep down from the north pole.


Fjordenhaven developed along these hot springs, making great use of the heat to produce both agriculture and energy for industry. The hot spots beneath the surface of the earth extend up a chain of craggy islands, and Fjordenhaven has inhabited all of these islands capable of supporting vegetation.


Fjordenhaven’s architecture is simple, with stone foundations and walls and steep wood-shingled roofs. They commonly decorate their entryways with scrimshaw carvings or decorative caribou antler displays.


Academic. Fjordenhaven’s capital has a large library with conference rooms and study halls, but does not have formal education. Mystics and historians pass on their wisdom orally, traveling from town to town sharing their knowledge.


Aristocratic and Political. Fjordenhaven is a traditional monarchy, the heirship passing from eldest child to eldest child. The younger siblings of the monarch are assigned governing positions over various regions, towns, or industries. Its current monarch is King Alfur Svansson.


Mercantile and Trade. Fjordenhaven has a surprising number of wares to offer in international trade. They have a large shepherding community and industrial textile mills that process wool into fine cloth. They also mine valuable minerals from the volcanic mountains.


Fjordenhaven maintains a few large caravels that make long voyages down Gathandia’s west coast, trading with Ruptaea, Vaenyth, and Pash.


Militant. Fjordenhaven’s folk are hardened by the adversity of long, cold winters. The Fjordenhavian military is small but strong, and they maintain several fortified outposts along the mainland’s coast and their islands.


Religious. Like many northern folk, the Fjordenhavian are fascinated by the northern lights. They believe it to be a manifestation of the Celestial Council’s will, and believe that when it appears in the sky, its colors and the sequence in which they appear foretell different events to come. Fjordenhavian priests interpret these signs, and the monarchy usually acts based on these predictions.


Social. Fjordenhavians punish crime with swift and decisive harshness. A thief might have their hand chopped off and exiled from their town. If they are given no reason to be suspicious, however, the common folk are usually very warm to visitors from other towns and other nations.


It is said that Fjordenhaven’s geysers and hot springs are rich with medicinal minerals. Those who bathe in the waters emerge cleansed of any skin deformities and even some diseases. I would love to witness this myself one day.

-Finneas


Underworld. There is little crime to speak of. Would-be thieves usually recognize that being caught and ostracized from society is too steep a price to pay in the harsh northlands.

SKEVIK

Skevik, the capital of the nation, is just north of the eastern river. Spread along the riverbank and the coast leading out to the Ice Sea, Skevik is the largest port city in Fjordenhaven. In addition to the library and the castle of the monarchy, Skevik also has a grand chapel with a beautiful pipe organ that is almost always in use, spinning its haunting melodies through the frigid air.

POINTS OF INTEREST

The Frostvale. A wide valley between two icy mountain ranges, the Frostvale is a massive graveyard. Divided into a miles-wide plot of land for the common folk, a sizeable plot for warriors that fell in battle, and cavernous mausoleums for deceased monarchs, Fjordenhavians visit the Frostvale when they wish to pay respects to any of their ancestors. Rumor has it that the current living monarch is capable of calling all of the deceased up from the ground. Their bodies, preserved in the frozen earth, will stand ready to defend Fjordenhaven when the end of the world draws near.


The Ice Chapel. Chiseled out of the glacier facing north from the end of the western mountain range bordering the Frostvale, the Ice Chapel is considered a holy observatory from which Fjordenhavians can pay tribute to the Celestial Council. The acoustics in the glacial chapel make singing take on a wondrous, ethereal quality, and the ice is so thin on the walls high above that observers can see through it to observe the stars and the aurora.

  • Pilgrims to the Frostvale have not returned to their town and are several weeks overdue. Can the heroes journey into the haunted valley and find them?
  • A volcanic eruption has disrupted a local town’s economy and fire elementals have beset the poor townsfolk and shepherds. The heroes must defeat these elementals and prevent lava flows from destroying the town.
  • The Aurora has prophesied that a horrible monster of winter and death will assail Fjordenhaven on the winter solstice. Can the heroes fortify the towns in time, and can they defeat the monster when it arises?

FORSTEN

Forsten exists almost entirely within the Old Forest. With mild summers and cold winters that often bring snow, the folk of Forsten are a tough lot. Most treat the forest with deep respect and a little fear.


Once a single kingdom along with Constel and Kendaria, Forsten has only seceded from Constel within the past couple generations. The oldest folk of Forsten would remember the days when it was the northern half of a larger kingdom, though virtually none living today would recall it being one with Kendaria, which split away after a civil war several hundred years hence.


Academic. Most Forsteners place more stock in one’s ability to chop timber or skin game than in their ability to recite poetry. Formal education is uncommon, but entertainers do hold a special place in society as they spin fanciful tales long after the sun has set.


Aristocratic and Political. Forsten is a troubled nation. Having only been its own autonomous country for a few score years, many burgomasters of middling towns feel that they are qualified to manage the entire nation. Although the young dwarf Janice Magillory is nominally the queen of Forsten, she commands little respect and has been unable to cajole other political figures into following her leadership.


Mercantile and Trade. Although its relationship with Constel is still quite tender, Forsten maintains a trade relationship with its neighbor. Many Forsten merchants are also sailors and trade with Kendaria to the south and Derk to the north. Brusch Clydesden, a renowned urkou explorer in his early life and skilled long-distance trader now that he is getting on in years, will occasionally guide a merchant vessel as far north as Yodvan.


Forsten primarily exports timber, although some of the locals fear that deforestation will anger the ancient gods of the Old Forest, as they believe happened in Constel.


Militant. Forsteners tend to be a strong, sturdy lot and make competent fighters. Many hunters, lumberjacks, fishers, and long-distance merchants have had to fight off numerous monstrosities from the untamed stretches of the Old Forest. Janice Magillory wants to establish a national army, but has been unable to rally support for such a movement.


Religious. Forsteners are quite spiritual and often superstitious. Many believe that small actions will have unforeseen consequences and worry about the wrath of the old gods. Most will pray to multiple pantheons, but especially the Shibmar Conclave of the Archfae.


Social. Forsten is not a particularly hospitable region. Fear of the forest has led many to treat strangers with suspicion or outright hostility, even if the strangers are just from another town in Forsten.


Underworld. Due to Janice Magillory’s utter failure to unite her country, crime syndicates flourish in the underbellies of all major cities in Forsten. Smugglers traffic weapons, gems, and drugs in and out of their nation, and mercenaries will take any protection or assassination job for the right price.


It’s sad how Forsten has crumbled. While it’s almost the same country as Kendaria, it hasn’t prospered in the same way.

-Flanagan

WOLDEN

Overlooking a port city at the base of the craggy cliffs, Wolden is an expansive fortress that could house a small army and enough merchants and craftsfolk to keep the army well-supplied and entertained. Built of stone and wood, Wolden is an impressive fortress and besieging it would certainly be a protracted affair, especially given that it can easily access both the land and sea with roads that are well-maintained within a day’s ride of the fortress.

POINTS OF INTEREST

Dreamhaze Mountains. See Constel for more information. The folk of Forsten are generally wary of going anywhere near these strange crests.


The Old Forest. See Constel for more information. Forsteners get most of their food and raw materials from the forest, but they also worry that they will awaken the forest’s wrath.

  • Janice Magillory asks the heroes to help her bring the mayors and town spokespeople into line so that she can unite the country.
  • An entire town’s worth of people is missing, so a worried mayor of a neighboring town calls on the heroes to investigate.
  • Brusch Clydesden was last seen heading towards the Dreamhaze Mountains with a strange, haunted look in his eye. He hasn’t been heard from in over a month.

GAVARE

A land riddled with natural disasters and monsters, Gavare’s hostile environment has bred a resilient and warlike population. Gavare straddles the southern Cloudpiercer Range - where the mountains are considerably easier to navigate - and occupies the land around most of the Smoldering Canyon. Common natural disasters in this land include sulfurous gas spraying into the air from the canyon, sandstorms whipping up in the desert, rockslides from the mountains, earthquakes thanks to a large fault line, and monsoons that batter the Gavarese states east of the Cloudpiercers.


Gavarese folk have traditionally believed that engaging in frequent ritual warfare with one another will appease the gods responsible for these natural disasters. Many are ready to move on from such beliefs, but the monarchs and high priests are reluctant to abandon tradition.


Academic. Scholarly arcanists in Gavare are fascinated by the Smoldering Canyon and are trying to convince the ruling elite to abolish the ritual wars and harness the magical energy within the Smoldering Canyon. This social transition has thus far been unsuccessful.


Aristocratic and Political. Gavare has eleven major states. Each is ruled by a monarch. Some such monarchs are also the high priests of their state, while other states separate the responsibilities of governance and religious leadership. These monarchs frequently negotiate trade agreements with one another and if threatened by a foreign entity will rally together.


Mercantile and Trade. At the behest of arcanist scholars in the nation, some Gavarese tribes have begun to mine the Smoldering Canyon for its precious metals. They have also established an ongoing trade relationship with Ahten, who pays high premiums to mine the Canyon in Gavare’s territory. Gavare and Thaaj trade frequently, especially the states of Gavare that can offer timber from east of the Cloudpiercers.


Militant, Religious, and Social. The nine states on the west of the Cloudpiercers, occupying the land around the Smoldering Canyon, engage in ritual warfare with one another. This is done to appease their destructive pantheon of gods, whom they believe destroy the world every hundred years. Given Gavare’s high volume of natural disasters, the Gavarese see these disasters as acts from the divine.


The two states that live to the east of the Cloudpiercers share the same religious beliefs, but this manifests in battle against the monstrosities that lurk in the Kozia Rainforest.


Most Gavarese worship gods out of fear, rather than gratitude. The Ashyidir Conclave of the Archfae, the Corrupted Avadri, the Fiends of Inferno, and the Exiles are commonly worshiped as they seem most likely to be responsible for frequent natural disasters.


These folk almost have the right of it, I’d say. The gods are cruel and callous things, but while the Gavarese sacrifice themselves on behalf of their gods, I say, to hell with ‘em all. Just take magical power for yourself and protect the folk you care about.

-Dungrel


Underworld. Gavarese crime syndicates have leapt at the opportunity to mine precious resources under the direction of scholars. They have a near-monopoly on mithril and adamantine mining in the Smoldering Canyon.

TLEZCAN

The largest state in Gavare is located in the Cloudpiercer Range and extends west into the desert near the Smoldering Canyon. The capital of this state, Tlezcan, is constructed on and around a large lake in the Cloudpiercer Range. It is accepted as the capital of the whole nation, as each other Gavarese monarch looks to the Tlezcani monarch as the first among equals. The current monarch of Tlezcan is King Mactezuma III.

POINTS OF INTEREST

Smoldering Canyon. Gavare controls most of the Smoldering Canyon, which produces vast quantities of valuable metals and gems. Among mundane precious metals, the canyon also produces magical metals such as mithril and adamantine. The Smoldering Canyon also produces magical energy that scholars are working to harness, and it yields plants whose pigments can be used to create magical tattoo ink.

  • The crime syndicate in the Smoldering Canyon has choked out all supply of mithril and adamantine, swearing that they will only reopen the mines once they are made monarchs.
  • Great monsters in the forests to the east of the Cloudpiercers threaten small Gavarese towns. Can the heroes fight off the monsters?

  • A series of multiple natural disasters batter Gavare, including one that rips a portal from the Smoldering Canyon to Inferno. The heroes have their hands full fighting fiends and must figure out how to close the portal amid the other natural disasters.

GETAR

Getar is an island nation with a strange geological makeup. The nation is three concentric ring-shaped islands: the smallest island, directly in the middle, is entirely industrialized and is home of their capital. The middle island forms a ring around the inner island, and likewise, the outer island forms a ring around the other two. Although there are natural variations in the islands’ coastal shapes, the overall appearance of the three islands is that of two rings, one larger and one smaller, both enveloping a dot.


Most Getarese feel that this geological makeup would have been impossible if not for the involvement of some kind of remarkably advanced gods and they worship the technology-driven deities known as the Avadri.


Getar used to be a powerful nation in the Inner Sea, but after Roshallan defeated Chee and commandeered its navy, they swept across the Inner Sea and smashed Getar’s military. They have let Getar maintain its theocratic way of governance in public, but in private, the theocrats are controlled by Roshallani militants.


Their geological structure is endlessly fascinating. Perhaps they have the right idea? Maybe Ancerra really was created by some godly force.

-Finneas


Academic. Most Getarese receive a mixed education. Cultivation of intelligence is highly valued in Getarese culture, so students usually learn a mixture of language skills, math, and history, but the focus is usually placed on science. Having discovered two strange minerals - Avadrium and Arcanium - in their shallow lakes, the Getarese enthusiastically pursue experimentation with these materials.


Political and Religious. Getar is a theocratic society. From the capital city of Pount, the Theocrats purportedly receive cryptic visions from the Avadri beyond and translate these mysteries into actionable policy. Citizens of Getar are expected to comply with these proclamations as both political and religious truth. In fact, now that Getar is controlled by Roshallan, the Theocrats simply do what Roshallani commanders order them to do.


Mercantile and Trade. Under the Roshallani Empire, Getar has become a valuable mining nation for the greater empire. They export their Avadrium and Arcanium to Roshallan’s mainland in exchange for undervalued compensation and the ongoing right to live on their own land.


Militant and Underworld. Weakened rebel forces engage in guerilla warfare against Roshallan, but to little avail. A network of Getarese thieves and saboteurs coordinate with the rebels of Chee in an effort to bring down the Roshallani.


Social. Before Roshallan’s occupation, most Getarese saw one another through a vague caste lens. The citizens of the outermost ring were considered the peasantry, and those on the innermost ring the nobles. Now that Roshallan has conquered Geter, most Getarese citizens see one another as victimized siblings and try to look out for their neighbors when able.

POUNT

The innermost island of Getar, its capital city, is home to the great temple of the Theocracy. This temple functions as both a religious center and a legislative hall.


Pount also has large industrial facilities where Avadrium and Arcanium are systematically processed and purified before being exported by the Roshallani navy. Well-equipped research facilities on the island are also staffed by renowned alchemists looking for new applications of these materials.

  • The Getarese want to recruit the heroes to help them covertly battle the Roshallani. Will the heroes join the side of the beleaguered rebels?
  • The Theocracy and/or leading scholars from the Getarese want the heroes to conduct some experiments with an Avadrium-Arcanium alloy. The resulting reaction teleports the heroes into the Celestial Sea, with no obvious way back…

HADLA

A quiet, uneventful, and unremarkable nation, Hadla can be the perfect place for retired heroes to spend the remainder of their years in undisturbed peace.


With steamy, rainy summers and damp, rainy winters, Hadla’s fertile soil can produce crops virtually year-round. The jungles that occupy its southwest can be dangerous, so younger would-be adventurers looking to make a name for themselves often head into the jungle looking for trouble. The rest of the nation, where most of its population resides, is quite peaceful and little ever seems to happen.


Academic. Bundimor has a large library, and most of the major cities and towns in Hadla have some form of library or school center. There are no major universities or academies in Hadla, so most scholarly information is passed down orally or ignored as unimportant.


Aristocratic and Political. Hadla operates on a traditional feudal model. The monarch delegates certain regions to the dukes and duchesses, who divide their regions into vassal states run by counts and settlements overseen by barons. Hadla’s current monarch is Queen Magya VI, who commands respect from the other nobles and of whom the common folk hold almost no opinion, positive or negative.


Mercantile and Trade. There are few natural resources of much worth in the nation aside from some pigment dyes that can be extracted from plants in the jungle. Capable of sustaining itself on its own food supply, Hadla’s economy primarily functions from inter-settlement trade, but some merchant caravans will occasionally journey north across a small mountain range to trade with Nalathesh. Others will descend into the Great Chasm to trade with Vadan and Unjaarn. Trade with Tafunga is very rare due to the undeveloped nature of the Hadlan jungle. There are no well-maintained roads and only a few outpost towns in this region.


Militant. As is traditionally the case of feudal nations, there is not a nationalized military. Instead, each noble has a small private militia that they can levy on their own behalf to quell bandits, or on behalf of their superiors when the dukes or monarchs fear that a neighboring nation might take interest in invading Hadla. All of the combined levied soldiers of Hadla would be unlikely to stave off an invasion, but none of Hadla’s neighboring nations have much interest in conquering them as they have little in the way of valuable resources.


Religious. Hadlans commonly worship the Celestial Council and the Stravon Conclave of the Archfae. Many Hadlans hold the belief that it can’t hurt to try to appease every deity, so many households have whittled likenesses of dozens of deities scattered throughout their houses and on their mantles.


Social. Hadlan society usually has some minor holiday coming up. With a focus on cycles of emotion, cycles of the seasons, and the cycle of life and death, every few weeks there will be a festival of one kind or another celebrating these states of existence.


I visited Hadla once. Didn’t like it. Too peaceful. Nothing exciting ever happened.

-Hanniah


Underworld. Many of the nobles in the feudal model want to expand their own importance and influence, and most are willing to employ underhanded means to achieve this. Hiring scoundrels to plant incriminating evidence on a rival noble is all too commonplace.

BUNDIMOR

The capital of Hadla overlooks the second-largest of the nation’s lakes. The monarch’s palace is a traditional stone castle with a large enough public park inside its courtyard that all of the capital city’s residents could live there in times of siege. Other than this impressive castle, Bundimor is just a larger version of any other Hadlan town: wooden homes, shingled or thatched rooftops, livestock and humanoids traversing the same muddy streets and working the same damp fields.

POINTS OF INTEREST

The Great Chasm. Hadla’s entire southeast border overlooks the Great Chasm. See Bridgemoor for more information.


Kozia Jungle. The Kozia Jungle is home to two full nations along with part of two other nations, including part of Hadla. The Kozia Jungle is vast and supports huge biodiversity, offering plenty of lethal challenges to young adventurers.

  • The heroes might be young upstart adventurers looking to test their mettle in the jungle.
  • The heroes are tasked with guiding a merchant caravan to Nalathesh, Unjaarn, or even Tafunga or Valstrom.
  • The king of sixty years has died without leaving an heir. Various nobles throughout the nation vie for the throne.

HUSK

By all mundane metrics, Husk should not exist as a nation. Its desert landscape is predominantly rocks and pebbles and there are no noteworthy areas that could function as arable farmland. In fact, chunks of pebbly landscape have been known to spontaneously levitate for a time before showering down on whomever is so unlucky as to be standing beneath.


And this unexplainable phenomenon is what gives Husk its appeal. When prospective settlers traversed across this strange and hostile land, they were fascinated by the magical phenomena that they witnessed. Defying logic, they built a city-state in the most stable land of the region and began studying these strange events. The formative years of Husk were brutal and deadly, but Husk now thrives as a city-state that attracts scholars and adventurers the world over.


Academic. Husk boasts a large university dedicated specifically to the study of magic and how the different Sources of Magic interact with one another. It was at the Husk Institute that a scholarly Bhrunai named Zitvin discovered that certain Sources of Magic, when applied in equal force against one another, trump each other like a five-way game of Rock, Paper, Scissors: each Source is stronger than two others, and weaker than two others.


Aristocratic and Political. Although it was originally a small community of a few curious researchers, as generations progressed, the ruthless Licten family took over by force and has ruled Husk ever since. The Lictens purport that they are the chosen of the Lumen Cloud, one of the strange magical phenomena of the area.


When a new Licten is ready to assume the throne of Husk, they journey into the desert to be claimed by the Cloud. Thus far, every Licten who has done so has returned with a mark as though struck by lightning, cementing their belief that they are divinely chosen.


Mercantile and Trade. Husk imports a vast majority of its foodstuffs, water, textiles, and basic raw materials. Capable manufacturers turn these raw materials into finished products, often infusing these items with minor magical properties. Husk was the first nation to commodify magical items and still dominates the international magic item trade. In addition to magic items, Huskan merchants sell bottled ambient magic with unpredictable effects. Careless travelers will be scammed and sold empty glass vials.


Militant. Husk has some of the fiercest infantry fighters in all Gathandia, but the Huskan army is so small that this does not actually mean much. Trained to fight with unique magical weapons, each elite warrior crafts their own magic weapon at the end of their training. While their army is small, it is nonetheless strong enough to dissuade any other nation from invading Husk.


Religious. The Lumen Cloud is the god of the region, and the Licten family is its chosen emissary to sapient creatures. The Lumen Cloud is a great glowing cumulus cloud that appears at random times over the region. When it is visible, normal daily affairs halt and the citizens of Husk praise the Cloud and pray that it bestows its everlasting power upon their nation and their beloved Licten family. The Cloud is especially spectacular to behold at sunset when it catches rays from the setting sun and magnifies its own glowing properties, or at nighttime when it refracts the silver light of the stars.


Social. Because they live in a region that experiences frequent chaotic magical occurrences, the folk of Husk tend to be both cautious and unflappable. Having seen many strange things, they accept each new ripple of magic with almost disinterested stoicism.


Underworld. The Licten family has failed to crush multiple criminal organizations that smuggle magic items and bottled ambient magic out of the region to sell at black market prices to other adventurers and merchants.


The Lictens are as dangerous as they are powerful, and they rival many of the strongest Falthic crime lords in the reach of their influence and their utter absence of morals.

-Avery

POINTS OF INTEREST

The entire region of Husk is a point of power where arcane, primal, and psionic magic swirl together with unpredictable results. Sometimes this causes rocks and pebbles to levitate and rain from the sky; other times it causes the Lumen Cloud to appear. Records show that it has snowed in Husk despite there not being a cloud in the sky, and occasionally portals to other planes will briefly open.

  • The Lictens ask the heroes to root out and crush a burgeoning rebellion.
  • The heroes must explore the grand Husk Institute to find answers to a magical riddle that a Sphinx has asked them.

KANDIFF

The steppes of Kandiff, although quite dry, are nonetheless temperate and a reasonably pleasant environment in which to live. The summers are warm and arid, the winters chilly and often snowy. Kandiff is a land of mediocrity: it is not so mountainous as Betaal to the west, not so fertile as Bishal to the east, and not so magical or wealthy as Husk or Lesmishan to the south. It is, ironically, this very mediocrity that allows Kandiff to thrive: if its ground were as fertile or its mines as valuable as its neighbors, it might have been conquered long ago.


Academic. Most Kandiffese value education but do not have the means to pursue it in any serious way. Local schools will teach children the basics of reading and writing, but few pursue a scholarly career.


Mercantile and Trade. Kandiff’s two largest industries are ranching and mining. Ranchers breed cattle throughout most of Kandiff, though in the western foothills, ranchers also raise herds of sheep and goats. The miners generally live in the east of the nation, extracting minerals from the Great Chasm.


Kandiff’s largest mining export is actually salt, which other nations will pay a high premium to acquire.


Militant. The town guard in each settlement is responsible for keeping the peace and protecting Kandiff’s citizens from any monsters. When needed, ranchers are usually competent auxiliaries to the town guard and are sometimes better fighters in the wilderness than the guards themselves. Although the combined forces of each town and city is not significant compared to neighboring nations, it is generally enough to keep neighboring forces from invading.


Political. Kandiff is a federal oligarchy. Towns and cities are divided into provinces, each of which elect a single representative. Together, there are nine representatives that serve as the Oligarchs. They pass national and regional legislation and assign executors and judges to oversee the laws of the land.


Religious. Given the importance of horses in Kandiffese culture, most folk worship the archfae A’rl’ng, god of equine beasts, as the patron of Kandiff. Horses and other equine creatures are treated with great respect. Many households have horseshoes nailed over their doorframes. Many also worship Immort, the turtle god, out of respect for their living capital city.


Social. Many try to embody the spiritual aspects of A’rl’ng and Immort in their daily lives. Both deities profess a philosophy of not worrying too much about the trials of life. A’rl’ng champions throwing off the shackles of strife, while Immort offers the assurance that one day things will sort themselves out. Kandiffese towns are quite hospitable and have festivals with the change of each season.


Underworld. Bandits live in the steppes between towns, and occasionally bandit groups will join together to pose a real threat to a city, but the guards and ranchers have thus far been able to quell such bandit uprisings.

APAKKU

The only truly remarkable aspect of Kandiff is its capital city. Apakku is an ancient alligator snapping turtle who has grown to be the size of a small mountain. Having grown up in the dusty streams of northern Kandiff, the turtle observed the humanoids in her region and grew to love their homey attitudes. As she grew to be the size of a horse, then of a house, her fame spread throughout Kandiff. She traveled from province to province, contentedly observing children at play and ranchers and miners hard at work.


Finally, when Apakku grew old enough to be the size of a large hill, she telepathically commanded the Kandiffese to rebuild their oligarchic capital on her shell. For a hundred years now, she has continued to grow, and Kandiff’s capital city has grown with Apakku as her shell accommodates more and more buildings. She travels across Kandiff from north to south and from east to west three times annually, bringing the Oligarchy with her to ensure that all in their nation are well.


A whole city built on the back of a turtle? Sounds like my new home. I wouldn’t mind spending a few months there each year, especially if the turtle is talkative.

-Lyla

POINTS OF INTEREST

Apakku, the Living City. The capital city of Kandiff draws much attention from international scholars and adventurers. Aside from being built on the back of a still-growing immortal turtle, the city itself is unremarkable.


The Great Chasm. See Bridgemoor for more details. Kandiff’s mines primarily extract salt from the Great Chasm.

  • Miners are lost in the Great Chasm, and the heroes have been hired to help.
  • A bandit king has declared himself the new ruler of Kandiff. The Oligarchy calls on the heroes to quell this new menace.
  • Town guards from the north need help shoring up their defenses against raids from Noord.
  • The heroes have heard that a cult plans on sacrificing Apakku. They take it upon themselves to intervene.

KENDARIA

A monarchic queendom with very old roots, Kendaria is generally viewed as a peaceful nation. An old forest sprawls across most of the nation, with towns and hamlets nestled in and around the forest’s hills and dales.


Kendaria occupies a temperate climate with warm, rainy summers and mild winters. The mountain range to Kendaria’s west will usually get snow caps, and while snow will occasionally fall in the Old Forest, it almost never sticks for more than a day.


This tranquil appearance hides fresh wounds and old superstitions, however. Queen Astaria Gillian III, whose family has ruled for several hundred years, only recently raised the nation out of a destructive civil war between three of her vassals. With a just and cunning reputation, Queen Astaria maintains popular support among most of her citizens, although there are whispers that she was only able to maintain her hold over Kendaria thanks to the help of a dark eldritch beast.


The Old Forest and the southern steppes hold some of the same undertones of menace. Although the forest and plains are both picturesque and monster sightings are uncommon, superstition abounds in both regions, and numerous tales of inexplicable activity fuel these superstitions.


Academic. Kendaria is primarily an agrarian society, so formal education is not common. There will usually be one or two scholarly folk in a given settlement, who happily teach children reading, history, and arithmetic when they aren’t helping their parents in the fields or shops, but literacy is not particularly high in smaller towns. In the handful of proper cities throughout Kendaria, formalized schooling is more common.


Aristocratic and Political. Kendaria is a monarchic feudal nation implementing maternal primogeniture. As each eldest daughter of the Gillian house comes of age, her mother prepares her to become the next queen.


Landed gentry serve as dukes or duchesses over multiple towns, while each settlement is managed by a baron or baroness. Dukes and duchesses report to the queen’s court and serve as intermediaries between the monarch and the towns that they oversee.


Mercantile and Trade. Many of Kendaria’s internal roads between its towns - especially those networking the Old Forest - have fallen into a state of disrepair. This has proven a great challenge for many of the smaller hamlets that rely on inter-settlement trade to shore up their economies.


Kendaria maintains land trade with its closest neighbors, Constel to the north and the city-state of Bridgemoor to the west. In Bridgemoor, Kendarian merchants also do business with Nalatheshi traders regularly. By sea, Kendaria keeps trade arrangements with Forsten and Derk up the northern coast and will occasionally trade with Nenva, although


Queen Astaria believes that spies from Nenva were responsible for starting the civil war that so damaged her country.


Militant. In the wake of the civil war, Queen Astaria has begun to restructure Kendaria’s military: no longer do the gentry have the right to levy soldiers themselves, but instead they must entreat the monarchy to lend out some of its standing army in times of crisis. Queen Astaria’s top general, Ser Grendor, rigorously trains lieutenants to lead soldiers on both land and sea. While competent, the Kendarian national army is still quite small.


Religious. As Kendarians tend to be superstitious, many are also very religious. Kendarian society relies heavily on the Old Forest for timber, fruits, and game, so Kendarian religious practices typically involve veneration of the Pashak Conclave of the Archfae and occasionally include prayers to the Celestial Council. On days or weeks of festivity, food offerings will often be burned in an effort to please the Pashak Conclave. As the Pashak represent vegetative life, the planting season in early spring is especially sacred to Kendarians.


Growing up in a Kendarian meadow was a delight, and the Old Forest is rich with many stories to tell!

-Flanagan


Social. Whispered conspiracies aside, Kendarians are usually a hospitable lot. As most settlements in the nation are on the smaller side, each person in a community usually knows most of their neighbors. When someone’s roof leaks after a summer thunderstorm, the community will often take time out of their day to pitch in and fix up the unlucky family’s rooftop. Kendarians view strangers and travelers with a mix of suspicion and rapt fascination.


Underworld. Organized crime exists within the capital city and a couple of the other major cities, but these are isolated gangs that stake their claim over a few alleyways and marketplaces. The smaller communities have low crime rates and harsh punishments for thieves and miscreants.

DALEWRIGHT

The capital of Kendaria, Dalewright nestles in and around a couple rolling dales that drop into the eastern sea. The queen’s palace stands proud on the highest hill in the urban sprawl, and large marketplaces, zigzagging streets lined with townhomes, and theatres comprise most of the city’s makeup. The western neighborhood, closest to the Old Forest, has a large temple dedicated to the Archfae, and the northern quarter has a small university. The university’s library has many historical books, but its professors of more modern sciences such as alchemy and the theory of magic are a few decades behind the rest of the world.

POINTS OF INTEREST

Dreamhaze Mountains. See Constel for more information. Kendarians generally avoid the mountains due to strange occurrences that have happened there.


The Old Forest. The Old Forest sprawls across most of Kendaria. See Constel for more information.


Southern Steppes. To the south and west of the Old Forest, a grassy plain stretches for dozens of miles before giving way to shallow badlands. The plains bloom gorgeously late each spring thanks to thunderstorms, and turn brown and withered in the fall.


Aside from Dalewright, the larger towns in the southwest badlands are the most prosperous in Kendaria due to the high quantity of trade with Bridgemoor.

QUEST IDEAS

  • The heroes must negotiate with an insubordinate vassal on the queen’s behalf.
  • The heroes must look into the rumors surrounding the queen: does she actually consort with an eldritch being?
  • Rumor has it that cultists have taken up residence in Dalewright. Is this true? Do they work for the queen’s eldritch creature?
  • Several small towns have lost multiple hunters and trappers in recent days. Something foul must lurk in the woods nearby.
  • A bridge has been completely washed away after a heavy storm. The heroes must go repair the bridge so that trade can resume between towns.

KESS

A dwindling shadow of a nation, Kess occupies a strip of land between the eastern mountains and the Great Chasm. It enjoys cloudy but pleasant weather aside from rather cold winters. Sadly, the otherwise enjoyable climate does little to cheer the nation’s absolutely impoverished denizens.


After multiple revolutions scoured the once-forested nation, Palorva rose to power. As a vampire and practitioner of occult magic, her oppressed citizenry have no hope of her eventually dying. While she has grand designs on conquering the entire region around the Great Chasm, her nation’s crumbling economy has thus far prevented her from expanding.


Academic. Palorva ensures that the serfs that work her fields are too overworked and underfed to spend any energy on academic ventures. She has burned hundreds of books and replaced them with her own alternate history books, so those among the merchant caste who are able to read are told a very different historical account praising Palorva as a saintly hero.


Aristocratic and Political. Any person or family with a claim to aristocracy was impaled while still living and then slowly drained of blood. Those who are still alive and think they might have a claim to nobility make absolutely no such proclamations. Palorva’s word is law.


Mercantile and Trade. The merchant caste of Kess is forced to keep set prices for their wares. No market competition is allowed under the watchful eye of Palorva. In order to bring more raw goods into Kess, Palorva is pursuing aggressive diplomatic negotiations with Forsten and Constel.


Militant. Palorva is vigorously training her military so that she can wage war on nearby nations. As of yet, her soldiers are poorly equipped and regardless of their discipline they would be overpowered on the battlefield.


Religious. No religious practice is allowed under Palorva’s regime, and all houses of worship have long since been burned to the ground. In private, many common folk entreat Tovare, Rasheen, and the whole Celestial Council for deliverance.


Social. Kess is a strictly communist society, with Palorva and her secret police ensuring that wealth and resources are distributed with absolute, unyielding equality, household by household based on the number of residents living therein. This leads to untold suffering, and when possible, serfs and merchants will quietly offer one another the goods and services that they need to subsist. If the secret police find out, punishment is swiftly forthcoming.


Underworld. What passes as crime in Kess might pass as common decency in any other nation. In addition to secretly offering one another extra food and clothing as able, some among the serf and merchant caste are also planning a rebellion against Palorva.


She’s not mean. She’s quite charming, actually. Makes you feel at home. And she’s incredibly dangerous.

-Grim

LORVAN

The capital city of Kess is nestled in the craggy foothills of the East Range. The streets of Lorvan are often shrouded in thick fog, which helps Palorva navigate the daylight without fear of retribution.


The capital has nothing in the way of entertainment. Aside from some lodgings, some inns and taverns with regulated prices, and a few shops that are equally regulated, Lorvan has nothing to offer except heartache.

POINTS OF INTEREST

The Great Chasm. Lorvan forces her serfs to constantly work the fields to produce food and mine the Chasm to produce resources that she can trade with other nations. For more information about the Chasm, see Bridgemoor.

QUEST IDEAS

  • Serfs from Kess get word out about their dire situation and request that the heroes help them overthrow Palorva.
  • Palorva hires the heroes to infiltrate Forsten or Constel and assess how she can most easily overthrow them.

LESMISHAN

Lesmishan is a wealthy nation sprawling along the lip of the Great Chasm. Having been founded a couple hundred years ago, it quickly became remarkably wealthy thanks to miners finding many veins of precious metals in the regions of the Great Chasm nearest their town. Lesmishan quickly grew from a single town to a nation of mining communities and trade outposts built precariously along the Great Chasm’s massive cliffs.


Aristocratic and Political. Lesmishan’s mighty banking guild - the Chasm Financing Co. - governs the nation. Each Lesmishanian town has a bank where representatives from the CFC choose a sheriff to keep order in the town and collect taxes for the CFC. The CFC will also loan out assets to people that they deem deserving. Both the bankers themselves and the citizens of any given town engage in wildly corrupt activity, but there are no written laws to enforce, so besides overt violence or resisting the CFC’s taxation, anything goes.


Mercantile and Trade. Mining an abundance of gems, precious metals, and strange technologies, means that Lesmishan has vast trading capital. They trade widely with their neighboring nations and even distant nations in exchange for all manner of luxury goods and raw materials that they cannot naturally produce in their arid land. Lesmishan is especially known for a lightweight but rock-hard metal - called avadrium - that occasionally seems to glow in thin stripes and has been noted to enhance arcane and psionic power.


I need me a reliable merchant to hole up in one o’ them mines and send me some avadrium. I’ll make you somethin’ legendary, you bet your last button I will.

-Ingrid


Militant. Lesmishan has a well-funded standing militia, and most of the CFC hire private guards as well. Most Lesmishanian miners are also capable of combat and are very skilled in the rocky, cavernous territory of their home nation. Furthermore, due to Lesmishan’s tremendous wealth, most aggressors can be bought off for a low price, as earning just a few silver coins is better than risking violent combat in most people’s books.


Religious. Many miners in particular are religious or superstitious in some mix. Untold monsters do lurk in the caverns of the Great Chasm, and most miners will claim that they’ve seen unexplainable things. Many worship the Celestial Council or Avatars of protection and guidance. Others carry charms and trinkets to ward off evil spirits.


Social. Lesmishan has a pretty bleak society. The rich hoard their wealth while the poor explore dangerous caverns in hopes of earning a little coin only to have it exorbitantly taxed by the bankers. Miners will often find that their friendships with one another are their only real solace in an otherwise unforgiving nation.


Underworld. There are likely secretive crime syndicates working in Lesmishan, both operated by Lesmishanians and by foreign operatives such as Nalathesh’s Sandsnakes. Most notably, of course, the bankers themselves operate much like a mafia.

LESMISHAN

The capital of Lesmishan, a city by the same name, is the largest mining town in the nation. As it is the oldest mining outpost, most of the veins of minerals in this particular region have since dried up except for those very deep in the caverns dotting the Chasm’s cliffs. While some mining still takes place there, most of the more shallow mineshafts have turned into black markets and criminal haunts. The city as a whole is wildly lawless and most miners spend far more than they earn in this city. The Chasm Financing Co.’s headquarters is in this city: a large stone bastion with enough cured food and water to survive two years under siege.

POINTS OF INTEREST

The Great Chasm. See Bridgemoor for more information on the Great Chasm. Lesmishanians often descend into the Chasm or build small outpost towns on the Chasm’s cliffs to access their mineshafts more easily.

QUEST IDEAS

  • The CFC needs the heroes to clear out some monsters from a lucrative mineshaft.
  • Miners ask the heroes to help them clear a cave-in so that they can rescue their companions.
  • A banker hires the heroes to undermine, blackmail, or rob another banker.
  • An outside nation asks the heroes to scout out the capital city to see how best to overthrow it.

MUSKSHIR

Muskshir is a loose federation of several tribes that inhabit the stretch of jungle between the Great Chasm and the eastern sea. It has humid springs, monsoon summers that can reliably be predicted, dry falls, and mild, rainy winters.


Some of the tribes in Muskshir are nomadic, moving from region to region with the change of the seasons. Most tribes are more or less stationary. Regardless of their approach, most buildings of any tribe are easy to deconstruct and reassemble elsewhere, which helps the Muskshiri avoid the worst of monsoon flooding. Some tribes build their permanent buildings in the tree canopies.


Academic. Largely uninterested in the pursuits of other nations, Muskshir does not have schools in the sense of buildings with books and tests. Instead, they have a rigorous apprenticeship system, with youth training under hunters, bakers, weavers, and the like to become experts in their crafts from a young age.


Aristocratic and Political. Although each tribe is self-governing, the Muskshiri share a culture and have historically all united into one when they feel their land is under threat. In such times, the leaders of each tribe will negotiate with one another and cast votes on the actions that their nation will pursue.


Tribal leaders are generally hereditary, with the eldest child of the leader becoming the new tribal leader when their parent passes on. Some tribes prefer a democratic election or a contest of strength, such as a wrestling tournament, to determine their next leader.


Mercantile and Trade. Muskshir usually just trades goods and services between Muskshiri tribes rather than with other nations. They produce a wide variety of plant-based goods, including valuable tinctures and salves, so alchemists and physicians pay top coin for Muskshiri herbs when a tribe does decide to trade with foreign merchants.


Militant. Most Mukshiri folk spend their entire lives in the jungle. As such, Muskshiri warriors are extremely competent in jungle warfare but are not very strong outside of familiar territory.


As an artist of carnage myself, I respect how vicious a Muskshiri squad of scouts can be in combat.

-Hanniah


Religious. The tribes of Muskshir generally share the same outlook on religion, venerating the Archfae above any other gods. The particular Archfae will vary tribe by tribe, but the Letspina and Vertumna conclaves tend to be the most worshiped. The exception is Amazonia from the Pashak conclave, who is generally accepted as the patron goddess of all Muskshir.


Social. Muskshiri are usually welcoming of other tribes but suspicious of folk from other nations. At times, tribes will go to war with one another, but these conflicts are quickly resolved through negotiations between the tribal leaders and often are low-lethality.


Underworld. Some Muskshiri tribal leaders are gradually working to undermine their entire nation. These corrupt leaders have been bought off by Vel, which supplies the leaders with wealth and occasionally with magical items. In exchange for these gifts, the leaders try to steer Muskshir in directions that will favor Vel.

POINTS OF INTEREST

World-Tree. An enormous World-Tree grows out of the center of the Muskshir Rainforest. Believed to be an incarnation of Amazonia herself, the Muskshiri revere this tree as sacred. When two or more tribal leaders seek to peacefully resolve a dispute, they do so under the World-Tree’s shade. The leaders hold their councils under the World-Tree as well, believing that Amazonia’s wisdom will guide their negotiations.


If someone were to dig into the earth and follow the impossibly deep roots of this mighty tree, they would discover that it is truthfully an offshoot of the even larger World-Tree in Doshairiel, thousands of miles away.


High in this World-Tree’s branches, climbers might find a portal to the Everwilds.

QUEST IDEAS

  • A merchant caravan approaches the heroes and asks that they guide the caravan through Muskshir safely, negotiating with the locals as they do.
  • Academics hire the heroes to research the World-Tree and see if it is in fact holy in some way.
  • A town that was devastated from an unexpected flood needs help from the heroes in the rebuilding process.
  • The heroes must destroy a portal that a fiend from Sanguineth opened up, or else Gathandia will face a grave threat.

NALATHESH

A hot, arid nation spanning the south of a great desert, Nalathesh is hard for travelers to access and is shrouded in mystery. Traders that do reach the desert kingdom can barter for delicious spices, gilded works of magnificent art, and the finest blown glass this side of the Cloudpiercer Range.


Nalathesh holds many secrets. It is one of the few nations that has floating islands soaring above its more temperate steppes to the south, and none have been able to determine exactly how these mountains of limestone maintain their endless flight. An enormous pit of flesh in the center of the nation draws much curiosity and fear from the outside world, and Nalatheshi scholars and miners work together in a gritty mining town to uncover the Flesh Pit’s secrets.


Nalathesh is an absolute monarchy ruled by an immortal Godking - or so says the ruler. The Godking only ever appears in public wearing a solid gold mask, so hushed speculations hold that the Godking is not one person, but many; the mask must surely be passed down from generation to generation.


On the whole, the Godking rules with a reasonably level hand, and so while taxes may be high most common folk are content with the system as it is.


Academic. Nalathesh tries to import as much academic talent as it can from other nations. Nalatheshi scholars eagerly learn from anyone who has news of the outside world or who might share new alchemical practices. The scholarly among Nalathesh are all part of a national guild dedicated to research, experimentation, and archiving of any magical item or creature that they can uncover. Nalatheshi scholars often live in developing towns alongside frontiersmen that delve into the Flesh Pit or the Chasm in search of treasure.


Aristocratic and Political. The Godking makes every decision of substance in Nalathesh, and his will is enforced by his Radiant Princes. These are highly educated magic-using military commanders capable of serving on the battlefield or in a legislative hall as needed. Nalathesh’s godking is named Neferu, and while he is somewhat unpopular among the common folk, he has also made Nalathesh quite prosperous. His right hand man, General Tenemei, oversees the Radiant Princes. While Neferu is begrudgingly respected, General Tenemei is outright feared for his cruelty. Prominent nobles, including the Luxxor family, publicly support the current administration and privately pressure Neferu to remove Tenemei from power.


It’s far from perfect, but it’s my home, and I would raise my shield and morningstar in its defense if it came to that.

-Marcus


Mercantile and Trade. Nalathesh’s caravans of traders are very skilled at surviving in the desert, and so while fewer traders come to Nalathesh directly, many Nalatheshi caravans trade with their nearest neighbors: Lesmishan to the north, Husk and Falth to the west, and Hadla to the south.


Bridgemoor sits directly on Nalathesh’s eastern border, and traders from Bridgemoor do frequently traverse Nalathesh due to their easy access to the nation. Nalatheshi traders also travel through Bridgemoor to reach Kendaria.


Militant. Nalathesh’s military is well-trained and well-equipped. All soldiers respond directly to the Godking or his commanders. The elite Bronzeguard are a legion of cavaliers that ride dracani into combat, decimating threats to the kingdom. They often patrol the Flesh Pit to ensure that its foul presence does not spread.


While far from the largest standing military on Gathandia, Nalathesh invests a tremendous amount of money and magical research into making their military as strong as possible.


Religious. Worship of the Godking is mandatory. National taxes are seen as a religious responsibility, and any sign of insincerity in one’s worship of the Godking is punishable with imprisonment.


Social. Nalatheshi culture revolves around the Godking, international trade, and the harsh but fascinating truths of desert living. Shade and wind are often celebrated as gifts from the Godking’s mercy, and sudden rainstorms might cause merchants and laborers to stop their work for the day and engage in revelry.


Underworld. A vast criminal network lurks throughout the nation, thus far undiscovered by the Godking. Known as the Sandsnakes, this network smuggles information, spices, and fine goods to the powerful and corrupt bankers of Lesmishan.

ARIDALA

A sprawling metropolis of multistory adobe apartments, vibrant bazaars, and confusing alleys, Aridala is the capital of Nalathesh. It rests on the only lake in the kingdom that doesn’t dry up each summer. The Godking’s palace is carved into the side of a sandstone cliff and is some of the most stunning architecture in Gathandia. The streets of Aridala are well maintained and crime is virtually nonexistent under the watchful eyes of the Radiant Princes.

POINTS OF INTEREST

The Desert. Most of Nalathesh is arid, scrubby desert. The terrain varies between pebbly sand in some areas, crumbling canyons in others, and rolling hills through the rest. Survival in this desert is easy for those who can identify edible plants and who know how to avoid venomous snakes and insects, but can be deadly for the uninformed. The crescent of land in Nalathesh’s south is much more fertile, sporting larger trees and greener grasses thanks to multitudinous freshwater springs.


The Flesh Pit. Near the center of Nalathesh’s expansive desert, there dwells a great pit of flesh. Miners have found that they can harvest this flesh and it makes fine jerky. Scientists use the flesh to conduct experiments with caustic reagents. Occasionally, the flesh awakens and eats some unfortunate folk, but the Bronzeguard stands ready for such events.


At the heart of the Flesh Pit, a full-grown M’l’ykkri waits with its hungering maw.


The Great Chasm. About half of Nalathesh’s border overlooks the Great Chasm. See Bridgemoor’s Points of Interest.


Floating Islands. Soaring above the fresh springs and lush greenery in the south of the kingdom, a chain of limestone islands float like immortal guardians.


Legend has it that the Godking keeps them aloft through sheer force of will as a sign of his power, but many scholars quietly doubt that this is the case. No one has yet been able to reach the floating islands; some of the Bronzeguard have tried with their dracani, but to no avail.

QUEST IDEAS

  • A person of interest has gone missing in the Flesh Pit.
  • Miners want to find a new vein of minerals within the Great Chasm.
  • The Godking is suspicious that criminal activity is happening in one of his cities and sends the heroes to investigate.
  • Criminals in one of the Godking’s distant cities recruit the heroes to help them smuggle spices out of the nation.
  • Scholars want the heroes to find a way to reach the floating islands and see what’s on top.

NAUSA

A remarkable city-state off the southern coast of Gathandia, Nausa is almost entirely underwater. Constructed primarily by amphibious humanoids, notably merfolk and dwellvur, Nausa serves as a gateway between land-based civilizations and subaquatic ones.


Nausa is shaped from massive coral structures, plaster, and thick, watertight weaves of seaweed. It is also enhanced with Avadrium, a strange metallic alloy that has magical properties. Nausa has a small port with a series of piers that jut above the surface of the ocean. This port contains a deep shaft with Avadrium lifts running up and down its length, allowing air-breathing humanoids to descend into Nausa’s interior buildings.


The vast majority of Nausa’s buildings reside on the ocean floor, hundreds of feet below the surface. Swimming hatches allow divers to easily access the ocean floor and return directly into the city, rather than having to dive from the surface of the ocean every time they wish to explore. These swimming hatches use two doors to prevent the water from flooding into the city.


Academic. Nausa has a large university that attracts all kinds of scientists, especially biologists eager to study wondrous sea life.


Aristocratic and Political. An elected triumvirate governs Nausa. Each triumvir has a six-year term, but elections to the triumvirate are staggered so that a new triumvir has a chance of getting elected once every two years. This offers some continuity as each new triumvir has a couple years to learn from those who already have experience in office, but it has led at times to chaotic decades of constant politicking.


Mercantile and Trade. Nausa exports very unique wares to merchant sailors, which brings a lucrative income to Nausa’s economy. Nausa exports shells, pearls, and coral skeletons as works of art or fine jewelry.


They also export a tough, fibrous seaweed cloth that functions much like waterproof leather or hide armor. But Nausa’s most valuable export is its pigment dye taken from the saliva of sea slugs that dwell on the ocean floor. This dye produces a rich purple hue and is highly sought after by royalty and prominent politicians.


Militant. Nausa does not maintain a military, as they live underwater and are both geographically and economically protected from invasion by any land-dwelling nation. The city watch is competent at keeping the peace within the city and are all trained as deep-sea divers, capable of mounting rescue missions or fighting off sea creatures beyond the scope of the city when needed.


Religious. Most Nausans practice the old faith held by the merfolk and dwellvur from before the Tear. On multiple occasions in ancient history, an enormous kaijan reminiscent of a turtle or a dinosaur is believed to have saved aquatic civilizations from another kaijan that looked like a blend of a squid and a humanoid. The former, called Jiruk, represents strength, honor, and sacrificial protection. The latter, called Thul, embodies an all-consuming desire to corrupt and destroy. While neither have been sighted in hundreds of years, many Nausans worship Jiruk as their savior and their patron god.


Social. Nausans are a mix of traditional and progress-driven folk. Traditionalists keep the practices of ancient aquatic nations alive and are more likely to be strongly religious. Progressives focus on trade with the land-based world and are very politically involved. They are generally not as concerned with matters of religion or the soul.


A fascinating example of aquatic and land-based life forms living together. Exquisite in its balance, the city addresses the needs of all its people.

-Finneas


Underworld. As is true of any nation that has valuable commodities, Nausa has its share of black market dealers that steal and resell pearls and their royal purple dye at criminal rates. Known as the Undertow, the largest syndicate has a well-organized mix of merchants and deep sea divers that keep a steady stream of dye flowing to the world’s black markets.

QUEST IDEAS

  • A contentious triumvirate election is coming up, and the heroes have been asked to help campaign on behalf of one of the candidates.
  • Deep sea divers want a little extra security as they search a newly discovered sinkhole, hoping to find more purple dye or to find Avadrium. Will the heroes protect them?
  • Nausa has sprung a leak. If the heroes cannot plug it, then the whole city-state might implode under the ocean’s pressure.
  • Murmurs of cult activity have everyone concerned. Rumor has it that the cultists want to awaken Thul from his watery grave.

NENT

A humid coastal state, Nent is known for its rocky shores, rolling hills, and diverse agricultural yields. It governed itself using a direct democracy for generations on end, but has been conquered by Roshallan. It is now a subsidiary state of the great empire.


Academic. Nent has a single university in Amphos, but all of its scholars have been exiled and replaced with Roshallani ones.


Aristocratic and Political. Nentese do not believe in aristocracy. Instead, they prefer to practice classical direct democracy: every citizen gets to vote on every single issue, and if a majority is not reached then status quo is maintained. As a relatively small nation, Nentese find it feasible to travel to a central gathering place once every year or two to vote on a wide variety of issues. Unfortunately for the Nentese, their democracy is no longer practiced now that they are a vassal state of Roshallan.


My heart aches for the Nentese. They did nothing to deserve such subjugation, nor do they have the means to overcome it.

-Chauncey


Mercantile and Trade. Nent’s hot and humid environment lends itself to growing a wide variety of agricultural goods. Many Nentese also raise sheep, cattle, pigs, and other livestock.


Militant. Nent no longer has any military to speak of, instead relying on Roshallan to protect them.


Religious. Many Nentese worship the Exiles in secret. This trend began after Roshallan annexed them, and those who keep this practice do so as they believe that the Exiles will be the most sympathetic to their current predicament.


Social and Underworld. Most Nentese are suspicious, both of foreigners and of one another. After Roshallan annexed their nation, Roshallan appointed some Nentese citizens to act as governors overseeing Nent’s various provinces. Most Nentese view this as a complete degradation of their democracy and resent their leaders for going along with Roshallan’s edicts. Many Nentese have taken it upon themselves to rebel against Roshallan and against those among the Nentese who were so morally bankrupt as to sell themselves into Roshallan’s pocket. This creates a vicious cycle, as the Roshallani insist on law-abiding Nentese suppressing their own people’s rebellion.

AMPHOS

Nent does not have a capital city so much as a central gathering place where the Nentese gather to vote on any issue. Amphos is an enormous amphitheater surrounded by inns and taverns that house Nentese citizens between votes.

QUEST IDEAS

  • Nentese rebels recruit the heroes to help them sabotage Roshallan.
  • The coastal hills are plagued by some kind of monster or pack of monsters. Can the heroes clear out this danger and return life to normal?

NEVNA

Nevna is primarily a coastal nation, as inland of its major cities there is nothing but rocky desert that has proven to yield little in the way of food or minerals. The temperate coasts yield some basic agriculture to supplement the Nevnan diet that is primarily fish and other seafood.


Over the centuries, Nevnan seafarers became more daring and greedy, plundering other ships and bringing the loot back to the mainland. Nevna’s rulers at the time denounced such actions, and those rulers were promptly deposed. Nevna is now respected and feared as a formidable pirate nation ruled by the wealthiest and most feared among the pirates: Jaken the Bronze, a trollkin mighty in both physique and influence.


While Nevna will occasionally engage in fair maritime trade with the coastal nations, most Nevnans prefer piracy, and their only real ally is the city-state of Vel.


Academic. Nevna places little value in scholasticism. A person’s capability aboard a ship or in the barren foothills should be all that you need to determine someone’s worth.


Aristocracy. Although once a monarchy, Nevna no longer places any stock in royal blood.


Mercantile. Nevna exports fine goods of many varieties. Virtually all of the finished goods that they sell are plundered from other merchant ships, so aside from the inherent danger involved it is a massively profitable market as long as they can find people to buy their wares.


Militant. Most Nevnan sailors are also capable fighters. Nevna has a huge navy, but a very small standing guard force in each of its coastal towns. Because of the vast and harsh desert isolating them from the rest of the world, they do not feel the need to have a large land army.


Political. Nobody in Nevna tries to maintain pretenses. Nevna is a criminal nation, run by criminals. Jaken the Bronze and other formidable pirate lords serve as the totalitarian rulers of their favorite port cities, and when a matter of national significance rises, these pirate lords will parley to discuss what needs done.


Religious. There are a handful of temples to sea gods throughout Nevna, but heartfelt worship is uncommon. Most Nevnan sailors respect the sea, but do not deify it, and think that the most valuable asset they have is not faith, but their own two hands.


Social. Nevna can be a rough place to live. Few people spare a second glance for the man getting mugged in the alley. In a cutthroat culture, one needs only look after themself.

ASHORI

Ashori, the capital of Nevna, is Jaken the Bronze’s personal lair. A ramshackle city built of limestone, mud huts, and driftwood shanties, Ashori doesn’t look like much. Even so, its markets have many fine goods from throughout Gathandia’s east coast. Skilled labor and exquisite crafts exist in abundance, but con artists and opportunists will prey on any who look foolish enough to fall for their wiles.


I chartered a voyage with Jaken the Bronze once. A tough and cruel man, but a remarkable warrior. I’d just as soon never end up on his bad side.

-Grim

POINTS OF INTEREST

The Glass Sea. About half a day’s sail east of Nevna’s northern coast, the ocean undergoes a strange phenomenon. While most of the ocean acts as one would expect, with bobbing waves and occasional small whitecaps, the Glass Sea is a stretch of ocean that is perfectly smooth. Ripples in the wake of a vessel vanish almost as soon as they form. Although the water looks smooth enough to walk on, sailors who have attempted this have all come back wet. The ocean doesn’t appear any shallower here than elsewhere, so the reason for this supernatural tranquility is still a mystery.

QUEST IDEAS

  • One pirate lord has stolen from another pirate lord, so the first pirate hires the heroes to steal the treasure back.
  • Kendaria or another worried nation hires the heroes to infiltrate and destabilize Ashori, or at least to scout it out and report back.
  • Rumor has it that Jaken the Bronze has found a map to powerful ancient treasure in a shipwreck somewhere in the Glass Sea.

NOORD

Travel in Noord is a dangerous venture. Glaciers and enormous cliffs give way to dense forests where becoming lost is all but guaranteed. A mountainous nation with craggy ravines and dark pine forests, the folk of Noord are often said to be as brooding as the landscape itself. Relying on shepherds, fishers, and foragers for much of the nation’s food, the Noords supplement their economic needs through extortion, aggressive trade, and outright banditry when needed.


I feel at home in Noord. Of the Gathandian nations, Noord reminds me most of my motherland, Teivos. One day I will sail back home across the ocean, but for now, Noord will serve as my surrogate homeland: cold weather and stern company, how I like it.

-Belozog


Academic. Noord maintains a handful of small schools for education in various fields. Young people are not allowed to attend schools and asked to serve their people through physical labor of some kind. Older folk who are no longer capable of intense physical labor are encouraged to attend school and commit their minds to the betterment of their nation.


Aristocratic and Political. Noord does not have a traditional aristocracy, but the older humanoids who have attended schooling are treated with respect and deference. They receive free food and lodging when they travel within Noord, and those who complete their schooling are looked to as community leaders. Regional governors and a national chancellor are self-selected from within this pool of educated elders. Noord’s current chancellor is Grumhilda Iceblack, a stern and grandmotherly mountain dwarf. Grumhilda is close friends with the Starborea’s governor, Dencassan Briarye. Thanks to their friendship, Noord and Doshairiel have become close trade partners.


Mercantile and Trade. Noord is well-situated with both a large forest and a mountain range that each contain many natural resources. Gathering food, game, and lumber from the forest, and minerals and iron ore from the mountains, Noord could be self-sufficient if it needed to be. Trading with and raiding other nations is a supplemental source of goods and income.


Militant. Every young adult is required to serve in the military and their training begins before they are ten years old. At sixteen, they join the ranks, and after four years’ service they may remain in the military or pursue an apprenticeship and career elsewhere in Noord society.


The Noord military is highly competent, large proportional to its population, and well-equipped. The military will engage in months-long thieving expeditions where they march into the steppes of Bishal and Kandiff, looting and pillaging whenever they find small trade caravans or stray ranchers.


Religious. Noord has no official religion. So long as a Noord puts the needs of their country above their own personal needs, they may practice whatever religious beliefs they wish. Many worship winter as a cruel but necessary deity that hardens and strengthens them through adversity, and they worship the northern lights as an omen of luck and fortune.


Social. Noords are a proud folk, and easily angered. They take pride in both their personal valor and the strength of their nation. A careless insult is punishable with a magnitude of broken bones, and while duels to resolve conflict are strictly forbidden in the military, they often happen both within and outside the confines of the military order.


Underworld. Each of Noord’s towns and cities have gangs that scrap over territory, but they are frequently snuffed out by the military only to form again after a few months of silence. There is no nation-wide organized structure to these gangs.

PHALANX

The mountainous capital of Noord, Phalanx resides in the northernmost stretches of the nation. Built on the icy cliffs on the northern end of the mainland Cloudpiercer Range, Noords believe Phalanx to be utterly impregnable. The elders of Noord have invested much time and energy in expanding Phalanx to the point that the capital now stretches across four mountain peaks, but is only accessible through one. In the unthinkable event of Noord’s military suffering a crippling defeat, the entire population of Noord could fit inside Phalanx. It has two freshwater springs underneath it, not to mention the vast quantity of snow and ice, so Noords would only need to worry about their food supply in the event of a prolonged siege. The harsh winters would then force any rational foe to retreat.


While it is built to survive warfare, Phalanx also offers amenities such as a sports arena and an orchestra hall.

POINTS OF INTEREST

The Glowing Forest. On the west side of the Cloudpiercer Range, strange trees grow in oddly regular patterns. These trees seem to be made of some synthesis of vegetable and metal matter, and they grow pear-shaped glass bulbs that occasionally glow from within. Noords are generally hesitant to enter the forest, but will occasionally go out and collect these glass lights, which they use to illuminate Phalanx.

QUEST IDEAS

  • The Noords ask the heroes to harvest resources from the Glowing Forest.
  • In the face of invasion from a terribly powerful enemy (perhaps fiends from Inferno), heroes need to help the Noords evacuate to Phalanx and then must endure a harsh winter fighting a supernaturally strong foe.
  • The Noords are on a rampaging war path, so representatives from Kandiff ask the heroes to infiltrate Phalanx and destabilize its defenses or assassinate its leaders.

PASH

Located at the mouth of a great bay that opens up into the ocean, Pash is an economically and militarily powerful city-state that facilitates major international trade. The Pashan International Bank is the largest bank in Gathandia and contains a vast sum of riches. When Pash is threatened by a foreign power, the autocrats weigh the costs of fighting the invading force or simply bribing them to move on. This calculation, when also weighed by the aggressor, usually mitigates bloodshed.


Pash also controls a handful of mining towns located along the Vaenyth River, south and east of Vaenyth’s border. The climate ranges from mild to hot each year and is always quite humid.


The remarkable settlement has inspired me to compose a song!

“Slash a pass through Pash you see

And just past Pash you’ll see the sea!”

Eh, terrible.

-Flanagan


Academic. Pash has a large internationally acclaimed university that offers virtually any course imaginable but specializes in economic theory. Pashan merchants that retire for a quieter end of life will often teach these courses. The Pashan Academy attracts scholars from many nations.


Aristocratic, Political, and Militant. Pash is ruled by an autocracy. Military generals are also legislators and magisters, overseeing every aspect of Pashan life. Both the Pashan army and navy are well-equipped, well-staffed, and well-trained. The military keeps everything in Pash carefully controlled. Their borders with Vaenyth and Roshallan are both heavily guarded and fortified.


Mercantile and Trade. Under the direction of the generals, the merchant lords and bankers of Pash play Vaenyth and Roshallan off of one another in order to keep either belligerent nation from conquering Pash. As Pash is built on both banks of the massive Great Bay, they thrive on running merchant ferries across the bay and docking international trade ships the world over. The river that feeds into the Great Bay is also quite rich in gold, so Pashan divers mine the gold from the riverbed to feed their economy. Pash has powerful and reliable banks that many wealthy merchants and royalty from dozens of nations will use to keep their assets safe.


Religious. Most Pashans fervently worship the Celestial Council and the Avatars, believing that their devout faith is part of what affords them ongoing safety from both Vaenyth and Roshallan.


Social. Pashans are not unkind, but they are generally quite cunning. Pashan merchants are ruthless hagglers, and the divers and miners are typically rugged and competent fighters.


As hard-edged as Pashans usually are, they are also hospitable, believing that treating visitors well will reflect positively and draw more international business.


Underworld. A huge crime syndicate thrives in Pash’s grimy underworld. It has its hands in every aspect of Pashan life, from mining to banking to ferrying merchants across the Great Bay. The crime lords also act as points of contact for other international syndicates, especially the mob bosses of Falth.

POINTS OF INTEREST

Floating Islands. Large chunks of floating rock hover above much of the Great Bay and the river that feeds it. These structures extend up the Vaenyth Range. Pashan bankers have paid mages to fly to the rocks and determine if there is anything valuable to be gained from them. The assessment was conclusive: the floating islands are rich in Arcanium and Primor, two rare and valuable minerals with unique magical properties. Pash is currently in the process of determining how best to mine these resources.


The Great Bay. The Great Bay is so wide that it takes just over a day to sail from one side to the other. The Vaenyth River feeds into the Great Bay, which opens into the ocean at its mouth. The main city-state of Pash is built on both of the Great Bay’s banks and on the islands that dot the region.

  • One of the most lucrative mining outposts has gone quiet, so the autocrats hire the heroes to investigate.
  • The first floating island mine is about to become operational, but a dangerous creature has taken up residence in the mineshaft. Can the heroes defeat it?
  • Roshallan, Vaenyth, or another nation poses a threat to Pash and is unwilling to accept bribes. Will the heroes help protect the independent city-state?

RENDEN

Renden is a nation of seafarers that brutally raid coastal towns and cities along the Inner Sea. Originally, the people of Renden shared much of their culture with Fjordenhavian, Noord, and Teivosi society, but over the generations, the people of Renden lost all shred of morality.


Known as the terrors of the Inner Sea, the Renden do not discriminate between any town or seafaring vessel of any nation. They will raid anything, leaving boats and buildings in burning ruins and leaving sailors and townsfolk to rend their clothes and beg for mercy.


Aristocratic and Political. Renden is administered by the Circle of Matriarchs. These are elder Renden women with a deep connection to the Primal Source. Capable of calming great sea storms, they rule from their enormous vessel, the Ravager, which often has several smaller boats and wide, flat barges lashed to it or anchored nearby.


While the Circle of Matriarchs make legislative decisions for their nation, the Kings of the Sea enforce these laws and conduct raids. Each captain of a coastal longship or a deep-sea pirate ship is considered a King of the Sea.


Mercantile and Trade. Renden do not trade with other nations, for they never willingly give anything to those who are not their own. Rather, they plunder and pillage whenever they need to supplement their internal economy.


Militant. The Renden are competent sailors and vicious coastal fighters. Their longships are narrow, quiet, fast, and low to the ground. This allows them to quickly come upon a coastal town and catch any watchguards by surprise. Renden deep sea vessels, like the Ravager, are much larger and slower.


Renden really isn’t doing anyone any favors. The threat of raids has only strengthened the hold that Falth’s coastal crime lords keep over their citizens.

-Charlie


Religious. The Renden have no religion. They live and die by the iron in their hands, the blood in their veins, and the salt air in their lungs.

THE RAVAGER

The Ravager is a twelve-masted boat of tremendous size. Covered in living vegetation grown by the magical hands of the Circle of Matriarchs, it could be mistaken at a distance for a small island. The Ravager contains an entire marketplace, two inns and taverns, and a council chamber where the Circle of Matriarchs make decisions for their nation.


Several large Renden barges often float nearby, housing stolen goods and additional taverns and marketplaces aboard their wooden decks.

THE ISLANDS

The Renden are more at home on sea than land, but they have many hidden coves and obscure islands in the Inner Sea where they stash their wares or occasionally go for leisurely hunting expeditions.

NACHTSTROND

The Renden will, at all costs and on pain of death, avoid Nachtstrond, or the Black Coast. This stretch of woodland appears to be in a constant state of blighted decay, and an enormous but withered World-Tree stretches its thorny boughs over the expanse of craggy forbidden shores. The Renden believe that the area is haunted by the souls of all humanoids killed violently. Needless to say, if this is true, then any Renden that sets foot on the shore will be torn apart by vengeful ghosts in a matter of moments.

QUEST IDEAS

  • The heroes must guide a merchant vessel through the Inner Sea and protect them from Renden attacks.
  • A coastal town has heard rumor that the Renden are coming and the mayor asks the heroes to protect the town.
  • The heroes, in fleeing from a Renden sea vessel, have shipwrecked on Nachtstrond. Now they must venture back into the sea and face the Renden, or press deeper into the haunted forest.

ROSHALLAN

A tremendous empire sprawling from Gathandia’s west coast to the Cloudpiercer Range, as far north as Chee and as far south as Nent, Roshallan is widely considered the most powerful nation on the continent and is unquestionably the largest. Roshallan’s natural environment changes dramatically given how large it is. It encompasses swathes of desert, two small mountain ranges, enormous stretches of plains, and dense forests. Roshallan is also the undisputed naval power in the lower Inner Sea. Roshallan’s navy has yet to be tested against the Renden of the upper Inner Sea.


Thus far, Roshallan has conquered Chee, Nent, Getar, and most of Thaaj, running each one as vassal states. Roshallani believe themselves to be destined to conquer the whole of Gathandia, bringing order and justice to a chaotic land. To learn more about Roshallan’s relationship with each of its vassal states, see their entries.


Roshallan is beautiful, oh yes. The pinnacle of humanoid accomplishment… and of humanoid greed.

-Chauncey


Academic. Roshallan has built some spectacular universities, primarily in its stretch of grassland along the southern coast of the Inner Sea.


These universities are some of the best-designed in Gathandia and attract Anedonian scholars and other prestigious thinkers despite Roshallan’s negative international reputation.


Aristocratic and Political. Roshallan’s emperor oversees the entire empire from the capital. The emperor delegates individual sectors of governance, such as finance or security, to their council, who work with regional monarchs to ensure that Roshallan’s interests are maintained. These rulers often employ a feudal system within their territory but are given the authority to structure the government of their own region however they deem best. Monarchs that show any signs of insubordination against the emperor are quickly taken care of. Roshallan’s first mighty emperor who began Roshallan’s era of conquest was Exandros. Its current emperor, Nerian, is still very competent if not yet as legendary as his predecessor. Emperor Nerian has every intention to decisively conquer Chehozran within the next five years.


Mercantile and Trade. Given its massive sprawl, Roshallan is a tremendous economic powerhouse. It facilitates trade within its vast empire by developing sturdy, durable roads that cut down on travel time. It also trades with Pash, Ahten, and Anedonia, each of whom have proven strong enough to resist Roshallan’s aggressions. Many international merchant vessels dock along Roshallan’s southwest coast.


Militant. Roshallan boasts a large and disciplined military. It has a naval presence in the Inner Sea thanks to the shipyards of Chee. On land, Roshallan’s military is divided into legions - groups of one thousand soldiers led by generals. Each general oversees a commander, who leads one hundred of the legion’s troops. Roshallan benefits from well-crafted weaponry and advanced tactical training.


Religious. The Roshallani worship the Celestial Council, believing that the Council has blessed them with prosperity due to their own righteousness. Roshallani believe that so long as they continue their divine mandate to unite Gathandia, fortune will continue to smile upon them.


Social. Roshallani citizens are fiercely proud of their nation. They are not very hospitable to foreign travelers, and are equally unfriendly towards the peoples of Roshallani vassal states. Annexed folk, such as Nentese, generally despise the Roshallani.


Underworld. Crime lords lurk in most large Roshallani cities, conducting their own business out of the sight of their ruling monarchs. Brigands and bandit gangs also make the countryside their hunting ground, preying on merchants and travelers.

TIBERIA

A shining city of grand proportions, Tiberia’s architecture reflects Roshallan’s splendor. It is built between the foothills of the Marble Mountains and Lake Tiberia. Many of its finest buildings are constructed of white marble mined from the mountains that sparkles gloriously in the sun. Tiberia offers much entertainment for its citizenry, and the Grand Coliseum draws crowds from all across the empire to watch magnificent battles. Tiberia’s temples are frequented by those who wish to make offerings to the Celestial Council, and the bathhouses are also a popular haunt of well-off citizens of the region. The emperor’s palace is nearly as large as the Grand Coliseum with scores of luxurious rooms for the emperor and their guests to enjoy.

POINTS OF INTEREST

Lava Fields. Roshallan’s eastern region is split by large lava fields that flow from the mountains down to the sea. While the land in the immediate vicinity is a charred ruin, the land just beyond is quite fertile and Roshallani farmers sow it to produce great quantities of food each year.


The Marble Mountains. A rocky, craggy mountain range rife with volcanic activity marks the border between Roshallan and Ahten. These mountains are full of marble, which the Roshallani mine as a great economic resource for themselves and for international trade.

  • The emperor asks the heroes to put down a regional king who has gotten a little too ambitious.
  • Farmers near the lava fields are devastated to find that a new flow is headed for their crops. Can the heroes redirect the flow or evacuate the farmers in time?
  • A large band of brigands have taken over a city and the hills surrounding it. Can the heroes negotiate with or defeat the bandit captains?

ROVEL

The most prolific seafaring traders in Gathandia live in Rovel. A large forested peninsula west of Falth and prominent in the Inner Sea, Rovel enjoys warm summers and chilly winters but virtually no major swings to unbearably hot or cold. Seasonal thunderstorms occasionally bring tornadoes with them, though this is not a common occurrence.


Rovel’s docks - especially the docks of Hollyport - are the largest in Gathandia, capable of housing massive fleets of military or merchant vessels. Although they share the Inner Sea with the bloodthirsty Renden, they have proven both militarily strong enough to withstand raids and economically cunning enough to buy out the Renden.


Academic. Rovel places a good deal of emphasis on a robust education. Merchants that trade internationally bring in most of Rovel’s economic success, and students that learn the cultures of other nations, learn the technical aspects of trade and commerce, and understand the physics of sailing are - in Rovelese belief - the most likely to flourish. Children are expected to receive a basic education and encouraged to take specialized classes in sailing, cultural communications, international commerce, or banking.


Aristocratic and Political. Rovel has a monarchy, but at any given point a king or queen is really no more than a figurehead. The real political and legislative power in the nation is in the hands of the merchant guilds, and they use the monarch to give legitimacy to any laws that they want enacted.


Mercantile and Trade. The powerful merchant families in Rovel really run the nation. If they were to remove their support from the monarch, then the monarch’s entire reign would economically fall apart. With their massive, well-armed trade caravels, Rovel’s merchant fleet trade far and wide, extending as far as Yodvan to the northeast and Pash to the southwest. Many merchant vessels keep small chests of gold on board as insurance against Renden raids. Renden raiders know that the Rovelese can put up quite a fight on the water, so they will often accept small tolls in exchange for letting the Rovelese sail through their waters otherwise unmolested.


Sailing a Rovelese ship would be a fascinating experience, I’d wager. I wonder where one might take me, and who I might meet.

-Lyla


Militant. Rovel’s seafaring vessels are fortified and well-armed. Their military is small but adequate to defend against Renden’s coastal raids.


Religious. Rovelese are more enamored of their economic success than displays of piety. Temples are not very common in Rovelese towns or cities, and the religious among the nation usually practice their religion in the privacy of their own homes with small gatherings of likeminded friends and family.


Social. Most Rovelese folk are very results-driven and enjoy bragging about their successes. They are willing enough to lend a hand to someone in need, but this is on some level just another data point that they can use when demonstrating that they are good, upstanding folk.


Underworld. Falthic spies run rampant in Rovelese society. Some such spies - and even some unscrupulous Rovelese - will sell merchant ship sailing routes to Renden, allowing the violent raiders to ambush boats.

HOLLYPORT

Rovel’s capital city is perhaps unsurprisingly its largest port. The monarchy has a decadent but not very defensible palace, and the most powerful merchant lords have incredibly well defended manors that are almost as plush and luxurious as the monarch’s palace. Hollyport has an enormous network of docks and piers that allow their large merchant vessels to maintain year-round trade with the nations in and beyond the Inner Sea.

  • A merchant leader approaches the heroes and asks them to investigate the leader’s guild, as rumors are circulating that Falthic spies have infiltrated the guild.

RUPTAEA

A nation clinging to chilly rocks and sparse boreal forests, Ruptaea’s harsh land has solicited a generous response from its people. The rainy summers and blizzard-ridden winters have instilled in Ruptaeans a sense of kinship and shared suffering. Ruptaeans will commonly invite one another over to share in what little food they have.


Academic. Most of Ruptaea’s settlements have schools that cover the basics of reading, mathematics, and history. Ruptaea has no major universities or advanced educational institutions, which is something that Queen Belle is trying to remedy.


Aristocratic and Political. The aristocracy of Ruptaea are the lords and ladies that govern each individual settlement. These aristocrats elect a monarch from among their ranks; that monarch rules until their death or abdication, at which point the aristocrats elect the monarch’s successor. The current monarch is a hondu named Belle, better known for her empathetic nature than for her political smarts.


Her reputation precedes her. Queen Belle is both beautiful and kind. I hope to one day kneel in her service.

-Chauncey


Mercantile and Trade. Ruptaea is economically weak with few natural resources to offer in international trade. It has some mineral mines, some stone quarries, and some forests that can produce timber and plant and animal byproducts, but none of its resources are notable enough to make Ruptaea competitive with other nations.


Militant. Ruptaea has a weak military, but they have a good relationship with Fjordenhaven and Eryndor. They dedicate most of their military resources to the border they share with Vaenyth.


Religious. In general, Ruptaeans are very religious. They worship many pantheons, making sacrificial offerings to the Celestial Council for good luck, to the Fiends and Exiles of Inferno to ward off their wrath, and to the Archfae Conclaves for bounty. They also worship the Avadri and Avatars, primarily because they do not want any pantheon to feel overlooked or slighted.


Social. Ruptaeans are humble folk, and this usually extends to the aristocracy as well. They will make plans for their days or weeks, but if a neighbor or traveler needs food or shelter, most Ruptaeans will invite them into their own homes and provide for those who need it, setting aside their own plans for the day.


Underworld. Small, localized gangs are uncommon but not unheard of in some of the larger towns and cities. Given Ruptaea’s poor economy, large crime syndicates have not formed in the nation as there is no major profit to be gained.

ESSUR

The capital of Ruptaea is Essur. Built on a hill rich with groundwater springs, it is one of the most defensible locations in the nation. A small palace of common stone polished to resemble marble is the residence of the reigning monarch. Marketplaces and cramped residential houses spread out in a tangle of crooked streets radiating from the palace. Many temples stand proud throughout the city as well, and the foundation has been laid for the new Essur University.

POINTS OF INTEREST

The Ruptaea Glacier. In the north, an enormous glacier looms over Ruptaea. Moving at its snail-like pace, it carves a trench on its journey west towards the ocean. In hundreds of years, it will likely complete its journey. Ruptaeans harvest ice from the glacier, and young adventurous types try to climb it and explore its frigid top.


The Storm. A couple days’ sail from Ruptaea’s west coast, a tremendous hurricane churns in perpetuity. It has been there for hundreds of years, never moving, never growing or shrinking either in size or in magnitude. Known simply as the Storm, the Ruptaeans do not know why it has sustained itself without moving, but so long as it continues to remain static it poses no threat to the nation.

QUEST IDEAS

  • Some foolish would-be adventurers have sailed into the Storm. Queen Belle asks the heroes to mount a rescue mission.
  • Vaenyth has been acting belligerent near Ruptaea’s border, and the aristocrats would like for the heroes to travel to some Vaenythan towns and tell them to back off.
  • A warm summer has cause chunks of the glacier to break off and melt, which is causing serious flooding in some Ruptaean towns. Can the heroes rescue the commoners?

SHADRADOTH

In the subtropical hills of Thunderhead Vale, Shadradoth has a storied and chaotic history. It seceded from Valstrom long ago after a bloody civil war. The king regent, who was an advisor to the recently deceased king, refused to abdicate when the king’s eldest daughter came of age and made her claim to the throne. The ensuing war split the nation apart, with Princess Shadra leading the region that eventually became Shadradoth.


Shadradoth upheld the heirship of Queen Shadra for several generations thereafter. However, the kingdom crumbled again when Shadra’s descendent, King Thunharad, did not sire an heir or pronounce any of his cousins next in line. Anyone who could claim relations to King Thunharad claimed that they were the heir apparent. The ensuing war devastated the country, and to this day Shadradoth still has not recovered.


A tale as old as time. All it takes is one power-hungry criminal and one legitimate ruler unwilling to budge, and you have civil war.

-Belozog


Academic. Shadradoth has many schools in its larger towns, most of which were established when Shadradoth was still part of Valstrom. However, few of these institutions are still staffed in any meaningful way.


Aristocratic and Political. The Shadradothan governmental system is a complete disaster. Half of the nation runs itself like a feudal system, but there is no central uniting authority. Many merchants and some whole towns or small regions instead govern themselves through democratic elections and consensus votes. The result is a mishmash of half-baked political theories that do not work on their own, let alone when interacting with one another.


Mercantile and Trade. Individual settlements often trade with one another within Shadradoth. Those on the border near another nation will also trade with their neighbors, but any form of organized international trade is nonexistent due to Shadradoth’s dysfunctional government.


Militant. While the political process by which settlements run themselves vary widely among Shadradothan towns, each town has a militia that can be rallied by whatever political force leads that town.


In this respect, the Shadradothan military is very feudal in its structure, lacking any nationalized model.


Religious. Some Shadradothans share religious beliefs with the Valstromians. They worship the Celestial Council and believe that the good or evil actions of an individual will be blessed or punished by the Council. Other Shadradothans believe that the Exiles should be venerated and see their own nation as exiled in a sense, as Shadra should have been the rightful queen of Valstrom.


Social. Shadradoth’s population is angry and distrusting. They cannot agree with one another on politics, religion, or basic cultural practices. This is less pronounced within individual settlements, where folk may overlook perceived slights in their neighbors’ behavior. On a national scale, however, many towns and regions despise one another.


Underworld. Given the chaos inherent in Shadradoth’s culture and politics, crime syndicates flourish. Notable syndicates include the Twice Strikes, the Hoods, and the Galepiercers. These syndicates operate widely in both Shadradoth and Valstrom.


The Twice Strikes get their name from their earned reputation of robbing a place twice: first nonviolently burgling it and getting a lay of the land, and second by brutally attacking any who try to defend the place. The Hoods are beloved by the peasantry as they rob from the rich and give their richest to the poor. Finally, the Galepiercers are so named for the elementals that they summon to cause chaos, which they use to draw attention from their illicit activity.

THUNHARAN

Formerly the capital city of Shadradoth, Thunharan is now a smoking ruin. After the tremendous civil war that ravaged Shadradoth, there is no central authority around which all regions can unite.

POINTS OF INTEREST

Cloudpiercers. The Cloudpiercer Range’s tallest mountains overshadow Shadradoth. One of the mountains is so enormous that nobody has ever seen its peak. It always disappears above the clouds, or is so tall that the slight haze of the atmosphere obscures its magnificent heights. Although foolish adventurers have tried to climb it, none have even come remotely close to seeing its peak.


Thundervale River. In the bend of the Thundervale River, the Shadradothans have discovered a wondrous point of power. The river strikes a cliff and flows up the face of it like an upside-down waterfall. It then continues on its flow southward towards Sozein.


As Valstrom’s sovereign territory begins on the other half of the river, Shadradoth and Valstrom have agreed to construct a neutral city at the base of the waterfall so that they can experience its wonder together. Explorers have found that the waterfall also contains a portal to the Everwilds.

QUEST IDEAS

  • Boiling discontent washes through Shadharoth as multiple nobles and democratically elected officials each claim that they should oversee the entire nation. The heroes might get involved to make a power play themselves or to back a contender whose views they find commendable.
  • A noble who claims to be the direct descendent of Shadra asks the heroes to guard them as they travel to the Great Cloudpierce, the tallest mountain in the world, where they believe they can procure evidence verifying their claim.
  • Valstrom has conquered the city that they have historically shared with Shadradoth at the base of the waterfall. Nobles and diplomats ask the heroes to negotiate with both word and sword to reclaim the Shadradothans’ right to occupy the city.

SKAVR

It is said that the Skavri are born with axes in hand and sent to battle fell beasts by their second birthday. A cold, hard nation of cold, hard people, Skavr sprawls across the northwest forests of the Inner Sea.


The Skavri stretch of forest is old and steeped in ancient legend. The boreal trees themselves seem to whisper of the many monsters, battles, and feats of heroism that they have witnessed over the generations. Skavr has chilly summers and icy winters. As brutal winds from the Ice Sea roll down on Skavr, snow smothers the nation and the Northern Lights seem to taunt the frigid settlements.


The Skavri are skilled woodcarvers and carpenters. Their buildings have steep, wood-shingled roofs that drain the heavy snowfall down to the ground, but their buildings are as decorative as they are functional. Relief scenes are carved in the outsides of many log cabins and merchant stores, and these scenes generally depict tales of Skavri heroism in the face of any number of horrid monsters or the dreaded Renden raids.


Academic. The capital of Skavr possesses a large library run by a handful of dedicated scholars, but beyond this there are no formal institutes of education in the nation.


Aristocratic and Military. Skavr is a nation of warrior-kings. Each town is governed by the strongest warrior, who tends to legislative and judicial responsibilities during times of peace and who leads their warriors from the front lines during times of war.


At any point during times of peace, another warrior may challenge their town’s warrior-king for the throne. The challenger and the incumbent battle with blunted weapons until one yields. When incumbents feel that they are too old to continue leading battles from the front lines, they may abdicate to a challenge whom they believe is a worthy heir.


All warrior-kings defer to the High King. The High King can also be challenged by warrior-kings, but not by any Skavri citizen who is not a warrior-king. Skavr’s current High King, named Muris Irontusk, is an enormous trollkin who has no decorum or manners but is nonetheless an intuitively successful leader.


Rule by the sword. They’re my kind of people.

-Hanniah


The Skavri are competent sailors and stalwart soldiers. The belligerent nation of Renden often attacks coastal towns and cities, but find that the Skavri can hold them off more often than not.


Mercantile and Trade. Skavr trades readily enough with both Eryndor and Fjordenhaven, as its two closest neighbors. Skavr generally exports mercenaries and carved wood in exchange for food and spices.


Religious. The Skavri are not particularly religious, but those who do practice religion often worship the Vertumna Conclave of the Archfae, venerating each season as it comes in turn year after year.


Social. Skavri are generally hostile to foreigners and prefer to keep to themselves. The exception is folk from Eryndor, whom the Skavri see as siblings in the fight against the horrors of the forest. The Skavri also tolerate Fjordenhavians, but they privately believe these folk to be too soft for the harshness of the far north.

ICEKAIRN

The capital city of Skavr is a fortified mountain city, hard to access and even harder to besiege. From the stony walls, the High King commands their warrior-kings and calls them to arms when a national threat arises.

POINTS OF INTEREST

Nachtstrond and Nachtskog. Skavr often fights off terrible monsters from the Black Coast or the Black Woods. See Eryndor or Renden for more information.

SOZEIN

Sozein populates the western stretch of the Kozia Rainforest. Like Tafunga, Sozein has tremendous trees that divide the rainforest into three separate habitats: the emergent layer high above the rest of the forest, the canopy where most natural life dwells, and the forest floor, reserved primarily for rodents and some reptiles such as snakes and crocodiles. The Sozeini swathe of the Kozia Rainforest also has dense bamboo forests, which are smaller than the rainforest at large but are impossibly dense to navigate.


Sozein’s cities are savvily built with drainage in mind, directing monsoon rains to flood their water reservoirs rather than their streets.


Academic. Sozein’s largest cities all have well-staffed educational institutions. Magic and its practical applications is a chief area of study among Sozeini scholars.


Aristocratic and Political. Sozein operates on a governmental system similar to a feudal system, but with only a dynastic emperor and the local governors of settlements. There are no lords or ladies that act as middle management between individual towns and the dynastic authorities, though there are emissaries from the emperor trained to speak on behalf of the royal family. The same dynastic family, the Long’han dynasty, has been in charge of Sozein for over a thousand years.


Sozein also has a great historical connection with the Nine Dragons and with dragonfolk!

Mercantile and Trade. Sozein is known for its spices, dramatically flavored food, bright pigments, vegetation (especially bamboo), and mineral-rich clay. It exports these goods to Nausa, Shadradoth, Valstrom, and its merchant vessels will also take their wares as far west as Nent and as far east as Vel.


Militant. Sozein has a cutting-edge military with fighters that obsessively train in the art of using magic in combat. Different contingents of soldiers will focus on the same function of magic, and some elite contingents blend soldiers from multiple functional specializations.


Religious. The Sozeini worship five of the six Archfae Conclaves in equal measure. They have little concept of the Vertumna Conclave as the only seasons in the Kozia Rainforest are humid, rainy, and monsoon. The other five Conclaves are all important to Sozeini culture.


Social. Sozein offers little social safety for those who are unable to keep up with their jobs or with the advent of magic. Merchants and governors are often unnecessarily cutthroat in an effort to ensure that they will not be left behind.


Underworld. Spies from Tafunga engage in elaborate undercover missions to destabilize Sozein as much as possible. They might stoke the flames of social unrest or undermine a strategically important governor at a crucial moment.

WYVANAI

The capital of Sozein, Wyvanai, is a tremendous coastal city from which Sozein’s ruling dynasty oversees their nation. It has the largest temples to the Archfae out of any Sozeini city, and has the second-largest university (the largest being at the World-Tree). The Wyvenai Palace is protected by dozens of magical guards and wards, and the imperial gardeners breed domesticated wyverns.

The World-Tree. Sozein maintains a tenuous peace with Tafunga at the World-Tree, which rests on the border between the two nations. While the Tafungans view the tree as a sacred site, the Sozeini are generally more interested in it from an academic standpoint. Magical scholars study the tree in hopes of learning how it grew so tall and what magical properties might be gleaned from it.


If the Sozeini were to trace the tree’s roots deep below the ground, they would find that it is an offshoot sproutling of the ancient World-Tree in Doshairiel.

TAFUNGA

Deep in the heart of the Kozia Rainforest, Tafunga is one of the most biodiverse regions in all Gathandia. The enormous rainforest can roughly be divided into three tiers. The emergent layer is composed of the tops of the highest trees, where raptors and other flying creatures roost. Beneath the emergent layer, the thick canopy is home to the majority of Tafungan life. Jaguars, monkeys, various birds and reptiles, and all manner of mythical creature make their homes in the thick, tangled overgrowth that ranges from a couple dozen to a couple hundred feet above the ground. Finally, the forest floor exists as a dark, shadowed realm that never sees the full light of day due to the thick canopy. Enormous rodents, snakes, and crocodiles lurk in this murky region.


The tribes of Tafunga primarily make their homes in the wide boughs of the canopy, though some tribes or criminal groups are denizens of the forest floor. Tribes that live in the canopy are generally stationary, and those on the forest floor are usually forced to be nomadic due to monsoon weather causing seasonal flooding.


Academic. Tafunga does not have schools confined to libraries and lecture halls. Instead, wise town and village elders will hold practical lessons with their students in the real world, showing them how to harvest mushrooms, interact safely with wild animals, navigate the trees, and the like.


Aristocratic and Political. Tafungan society revolves around tribal communities. These tribes generally cooperate with one another, and tribal leaders will typically defer to whichever leader in a negotiation has the most populous tribe. Tribes will often team up with one another to fight monsters or foreign nations that pose a threat to all involved. If someone feels that their leader is insufficient to the task, then they can challenge the leader to a psionic duel imposed by mushroom consumption.


Mercantile and Trade. Tafunga is most internationally notable for its exportation of many types of mushrooms with culinary, medicinal, and recreational properties. Tafungan merchants generally do not trade with Sozein due to their ongoing conflicts, but they will readily sell their mushrooms to Hadla, Nausa, Valstrom, and Shadradoth, as well as to any international merchants who make the journey to Tafunga. However, upstanding Tafungans do not sell the starcap mushroom to any foreigners, as this mushroom is considered sacred and commodifying it in any form of trade arrangement would be seen as blasphemous.


Militant. While all of Tafunga’s tribes together do not have a particularly large military, they do have a key advantage in tactical combat. Tafungan soldiers consume mushroom spores that allow them to communicate telepathically with other soldiers, which allows them to silently coordinate their attacks. This makes them especially effective at sabotage, infiltration, and similar high-skill missions.


Religious. Shamans in Tafungan society use starcap mushrooms to induce astral projection and vivid hallucinations. These shamans will then receive divine counsel from the gods. In these trips, the gods that the shamans speak with are usually the Archfae, although sometimes they are imaginary.

LOOTSDAOT

Tafunga’s largest tribe is Lootsdaot, which has a tremendous city in the canopy of the rainforest an hour’s walk north of the Kosia Sinkhole. Like other Tafungan tribes, Lootsdaot does not have any major buildings for administration, religion, or education, all of which occur in the natural world and among the citizenry. Lootsdaot does maintain large bathhouses on the forest floor, as well as performance theatres and large inns and taverns in the canopy.

POINTS OF INTEREST

Kozia Sinkhole. In the heart of Tafunga, less than an hour’s journey from the outskirts of Lootsdaot, the Kozia Sinkhole drops some two hundred feet down into the ground. It experiences flooding during monsoon season, but the Tafungans have dug artificial cave networks in and around the sinkhole to mitigate as much flooding as possible. The Kozia Sinkhole contains a jungle of exclusively mushrooms and similar fungi. Massive mushrooms grow out of even larger mushrooms beneath them, and smaller mushrooms grow on and around the enormous ones. The Kozia Sinkhole is mined for its mushrooms the way another nation might mine gold.


The World-Tree. Tafunga and Sozein have a tense peace at the World-Tree which rests on the border between the two nations. The Tafungans view this as a sacred site, and the Sozeini as an academic one. See Sozein for more information.

Teivos

Several large clans, comprised of tribes that each have several dozen family units, maintain a nomadic lifestyle, roaming across thousands of miles in the northern tundra, the Ice Sea, and parts of the Inner Sea. The clans usually follow herds of game animals such as bison, caribou, or even dolphins and seals. These clans, collectively known as the Teivos, have differing temperaments and differing relationships with other nations, but they all share basically the same culture with the other clans.


Mercantile. Each Teivos clan is self-sufficient. By hunting game or taking from vegetation, they supply their own food, make their own clothes, and fashion any tools, boats, carriages, and the like that they might need. Many enjoy some form of crafting as a hobby, like whittling or jeweling. These craftspeople will occasionally trade with merchants of other nations when they are near a settled area.


Political. Each clan maintains itself differently. Some prefer to hold democratic gatherings where each tribe collectively decides what the best course of action is and representatives from the tribes negotiate the clan’s actions based on this input. Others are almost feudal with a clan chieftain who delegates some responsibilities to tribal leaders. While each clan is self-governing, all clans defer to the High Queen, the leader of the Imurtok clan.


Religious. The clans share a belief in the sacredness of all plant and animal life, along with the changing of the seasons. The clans worship different Archfae conclaves accordingly, and most tribes have specific gods that they feel most connected with. Above all, though, the clans worship the mysterious and elusive Avadri, whom the clans believe created the cosmos and all within it.


Social. Most clans are reclusive and avoid contact with permanent settlements, aside from occasionally trading with their merchants. The folk of each tribe generally welcome other nomads to their campfires and share what food they have with any who need it.

CLANS

Atpar. The smallest of the six major clans, the Atpar are skillful sailors. They are mighty oceanic hunters, capable of taking down a killer whale while only sailing in large canoes. Needless to say, they feel more comfortable at sea than any other clan. Their patron deity is Porov.


Deniput. The hunters of Deniput are the most skilled foresters among all the clans. They spend most of the year north of the Inner Sea and try to undertake their northern pilgrimage during the height of summer, hoping to mitigate the dangers on the Ice Sea. Their patron deity is Wolven.


Imurtok. Imurtok is believed to be the oldest clan. All other clans, if they trace their lineage back far enough, came from Imurtok at some point. The Imurtok clan is also viewed as the leader of all Teivos clans, ruled by a High Queen that oversees the other clan leaders. Their patron deity is Vele.


Kanonar. Kanonar has a reputation of being the most aggressive clan. Kanonar’s young warriors often go out of their way to find and fight monsters, and Kanonar has been known to get into conflicts with other nations. Their patron deity is Sekh.


I traveled with the Kanonar for a time. We fought many beasts together and sang many revelries!

Nannuret. The largest of the major clans, the Nannuret are unique in that they only complete their pilgrimage once every two years. Seen as wise and deliberative, the Nannuret have the most complicated governmental system. Their patron deity is Woden.


Tipiara. Viewed by the other tribes as the most in touch with the spiritual world, Tipiara has many mystics and oracles among its ranks and often travel in patterns that make little sense to the other clans. Their patron deity is Stoll.

PILGRIMAGE

Each clan will make a pilgrimage to the holiest site of the Teivos at least once per year unless tremendous circumstances impede them. This holy site is an ancient ruin of a city completely filled with technology far beyond anything the Teivos have ever seen, in their own culture or anyone else’s. The city is nestled in the middle of concentric mountain peaks at the magnetic north pole of Ancerra and descends miles into the earth. The forgotten city - Vadreheim, they call it - somehow wards off the worst elements of winter and miraculously always has enough food and water for Teivos pilgrims.


The Teivos believe that this ancient technological marvel is all that physically remains of the Avadri on the whole planet, and so they treat the site with great reverence and guard its location with fierce secrecy. Often, Teivos pilgrims will take small pieces of technology with them from the great site, gradually learning how to use the technology before returning the next year. It is a sacred custom to return one’s souvenir before taking a new one, so any Teivos tribesperson is likely to only have one piece of Avadri technology on them at any given time.

  • The Teivos have found that a horrible monster has taken up residence in one of their favorite vales. Perhaps the heroes can defeat the monster in exchange for the honor of being allowed on a journey to Vadreheim.

THAAJ

A desert city-state in the eastern reaches of Roshallan’s power, Thaaj is powerful not for its agriculture, but for its proximity to the Smoldering Canyon. Thaajan miners labor tirelessly to gather valuable and magical metal from the canyon.


Thaaj is also hellbent on defeating Roshallan. Its current general, Prince Belan, is concocting an elaborate plan to march a great offensive of elephants through the volcanic mountain range. This should lend them the element of surprise and allow them to smash down from the foothills onto Roshallan’s capital.


They’re fighting for what they believe in. I wish them the wisdom of the Celestial Council and the protection of many divine emissaries.

Academic. Thaaj takes great pride in Smoldering Canyon Academy, an educational institution that attracts scholars and mages the world over. In addition to traditional courses, the school’s proximity to the Smoldering Canyon encourages scientists to pursue a vast number of experiments to learn more about the nature of magic.


Aristocratic and Political. Thaaj is a monarchy. The queen personally selects her advisors from successful merchants, scholars, and military commanders in her nation. The queen’s eldest daughter becomes the heir, and her eldest son grows up to become the commanding general of Thaaj’s army. Thaajan generals tend to be very loyal to their sisters, making them dedicated protectors of the nation.


Mercantile and Trade. Thaaj extensively mines the Smoldering Canyon, extracting tremendously valuable minerals. Precious metals and gems can be found in the Smoldering Canyon, along with rare magical metals such as avadrium, arcanium, primor, adamantine, and mithril. They also extract valuable inks that can be used in the creation of magical tattoos.


Militant. Thaaj has a small but mighty military. Their heavy cavalry, consisting of elephants, is especially renowned. UNJAARN

Once a nation of nearly twice its current size, Unjaarn has many layers of history in its large cities crumbling under their own weight. The young nation of Vadan used to be part of Unjaarn, and while Unjaarn maintains its traditional customs, Vadan is much more driven by change and new philosophies. This leads most Unjaarni to disregard the Vadanians as foolish.


Swampy and humid year-round, the people of Unjaarn are almost all at least competent in boating through bogs and marshes. The wilderness of Unjaarn is a tangle of mangroves, hemlock, and black spruce among other tropical and subtropical trees, all of which is overgrown with lichens, kudzu, and parasitic plants like strangler figs. All manner of dangerous animals and strange monsters lurk in the murky waters outside of civilization, but skilled rangers can navigate the swamps skillfully to avoid the worst of these creatures.


Academic. Unjaarn’s largest cities have well-equipped schools of philosophy, science, and fine arts. Attendance at such schools is reserved for the top three castes. Trades workers commonly apprentice under masters to learn their crafts.


Unjaarni Castes. Unjaarn maintains a rigid caste system. An Unjaarni will remain in the same caste that their parents were born into, as will their children, in perpetuity. Some Unjaarni believe that this system helps foster internal peace as nobody has to compete for higher status: their status is preordained. Other Unjaarni think that this limits the potential creativity and competence from many of the lower castes and the system should change. Needless to say, a majority of the Unjaarni that are happy with the system as it currently stands are those in the upper castes, although no group holds completely monolithic views. Many Unjaarni that are completely unsatisfied with the caste system will emigrate to Vadan, which has the same cultural history but has much more social mobility.


Brahma. The first and highest caste of Unjaarn are its religious leaders. While they do not hold any governmental authority, the other castes look to them for moral guidance and their counsel often sways the Nelida.


Nelida. The second caste are the elite among the Lectuadi. There are only a few dozen Nelida families in Unjaarn; they are regional and national legislators, judges, and generals.


Lectuadi. The third caste is the lowest caste allowed (and in fact required) to attend formal schooling. Local legislators and judges come from this caste. Lectuadi are also appointed to tactical roles overseeing the Duskiran.


Duskiran. The fourth caste is the armed forces. Both local town guards and the national infantry are considered Duskiran. Captains and generals are from the fourth caste, however.


While many Likishuan and Kirish attempt to emigrate to Vadan for upward social mobility, Unjaarn is also in a heated conflict with Vadan. The border between the two nations is a violent and contentious place.


Kirish. The trades workers and merchants share the fifth caste. Blacksmiths, tanners, weavers, and traders live slightly more comfortable lives than the Likishuan, but are still forbidden from pursuing formal schooling.


Likishuan. The sixth and lowest caste of Unjaarn is the manual laborers, who maintain the nation’s agriculture and infrastructure. Even the lowest caste is treated with basic humanoid dignity, as the higher castes recognize that it is the Likishuan who keep the nation functional.


The Likishuan and Kirish castes have a dialect of speaking that is almost incomprehensible to the upper castes. While the words are generally the same as the common language, the accent and speech patterns are so thick that the Likishuan and Kirish can easily communicate private messages to one another.


Religious. At religious ceremonies, the castes are made to sit in order of their caste, with the Likishuan furthest from the altar, the Nelida closest to it, and the Brahma performing rituals that involve a sacrificial offering of food and drink to the gods.


Unjaarni believe that every pantheon exists, and likely many more that nobody is aware of. Each town and household may have specific deities that they feel especially drawn to worship as their patrons. The Shibmar Conclave of the Archfae - specifically Lob, Tamat, and Khailain - are nationally venerated and believed to be some of the most powerful gods out of any pantheon. Most Unjaarni believe in reincarnation, though they do not scientifically understand Essence.


Their caste system seems strange to me, but it has worked for thousands of years. Perhaps there are lessons to learn from their culture.

Mercantile and Trade. Unjaarn exports many potent spices, artistic clay goods, and flavorful foods. It also exports colorful pigments that make bright dyes. Unjaarn is locked in economic conflict with Vadan, each trying to outmaneuver the other in international trade.


Underworld. There are individuals in every caste who are willing to break convention. A thinly spread but well organized network of smugglers help people cross the border to Vadan when desired.

NELIDI

The capital of Unjaarn is a massive city spread across several small islands in a shallow area of the swamplands. With a population of over three million, Nelidi is the largest city on the east of the Cloudpiercer Range.


The largest Brahma temple is in Nelidi, along with multiple elite schools and the palace from which the Nelida run their nation.

VADAN

A network of swampy bayous and steamy marshlands, Vadan recently seceded from its southern neighbor, Unjaarn. Although they share a culture and belief system, the Vadanians differ strongly from the Unjaarni in their approach to governance. While Unjaarn is a rigid caste system, Vadan runs on an aggressively capitalist government. Many folk from Vadan would prefer to live in Unjaarn, and vice versa, but the border between the two nations is a dangerous and often violent region.


Academic. Vadan’s capital has a large university, but very few scholars reside in it year-round, which makes it difficult for the university to grow.


Mercantile and Trade. Vadan exports food, spices of many varieties, and clay to neighboring nations. They also mine the Great Chasm to their north and export gems and rare metals. Vadan is locked in a trade war with Unjaarn, trying to lock down trade partnerships with other nations and block Unjaarn from doing so.


Militant. The Vadanian military is tremendously patriotic but poorly trained. Having recently secured independence from Unjaarn, the Vadanian military now struggles to bring discipline and structure to its ranks. In skirmishes with Unjaarni, the Vadanians usually win in battles that transpire in thick underbrush but lose if the battle takes place on an open lake or plain.


Political and Social. Vadan has a very free-market approach to governance. Those who seize on risky opportunities might flourish or might end up being left out to dry. There is no social safety net other than the generosity of neighbors, and many would rather ensure that they get ahead instead of spending time offering charity and risking getting dragged down themselves. Because of Vadan’s highly stressful and volatile system, some Vadanians emigrate to Unjaarn, where the rigid caste system provides stability.


Their economy has totally run amok, and there is poverty everywhere you look. I’m not sure that Unjaarn has the answers either, but in Vadan, thousands of people go to bed hungry every night. Something has to change.

-Chauncey


Religious. Vadanians largely share the same religious beliefs as the Unjaarni. There are many hundreds of gods, and individual communities may pay special homage to specific deities. As in Unjaarn, three specific gods from the Shibmar Conclave of the Archfae hold special prominence: Tamat, Khailain, and Immort.


Underworld. Many independent smugglers help transport people south into Unjaarn when they wish. Some of the most successful smugglers know which Unjaarni officials can be bribed to help secure a higher caste for the immigrant.

VADAN

The capital of Vadan, by the same name, was the third-largest city in Unjaarn before the secession. While most of the citizens of the capital strongly prefer Vadan’s system of governance to Unjaarn’s, there are clear signs of poverty in Vadan’s mucky alleyways, shantytowns, and glamorous fenced-off neighborhoods.


The capital is home of Vadan’s only university, which is also where the governmental buildings are located. Vadan is run by a parliament of the fifteen wealthiest citizens of the country. This is reassessed every half-year, which in theory would allow for a quickly rotating door of legislators. In practice, a couple dozen wealthy elites spend most of their time running their businesses or passing legislation that benefit their business or hurt their rivals, ensuring that they remain in the legislative shortlist.

VAENYTH

In a word, Vaenyth is cruel. Its jagged shale hills are full of dangerous monsters and deep chasms. Its sparse woodlands are equally perilous. Vaenyth’s mountain range plays havoc on weather patterns and the nation suffers from seasonal typhoons, hailstorms, and tornadoes.


Vaenyth’s population is a humanoid incarnation of this turbulent cruelty. With a savage governing system and near-constant civil wars, Vaenyth is a nation of anger and blood.


Academic. Vaenythan culture places little value in formal education. One’s capacity to perform their job well is usually all that matters.


Aristocratic and Political. With perhaps the most bizarre governing system in Gathandia, Vaenyth chooses their new monarch once every ten years. When they do so, each noble family who wishes to make a bid for the monarchy sends forth a champion to fight on their behalf. These champions each battle to the death in a massive arena riddled with traps and captured monsters. The sole surviving champion is ritually slaughtered, and the new monarch bathes in their blood before ascending to the throne for the next ten years.


So, I’ve been known to summon the occasional demon, but even I think that the Vaenythans have all lost their bloody minds.

-Dungrel


Mercantile and Trade. Vaenyth generally does not trade with other nations, with the exception of Pash. Vaenyth produces iron ore, stone from quarries, and animal and plant byproducts, which primarily circulate within Vaenyth’s own economy.


Militant. Vaenyth’s military is quite strong and well-equipped but lacks discipline and cohesion. More often than not, Vaenythan military contingents go to war with one another than with common enemies.


Religious. Vaenythans primarily worship the Exiles and the Archfiends of Inferno. They believe that the most powerful deities are those who can survive in the least comfortable circumstances. This power then translates into understanding and knowledge. Vaenythan priestly rituals often involve animal slaughter and occasionally include humanoid sacrifice.


Social and Underworld. Criminal activity is standard in any Vaenythan settlement. Cheating on taxes, mugging a neighbor in a back alley, petty burglary, and vengeance-driven murder are all commonplace. The only law of the land is the proclamations of the monarch, and even then, these are more suggestions than hard and fast decrees.

BLOODSTONE

The capital citadel of Vaenyth and the death-soaked throne of the monarchy is Bloodstone. This is a massive fortress carved from stone and reinforced with an iron-Arcanium alloy developed by ancient mystics long ago and since forgotten to history. A hundred thousand soldiers have been turned away by a mere eighty that held Bloodstone, the legends say.

POINTS OF INTEREST

Floating Islands. The floating islands that stretch as far south as Pash’s Great Bay soar high above Vaenyth. Given their disinterest in scholarly pursuits, Vaenythans have no idea that Arcanium and Primor could be mined from these islands in abundance.

  • Vaenyth’s rulers ask the heroes to infiltrate Pash and siphon large sums of money back into Vaenyth’s own pockets.
  • The monarch of Vaenyth asks the heroes to find a way to reach the floating islands and crash them down to the ground so that they can be studied.
  • Vaenyth has decided to hold a special electoral battle with teams of champions. The heroes have been chosen as one such team.

VALSTROM

In the shadowy Thunderhead Vale to the east of the Cloudpiercer Range, the kingdom of Valstrom sprawls along the Thunderhead River. With the Cloudpiercers to Valstrom’s west and north, and the Hadla mountains to its east, Valstrom catches a tremendous amount of rain and humidity from the Kozia Rainforest. Valstrom’s summers are hot and humid, its winters chilly and often snowy. Its falls are vibrant with bright leaves, its springs with bright flowers.


Valstrom’s charming seasons have drawn many peoples over the generations, and in the clearings of the subtropical forests its ancient towns are steeped in layers of history. Most towns and all of Valstrom’s cities are heavily timber-based, with log buildings and wooden tiles or thatched roofs. A few fortified buildings, such as the town watch and the lord or lady’s castle, are predominantly classical stone castles.


Generations ago, Shadradoth split away from Valstrom as an independent nation. When the king passed away, his chief advisor became king regent, but refused to abdicate when the late king’s daughter came of age. The resulting civil war tore the kingdom in half, and Princess Shadra, unable to claim the throne from Rehan Jehoram, had to make do with her half of the kingdom. The Jehorams continue their rule to this day.


A pleasant natural environment mixed with dozens of tragic stories. I may be here a while.

-Flanagan


Academic. Valstrom’s larger towns usually have some kind of academy or university. Some of these are in a state of disuse, but many still have thriving libraries, research facilities, and classes.


Aristocratic and Political. A classical feudal society has been implemented in Valstrom for all of its recorded history. Mayors report to counts and countesses, who report to dukes and duchesses, who report to the monarch. The monarch’s eldest child assumes the monarchy after their parent passes away or abdicates the throne.


Mercantile and Trade. Valstrom has had its share of unrest, but over many generations it has proven to be a reliable trade partner. Its neighboring land nations include Sozein and Tafunga to the south, and Falth, Nalathesh, and Husk to the north. Valstrom maintains a robust trade relationship with Shadradoth despite their historical tensions. Merchants competent in mountain passes will also trade with Hadla to the east.


Militant. Valstrom maintains its classical feudal system in how its military functions. Each mayor has a small local army that keeps the peace in and around their settlement. Counts and countesses also have small armies, and have the political capital to levy the armies of the mayors that live within their territories. Dukes and duchesses can likewise levy any army under the control of any count in their province, and the monarch can of course levy any duke’s or duchess’s forces when the need arises.


Religious. Valstromians traditionally worship the Celestial Council, seeing its leaders as the gods that bless or curse them in accordance with their piety, or lack thereof. Any settlement will have at least a small temple with an altar dedicated to these gods, and the larger cities compete with one another to have the grandest, most beautiful temple out of any Valstromian society.


Social. Most Valstromians are content with their feudal hierarchy, but some are discontent with it. Many of the peasantry have their basic needs met: food, drink, comfortable shelter, and basic leisure activities. These peasants often don’t care whether they get ahead in life or not, so long as their needs remain fulfilled. Some of the peasantry, by contrast, think that the feudal system is out of date and should have been abolished long ago.


Many counts and dukes also feel tremendous discontent. Having achieved success, but knowing that the dukes will always have more power and influence, counts and countesses often lose sleep scheming about how they might get ahead in the political system. Dukes and duchesses, in turn, often fret about maintaining the power they have.


Underworld. Valstrom’s traditional feudal structure leaves ample room for crime syndicates to operate in the shadows, as details often get lost in the chain of command from mayor to monarch and back. Notable syndicates include the Twice Strikes, the Hoods, and the Galepiercers (see Shadradoth for information on each syndicate).

STORMVALE

The capital and oldest city in Valstrom is Stormvale. Stormvale is an expansive city that extends from the banks of the Thundervale River up into the foothills of the Hadla Mountains. Parts of Stormvale have fallen into utter disrepair and are almost more forest than city at this point, but much of the city - especially that closest to the river - thrives as a bustling metropolis.


The royal castle overlooks the river and is surrounded by an artificial moat connected to the river. On the mainland, the Stormvale University holds classrooms and dormitories full of intellectuals.


The Stormvale Cathedral is also a marvel of architectural and artistic achievement, and the religious among the nation often travel to behold the wondrous stained glass depictions of the various members of the Celestial Council.

POINTS OF INTEREST

Cloudpiercers. See Shadradoth for information on the Cloudpiercer Range and the tallest mountain in the known world.


Thundervale River. The Valstromians and Shadradothans have built a shared city at the base of a magical waterfall in the Thundervale River. See Shadradoth for more information on this remarkable place.

QUEST IDEAS

  • Valstrom faces another heirship crisis, and various nobles across the kingdom make their power plays. The heroes must interact with these nobles and determine who they wish to support.
  • The entire Valstromian/Shadradothan town at the base of the magic waterfall has vanished. Perhaps it’s in the Everwilds.
  • Monsters have been reported in the overgrown neighborhoods of Stormvale. Will the heroes free the city from these menaces?
  • Adventurers and a famous noble have gone missing after boasting that they could climb the Great Cloudpierce, the tallest mountain in the world. Another noble calls upon the heroes to mount a rescue mission.

VEL

A tiny but powerful city-state clinging to the craggy beaches of Gathandia’s southeast peninsula, Vel is ruled by a kleptocratic oligarchy known as the Mages of the Beach. The Mages control every aspect of life in Vel and their powerful diviners keep a watchful eye on the city-state, ready to quash dissent at a moment’s notice. Common laborers and merchants live in crowded, narrow, and steep switchback streets built precariously along the beach’s steep cliffs, and the Mages’ enormous towers soar high above the cliffs like jagged teeth.


On the beach itself, trained cannon crabs ward off any plunderers and a single fortified pier allows merchant vessels to trade with Vel.


Vel’s influence is felt far and wide internationally, and the Mages of the Beach have a thinly concealed desire to dominate the world, first through the soft power of influence and then through the hard power of conquest.


Academic. The Mages of the Beach have a large university in one of their central towers. They commonly invite well-known scholars to spend time teaching in their university’s halls, and while scholars visit Vel, the Mages aggressively try to brainwash these scholars to mirror Vel’s philosophical talking points when the scholars set forth for other universities.


Aristocratic and Political. The Mages of the Beach hold unquestionable control over every aspect of life in Vel. Any administrative position or legal office is held by a Mage of the Beach.


Mercantile and Trade. Vel is currently pursuing an international strategy of soft influence. They export entertainment goods, games, novels, and leisure activities, trying to inculcate Velan culture into every other nation on Gathandia.


Highly interested in the city-state of Husk for its location on a point of power, Velan scholars are working on developing a way to open a permanent teleportation portal between two areas so that they can trade with Husk much more readily.


Militant. Under the command of General Vilhelm Cynthar, a skilled combat mage, Vel’s military is highly trained and effective. Every Velan soldier is capable of basic magic casting of some kind and is trained in fighting with unique magical weapons.


Religious. The Mages of the Beach have introduced a unique religion to Vel. Many gods are inscribed on illuminated cards, which citizens are encouraged to buy and trade. The Mages of the Beach started with pantheons that are commonly accepted throughout Gathandia, and released fewer copies of certain gods to increase their scarcity. The more god-points you have, the more religious you are and the more blessed you will be, the Mages declare.


They have released several “seasons” of cards and have made up a vast number of fictitious pantheons so that they can keep printing more and more cards. The more cards that they print, the higher number of god-points a commoner will need in order to maintain their good fortune.


Their bastardization of religion for personal profit is utterly disgraceful. Tovare forgive me, but I hope the whole lot of them rot in the dungeons of Melaneth.

-Marcus


Social. It is governmentally required that every merchant and every commoner is happy and merry at least 80% of any given week. In order to promote this idea, mandatory festivals and gaming tournaments are commonplace.


Underworld. The vigilant diviners among the Mages of the Beach are constantly on the lookout for signs of criminal conspiracies and are quick to make examples of anyone who gets conspiratorial inclinations. They do not tolerate black markets in Vel, but encourage Velan merchants to engage in black market negotiations in other nations.

QUEST IDEAS

  • The heroes might have a run-in with Velan merchants or mages anywhere in Gathandia. This may lead the heroes to look for a way to reduce Vel’s influence or even overthrow the malignant city-state entirely.

YODVAN

Long ago, the bravest sailors from Fjordenhaven braved the Ice Seas of the north and made landfall on a strange and wondrous island of snow. They explored a forest whose plants seemed to be made of a vegetable-metallic hybrid and ventured into the fog-choked mountains beyond. There, they found Yodvan’s true treasure: a mythically hard and strong material that came to be known as adamantine.


Several expeditions later, the Fjordenhavians had sent enough people and supplies to this island that they were able to establish a permanent outpost. It grew into a city, and from there several more towns were established as eager miners and explorers ventured into the Glowing Forest and the Mountains of Adamant. Eventually, facing political pressure from his youngest sister and struggling to manage Fjordenhaven proper and the distant island of Yodvan, the king of Fjordenhaven gave the island of Yodvan to his sister as an independent nation.


The snowblown island of Yodvan experiences days where the sun does not set during the summer, and experiences long nights during winter. In the darkest days of winter, the sun does not rise at all.


Academic. Aurora’s Hearth, the capital of Yodvan, has a large scientific academy that conducts endless experimentation with adamantine and the various biotechnical discoveries that explorers find in the Glowing Forest.


Aristocratic and Political. Like Fjordenhaven, Yodvan practices a monarchy of pure primogeniture: the eldest child of each monarch becomes the next monarch. Subsequent children (or other family members) are assigned to be counts and countesses over Yodvan’s towns.


Mercantile and Trade. To this day, Yodvan remains independent but is closely allied with its parent nation: Fjordenhven sends foodstuffs to Yodvan in exchange for an influx of glass lights and adamantine. Yodvan can sell its commodity goods for enormous returns in virtually any nation that it can reach.


A bulb of pear-shaped glass that emanates light… What other wonders might this strange island hold?

-Belozog


Militant. Yodvan does not have a standing infantry, but has highly trained and well equipped town guards. Most town guards have “electric torches” fashioned from the glowing glass lights that people harvest from the forest. They are also equipped with adamantine shields and spears, making them quite tough to beat. Yodvan does maintain a reasonably powerful navy with vessels and sailors that are well-equipped for dealing with icy waters.


Religious. Yodvan observes many of the same religious practices that are held by their southern neighboring nation, Noord. Most folk worship both winter as a harsh but strengthening goddess, and Aurora - the northern lights - as a goddess of bounty, beauty, and good fortune. They also pay homage to the Fjordenhavian patron god of fire, Aeshir, but he is not as prominent in Yodvanian religion as Aurora or Strack, the winter.


Social. Yodvan’s culture is a blend of Fjordenhaven’s and Noord’s. Having generally adopted the religious beliefs of Noord, Yodvan has the less militant attitude shared by the Fjordenhavians. The folk of Yodvan are generally warm and hospitable to strangers, but punish crime very severely.


Underworld. A thriving crime syndicate constantly smuggles new inventions from Yodvan out to the rest of the criminal world throughout Gathandia. This is dangerous work: if caught, thieves are dumped in frigid water and left to the fish. Even so, the lucrative profit is enough to lure many into this line of work.

AURORA'S HEARTH

Aurora’s Hearth is the first outpost founded by Fjordenhaven hundreds of years ago. It has since developed into a sprawling city that hugs the coastline for dozens of miles. The old town, where Fjordenhavian sailors first made landfall, is where the monarch’s palace stands tall and proud against the cold. The Yodvanian Academy of Sciences is a few miles south of the old town.


Aurora’s Hearth is clean, well-maintained, and well-lit at night, thanks to the glowing glass lights harvested from the forest. The city watchguards do an excellent job of mitigating violent crime and home burglary, but struggle to tamp down on controlled goods being smuggled out of the city’s numerous ports.

POINTS OF INTEREST

The Glowing Forest. The glowing forest was created from ancient Avadri technology, though this has not yet been discovered by Yodvanian researchers. Its vegetation grows in strangely intentional patterns: trees might have limbs at precise angles, and a bush’s leaves might all be exactly the same size. The vegetation seems to be made of an alloy of both plant and metal materials, and scientists are still experimenting with the best uses for this material. Glass bulbs grow from many trees and bushes in a variety of shapes, and these bulbs will often glow with internal light. Scientists have discovered how to control when these lights turn on and off, and the bulb’s light will last for several years after being plucked.


Other discoveries no doubt lie deep in the Glowing Forest, but strange monsters of untold power lurk in the forest’s depths as well. Determined but cautious, the Yodvanians have made slow progress in uncovering the secrets within the forest’s depths.


The Mountains of Adamant. Shrouded in thick fog and palpable mystery, the Mountains of Adamant loom cold and foreboding along the center of the large island. The promise of great wealth in the form of adamantine lures many miners and adventurers, but both the terrain and the fauna are vicious in these mountains, not to mention the winds that could rip flesh from bone. Jagged cliffs, treacherous glaciers, and narrow passes each pose threats to explorers. Terrifying creatures that lurk deep in the mountain’s caves often leave even stoic heroes babbling with inarticulate fright.

QUEST IDEAS

  • Yodvan holds many valuable materials, notably adamantine and glowing glass. The heroes might be recruited by Yodvanian scientists or by patrons from another nation to travel deeper into the Glowing Forest or the Mountains of Adamant to find more of these materials or to make new discoveries.
  • An eldritch being has awakened in a mineshaft in the Mountains of Adamant, and so officials call on the heroes to stop the monster before it can cause lasting damage.
  • An incredibly brutal winter has completely buried a town in snow. The heroes must oversee efforts to rescue the town, but starving polar bears and hungry abominations from the Glowing Forest have other ideas for the town’s trapped citizens.
  • The heroes are recruited by a network of magic item smugglers working for Husk or Vel. They want the heroes’ help in procuring a new item that scientists have created with the plant-metal of the Glowing Forest.
