FEATURES


Dark Flight. Uvior gains a fly speed equal to his walking speed while in dim light or darkness. If he is exposed to bright light while flying, he falls.


Growing Horde. When Uvior Takes a Rest, at the end of that time he automatically summons 1d4 Skeletons.


ACTIONS


On Uvior’s turn, he can MOVE and take TWO of the following actions.


Move. Uvior moves up to his speed.


Assailing Shadow. Ranged 60 ft Arcane Spell. +3 vs WILL of a creature in dim light or darkness. Hit: 6 (1d12) necrotic and target is “Tethered.”


Draining Touch. Reach 5 ft Natural Weapon. +3 vs AR. Hit: 8 (1d10+3) necrotic and target is “Tethered.”


Snuff. One source of nonmagical light or light from a divine prayer or primal chant within 60 ft of Uvior is extinguished.


OR Uvior can MOVE and take the following action.


Undead Horde. Uvior summons 1d4-1 Skeletons, which appear in random unoccupied spaces within 30 ft of him.


REACTIONS


Tether (1/Round). When Uvior takes damage, he may use his reaction to choose a creature that he can see within 60 ft of him. If that creature has been Tethered since the start of Uvior’s last turn, then that creature takes ½ of the damage that Uvior would have taken.


FIGHTING MOGG


Mogg fights cautiously, hiding as much as able and using his magic to animate furniture to fight for him. Other gretchlings that participate in this battle gain access to the Disarm maneuver and will attempt to run off with the heroes’ weapons and gear.

Arcane Cantrips. Using INT, Mogg can cast the following cantrips.

Arcane Spellcaster. Using INT, Mogg can cast the following spells. He has 20 SP.


(Spells) Circle of Warding, Fireball, Mage Armor, Minor Illusion, Swift Teleport


Spellbook. Mogg can spend 1 week transcribing a spell that he has found on a scroll, rune, or the like into his spellbook and practicing it. He then learns that spell if the spell costs 20 or fewer SP.


Poisonous Skin. Mogg’s skin secretes a paralytic poison. When a creature makes an unarmed strike or attempts to Grapple Mogg, Mogg makes an END attack against their FORT. Hit: Creature becomes Slowed for 10 seconds.


Unstable Magics. When Mogg reaches 0 HP, he explodes. He makes an END attack against the REF of all creatures within a 15 ft radius. On a hit, deal 4 (1d8) fire damage.


Magic Consumption. If in the presence of a magic item for 11 (2d8+2) days, Mogg can consume enough magic to cause Uncommon and Rare items to become mundane.


ACTIONs


Mogg can MOVE and take ONE of the following actions.


Move. Mogg can move up to his speed.


Knife. Thrown 20 ft One-Handed Weapon. +4 vs AR. Hit: 6 (1d4+4) sharp.


Arcane Cantrips. Mogg can use one of his cantrips.


OR Mogg can take ONE of the following actions.


Arcane Spellcaster. Mogg can use one of his spells.


Awaken Furniture (1/Rest). Mogg targets enough furniture within 60 ft of him to fuse together into a Large creature, a Furniture Golem.

RESOLUTION

The adventure ends when the animosity between Uvior and Mogg is resolved. This will happen in one of three ways:


  • The party confronts and defeats Uvior
  • The party confronts and defeats Mogg
  • The party negotiates a peace between them

The best possible outcome for the heroes is negotiating a peace between Uvior and Mogg, and using Greater Restoration on Humphrey the Cleric. Doing so will result in the heroes getting the maximum amount of treasure from both Uvior and Mogg (see their respective random encounter entries for this treasure), and earns them +4 Goodwill whenever they enter a settlement that is home to an adventurer who was turned to stone at Uvior’s tragic banquet.
